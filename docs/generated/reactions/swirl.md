# Swirl and Viridescent Venerer

## Swirl Reaction Mechanics

Swirl is a transformative Elemental Reaction in Genshin Impact triggered when Anemo comes into contact with Hydro, Pyro, Electro, or Cryo. Upon triggering, Swirl deals Anemo damage and spreads the absorbed element to nearby enemies. The damage dealt by the Swirl reaction itself scales exclusively with the Elemental Mastery (EM) of the character triggering the Swirl, and is unaffected by ATK, CRIT Rate, or CRIT DMG. The base damage of Swirl is determined by the character's level and their EM.

The formula for Swirl damage is:
`Swirl DMG = Base Swirl DMG * (1 + EM Bonus + Reaction DMG Bonus)`

Where:
*   **Base Swirl DMG** is a fixed value determined by the character's level.
*   **EM Bonus** is derived from the character's Elemental Mastery. Each point of EM provides a small percentage increase to Swirl damage. At level 90, 1000 EM provides approximately a 400% increase to Swirl damage.
*   **Reaction DMG Bonus** can come from various sources, such as the Viridescent Venerer artifact set, certain character passives, or specific weapon effects.

Crucially, Swirl has a unique property: it can absorb and spread one of the four elemental types (Hydro, Pyro, Electro, Cryo) upon contact. The element absorbed is determined by the last element applied to an enemy before the Anemo ability hits. If multiple elements are present, the Anemo ability will absorb the element that was applied most recently. This absorbed element is then applied to enemies hit by the Swirl, potentially triggering further reactions. For example, if an enemy is affected by Hydro and then hit by an Anemo ability that Swirls Hydro, the Swirl damage will occur, and nearby enemies will also be afflicted with Hydro.

The damage from Swirl itself is considered Anemo damage. However, the elemental application from the spread component is of the absorbed element. This dual nature makes Swirl incredibly versatile for both dealing damage and enabling other elemental reactions.

An important limitation of Swirl is its inability to react with Geo or Dendro. Anemo abilities will not trigger a Swirl reaction when in contact with enemies affected by Geo or Dendro. This means Swirl cannot spread Geo or Dendro, nor can it deal damage based on these elements.

The internal cooldown (ICD) for elemental application via Swirl is generally 2.5 seconds or 3 hits. This means that an Anemo character can only apply the absorbed element through Swirl once every 2.5 seconds or every three instances of triggering Swirl, whichever comes first. This ICD applies to the elemental application, not the Swirl damage itself, which can occur more frequently if multiple enemies are hit.

## Viridescent Venerer Artifact Set

The Viridescent Venerer (VV) artifact set is a cornerstone of Anemo support characters in Genshin Impact, providing an unparalleled utility through its 4-piece bonus. This set is almost universally considered mandatory for any Anemo character focused on supporting a team, especially those who can reliably trigger Swirl reactions.

The set bonuses are as follows:

*   **2-Piece Bonus:** Anemo DMG Bonus +15%
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases Swirl DMG by 60%. Decreases the Elemental RES of enemies hit by Swirl by 40% for 10 seconds. This effect can only be triggered once every 10 seconds, even if the character is not on the field.

The 4-piece bonus is what makes Viridescent Venerer indispensable. The 40% Elemental RES shred is a massive damage amplification for the team, affecting all elemental damage types that can be Swirled. This RES reduction applies to Hydro, Pyro, Electro, and Cryo elements. It is crucial to understand that this RES shred is applied *after* any other RES reductions or buffs, making it one of the most potent damage multipliers in the game.

The mechanic of the 4-piece bonus requires the Anemo character to trigger a Swirl reaction. Once a Swirl occurs, the enemy's corresponding Elemental RES (Hydro, Pyro, Electro, or Cryo) is reduced by 40% for 10 seconds. This debuff has a 10-second internal cooldown, meaning it can only be refreshed or reapplied every 10 seconds. The character triggering the Swirl does not need to be on the field for the RES shred to take effect, as long as they have the 4-piece VV set equipped. This allows for flexible team rotations where the Anemo support can switch out after applying the debuff.

The Viridescent Venerer set is so powerful that it dictates the role of most Anemo characters. Even Anemo characters who have strong personal damage or utility often find their best use as VV holders, prioritizing the RES shred over their own damage output. This is because the team-wide damage increase from the 40% RES reduction far outweighs the personal damage gain from other artifact sets for most Anemo characters.

When building a character with Viridescent Venerer, the main stats on the Flower and Feather are fixed (HP and ATK, respectively). The Sands, Goblet, and Circlet should ideally be chosen to maximize the character's Swirl damage and utility. For the Sands, Elemental Mastery is often preferred to boost Swirl damage and the RES shred's effectiveness. For the Goblet, Anemo DMG Bonus is standard, but if the character's primary role is purely to apply VV and their EM is already high, an EM Goblet can sometimes be considered for maximizing Swirl damage. For the Circlet, CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG can be used if the Anemo character has significant personal damage scaling, but Elemental Mastery is often prioritized for maximizing Swirl damage and the RES shred's impact.

The 2-piece bonus provides a 15% Anemo DMG Bonus, which is beneficial for the Anemo character's own damage, but it is secondary to the impact of the 4-piece set.

## Key Anemo Characters and Swirl Synergies

Several Anemo characters excel at utilizing Swirl and Viridescent Venerer, each with unique mechanics that enhance their support capabilities.

### Sucrose

Sucrose, a 4-star Catalyst user, is a premier Anemo support renowned for her ability to gather enemies and share Elemental Mastery. Her Elemental Skill, Astable Anemohypostasis, pulls enemies towards her and launches them, dealing Anemo DMG. Her Elemental Burst, Forbidden Creation - Isomer 75 / Type II, creates a large Wind Spirit that continuously pulls enemies and deals Anemo DMG.

Sucrose's true strength lies in her passive talents. Her A1 passive, "Catalyst Conversion," grants her a 20% chance to refund 100% of the Elemental Energy used to cast her Elemental Skill. This significantly improves her energy recharge and allows for more frequent use of her abilities. Her A4 passive, "Nourishing Increments," is arguably her most impactful. When Sucrose triggers a Swirl reaction, all party members (excluding herself) gain Elemental Mastery based on her own EM. The amount shared is 20% of her EM, with a bonus 4% for each point of EM above 240. This EM share directly buffs the damage of Swirl reactions triggered by other characters, as well as the damage of other elemental reactions like Vaporize, Melt, Electro-Charged, and Overload. This makes Sucrose an exceptional enabler for teams that rely on high EM for their reaction damage. Building Sucrose with high Elemental Mastery (aiming for 700+ EM) is crucial to maximize both her Swirl damage and the EM buff she provides to her team.

### Kazuha

Kaedehara Kazuha, a 5-star Sword user, is another top-tier Anemo support celebrated for his crowd control, damage buffs, and significant EM scaling. His Elemental Skill, Chihayaburu, pulls enemies towards him, dealing Anemo DMG and lifting him into the air. He can then perform a Plunging Attack, Midare Ranzan, which deals Anemo DMG and creates a small wind tunnel that pulls enemies. His Elemental Burst, Kazuha Slash, creates a large field that deals continuous Anemo DMG and pulls enemies.

Kazuha's unique strength comes from his A4 passive talent, "Poetics of Fuubutsu." When Kazuha triggers a Swirl reaction, he grants his party members an Elemental DMG Bonus for the element that was Swirled. The amount of this buff is based on his own Elemental Mastery. Specifically, for every 1 point of Elemental Mastery Kazuha possesses, he grants 0.04% Elemental DMG Bonus to the corresponding element. This buff applies to Hydro, Pyro, Electro, and Cryo. This makes Kazuha an incredibly potent damage buffer, as the RES shred from Viridescent Venerer combined with his Elemental DMG Bonus can lead to astronomical damage numbers for the main DPS. Building Kazuha with high Elemental Mastery (often aiming for 800-1000+ EM) is paramount to maximizing his buffing capabilities, while still retaining enough CRIT stats and ER for personal damage and consistent Burst uptime.

### Venti

Venti, a 5-star Bow user, is the king of crowd control, capable of gathering most smaller enemies into a single, easily manageable vortex. His Elemental Skill, Skyward Sonnet, deals Anemo DMG and launches enemies into the air, creating a wind current. His Elemental Burst, Wind's Grand Ode, fires an arrow that creates a massive Stormeye, which sucks in enemies and deals continuous Anemo DMG.

Venti's primary utility comes from his unparalleled crowd control and his energy refund mechanic. While his personal Swirl damage is decent, and he benefits from Viridescent Venerer, his true value lies in his ability to group enemies so effectively that other characters can easily hit multiple targets. Furthermore, his A1 passive talent, "Energy Symphony," grants him an energy refund when his Elemental Burst hits enemies. If the Burst triggers a Swirl reaction, it refunds 15 Elemental Energy for each enemy affected by the Swirl, up to a maximum of 100 Elemental Energy. This can effectively make his Elemental Burst cost 0 Energy in many scenarios, allowing for near-constant uptime and enabling him to continuously group enemies and trigger Swirls. This energy refund is particularly potent in teams where enemies are susceptible to being gathered and Swirled, allowing Venti to maintain his Burst uptime without requiring excessive Energy Recharge on his artifacts.

### Other Anemo Characters

Other Anemo characters like Jean, Sayu, and Xiao can also utilize Viridescent Venerer. Jean, a 5-star Sword user, provides healing and decent Anemo damage with her Skill and Burst, and can hold VV to shred enemy RES. Sayu, a 4-star Claymore user, offers healing and mobility with her unique rolling mechanic, and can also act as a VV holder. Xiao, a 5-star Polearm user, is primarily a main DPS who deals significant Anemo damage during his Burst. While he can technically hold VV, it is generally not recommended as his damage scales heavily with ATK and CRIT, and his personal damage output is far greater than the team-wide benefit of him holding VV. Instead, he typically uses 2-piece Gladiator's Finale/Desert Pavilion Chronicle and 2-piece Viridescent Venerer, or a full 4-piece Desert Pavilion Chronicle set. However, if he is the only Anemo character available and the team heavily relies on RES shred, a 4-piece VV build can be considered for him, albeit at a significant personal damage cost.
