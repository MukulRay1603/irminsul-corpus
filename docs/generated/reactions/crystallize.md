# Crystallize and Geo Reactions

Crystallize is a Geo-based elemental reaction that occurs when Geo attacks interact with Hydro, Pyro, Electro, or Cryo. This reaction does not deal damage itself but instead generates an Elemental Shard corresponding to the element that reacted with Geo. These shards are then automatically picked up by the active character, providing them with a temporary elemental shield. The strength and duration of these shields are determined by several factors, primarily the level and Elemental Mastery (EM) of the character who triggered the Crystallize reaction.

## Crystallize Mechanics

When a Geo attack hits an enemy affected by Hydro, Pyro, Electro, or Cryo, a Crystallize reaction is triggered. The specific element of the resulting shard is determined by the element that was applied to the enemy. For example, a Geo attack hitting a Hydro-infused enemy will produce a Hydro shard. The character currently on the field will automatically absorb the shard if they are within its pickup radius, which is approximately 3 meters. Upon absorption, the character gains an elemental shield of the shard's element.

The base shield strength of a Crystallize shard is calculated using the following formula:
`Shield Strength = (Base Shield Strength Value + EM Bonus + Level Bonus) * (1 + Shield Strength Bonus)`

The Base Shield Strength Value is a fixed amount determined by the character's level. The Elemental Mastery (EM) of the triggering character provides a significant boost to the shield strength. Specifically, each point of EM increases the shield strength by 0.12% of the base shield value. The Level Bonus further contributes to the shield strength, with higher-level characters generating stronger shields. Finally, any general Shield Strength Bonus buffs, such as those from artifacts like the Tenacity of the Millelith set or from certain character abilities, will multiply the total shield strength.

The duration of a Crystallize shield is 15 seconds. However, if a character is hit by an attack that matches the element of their current Crystallize shield, the shield will absorb damage and then shatter, disappearing prematurely. This interaction means that Crystallize shields are most effective against elemental damage types that do not match the shard's element. For instance, a Hydro shard shield will effectively block Pyro, Electro, and Cryo damage but will shatter instantly upon taking any Hydro damage.

## Geo Resonance: Solidify and Amplify

Geo Resonance, achieved by having at least two Geo characters in the party, provides significant buffs that enhance the effectiveness of Geo-focused teams. The primary benefits are:

1.  **Fortress of the People:** Increases damage dealt by all party members by 15% while they are protected by a shield. This bonus applies to all damage types, including Geo, elemental, and physical damage. It is a multiplicative bonus that significantly boosts the overall damage output of the team.
2.  **Crystalize Resonance:** Increases shield strength by 20%. This bonus stacks multiplicatively with other shield strength bonuses, making it easier to maintain strong, long-lasting shields.

These two bonuses synergize exceptionally well with Crystallize, as the shields generated by Crystallize reactions benefit directly from the increased shield strength, and the damage buff applies to all characters while they are shielded. This makes Geo Resonance a cornerstone for any team aiming to leverage Geo's defensive and supportive capabilities.

## Geo's Unique Role: No Elemental Amplification

Unlike Hydro, Pyro, Electro, and Cryo, Geo does not participate in elemental reaction amplification mechanics such as Vaporize, Melt, Overload, Superconduct, or Electro-Charged. Geo attacks, when they trigger Crystallize, do not consume the elemental aura on the enemy, nor do they deal amplified damage based on EM or CRIT stats. This fundamental difference means that Geo characters are not typically built for raw damage amplification through these reactions. Instead, their strength lies in consistent damage, utility, and defensive support.

This lack of amplification means that Geo teams generally do not achieve the same explosive burst damage potential as teams focused on Vaporize or Melt. However, they compensate with unparalleled defensive stability and consistent, reliable damage output.

## Zhongli's Universal RES Shred

Zhongli, the Geo Archon, possesses a unique and powerful mechanic that significantly benefits Geo teams and even non-Geo teams: his Pillar of Geo and his Elemental Burst, Planet Befall, both apply a Universal RES Shred. Specifically, any character within the radius of Zhongli's resonating Pillar of Geo or affected by Planet Befall has their Physical and Elemental RES reduced by 20%. This shred applies to all enemies, regardless of their elemental aura, and is a multiplicative reduction. This means that enemies take 20% more damage from all sources.

This Universal RES Shred is incredibly potent because it affects all damage types and bypasses the need for specific elemental auras or reactions. It is one of the primary reasons Zhongli is considered one of the best support characters in the game, as he dramatically increases the damage output of any team he is in, especially Geo teams that already benefit from Geo Resonance.

## When Geo Teams Shine

Geo teams, particularly those built around Crystallize and Geo Resonance, excel in several scenarios:

*   **Sustained Damage and Survivability:** Geo teams are ideal for content that requires prolonged engagements or where enemy damage output is very high. The combination of strong shields from Crystallize and Geo Resonance, coupled with consistent damage from Geo characters like Arataki Itto, Gorou, and Albedo, allows for a highly stable and survivable playstyle.
*   **Against Enemies with Elemental Auras:** Crystallize is effective at generating shields against enemies that consistently apply elemental auras, such as Abyss Mages or certain bosses. The ability to generate shields of various elements can provide protection against a wide range of incoming elemental damage.
*   **When Universal RES Shred is Key:** Teams featuring Zhongli benefit immensely from his RES shred, making Geo a viable option even when not the primary damage dealer. This shred amplifies the damage of all party members, making it a powerful generalist buff.
*   **Specific Character Synergies:** Characters like Gorou are specifically designed to buff Geo teams, providing significant DEF and Geo DMG bonuses to Geo characters. This makes dedicated Geo teams with Gorou incredibly potent.

## When Geo Teams Are Suboptimal

Despite their strengths, Geo teams have certain limitations and can be suboptimal in specific situations:

*   **Low Burst Damage Potential:** Compared to reaction-heavy teams that can achieve massive Vaporize or Melt multipliers, Geo teams generally lack the same explosive burst damage. This can make them slower for clearing content with very tight time limits or against enemies with very high HP pools that require rapid damage spikes.
*   **Lack of Elemental Application for Reactions:** Geo characters, with the exception of some niche interactions or specific character kits, do not reliably apply elemental auras needed for other characters to trigger powerful reactions. This limits their synergy with characters who rely on triggering Vaporize, Melt, or Electro-Charged.
*   **Enemies Immune to Geo or Shields:** Certain enemies, such as the Geo Hypostasis during its shield phase or some shielded enemies in the Spiral Abyss, can resist Geo damage or negate the benefits of shields. In these cases, Geo teams may struggle to deal damage effectively.
*   **Against Enemies with High Geo RES:** While Zhongli's RES shred is universal, enemies with inherently high Geo RES can still pose a challenge for pure Geo damage dealers.

In summary, Crystallize and Geo Resonance provide a robust defensive foundation and consistent damage amplification, making Geo teams excellent for survivability and sustained combat. Zhongli's Universal RES Shred further enhances their damage potential. However, their lack of participation in elemental amplification mechanics means they typically fall short in raw burst damage compared to reaction-focused teams, and they can be less effective against specific enemy types or in time-sensitive challenges.
