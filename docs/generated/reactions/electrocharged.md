# Electrocharged Reaction

The Electrocharged reaction is a unique Elemental Reaction in Genshin Impact that occurs when Electro is applied to an enemy affected by Hydro, or vice-versa. Unlike most other elemental reactions, Electrocharged does not consume either of the triggering elemental auras. This means that an enemy can remain affected by both Hydro and Electro simultaneously, allowing for sustained damage and enabling other reactions. The reaction itself deals Electro DMG based on the triggering character's Elemental Mastery and Level, with a base multiplier of 1.2x for Level 90 characters. This damage is applied periodically, with a tick rate of approximately 1 second.

## Mechanics of Electrocharged

The core mechanic that distinguishes Electrocharged is its non-consuming nature. When Electro hits a Hydro-affected enemy, Electrocharged triggers, dealing damage. Crucially, the Hydro aura on the enemy is *not* consumed by this reaction. Similarly, if Hydro hits an Electro-affected enemy, Electrocharged triggers, and the Electro aura is not consumed. This allows for a persistent state of both Hydro and Electro auras on an enemy, which is fundamental to its utility.

The damage dealt by Electrocharged is classified as Elemental DMG and scales with the Elemental Mastery and Level of the character who triggered the reaction. The base multiplier at Level 90 is 1.2. This damage is further modified by the character's stats, including CRIT Rate and CRIT DMG, allowing for significant personal damage contributions from the triggering character. The reaction also benefits from damage bonuses such as those from the character's own Elemental Skill or Burst, or from artifact sets like Thundering Fury or Emblem of Severed Fate.

A key aspect of Electrocharged damage is its periodic nature. The reaction deals damage in ticks, occurring roughly every second. This consistent damage output makes it valuable for sustained pressure on enemies. Furthermore, the Electrocharged reaction has a unique chaining property. When Electrocharged is triggered on one enemy, it can chain to up to three other nearby enemies that are also affected by Hydro. This chain reaction deals 50% of the initial Electrocharged damage to the secondary targets. This AoE potential is significant, especially when dealing with groups of enemies.

The non-consuming nature of Electrocharged also allows for simultaneous application of other elemental reactions. For instance, if an enemy is affected by Hydro and Electro (via Electrocharged), applying Pyro will trigger Overload, applying Cryo will trigger Superconduct, and applying Anemo can trigger Swirl. This flexibility enables complex team compositions that can capitalize on multiple reactions at once, amplifying overall damage output.

## Team Compositions Utilizing Electrocharged

The unique properties of Electrocharged lend themselves to several effective team archetypes. The primary goal in these teams is to maintain consistent Hydro and Electro application to maximize Electrocharged procs and benefit from its chaining and non-consuming nature.

### Fischl and Beidou Focused Teams

Characters like Fischl and Beidou are cornerstones of many Electrocharged teams due to their off-field Electro application. Fischl's Oz provides consistent Electro application, while Beidou's Stormbreaker, when activated, deals Electro DMG to multiple enemies and bounces between them, further enhancing AoE potential and Electro application.

A typical Fischl/Beidou Electrocharged team might include:

*   **Electro Applicator:** Fischl or Beidou (often both for higher Electro application uptime).
*   **Hydro Applicator:** Xingqiu or Yelan are ideal due to their consistent off-field Hydro application via their Elemental Bursts. Barbara can also be used for Hydro application and healing, though her application is less consistent. Kokomi offers strong Hydro application and healing, making her a valuable pick.
*   **Flex Slot:** This slot can be used for another Electro character to ensure Electro aura uptime, a character that provides buffs (e.g., Kazuha for Anemo Swirl and Elemental DMG bonus, Sucrose for crowd control and EM sharing), or a dedicated healer/shielder if survivability is a concern.

The synergy here lies in Xingqiu/Yelan applying Hydro consistently, allowing Fischl and Beidou to trigger Electrocharged reactions repeatedly. The Electrocharged damage itself contributes significantly, and the chaining effect helps clear groups of enemies. Beidou's Burst, in particular, benefits greatly from Electrocharged as its bouncing Electro DMG can trigger further Electrocharged reactions on nearby Hydro-affected targets.

### Childe (Tartaglia) Teams

Childe's kit is uniquely suited for Electrocharged teams due to his Riptide mechanic and his Hydro Infusion state. When Childe's melee stance hits enemies affected by Electro, Electrocharged is triggered. His Riptide ability, which marks enemies after defeating them or hitting them with charged attacks in melee stance, can also trigger Electrocharged reactions if the marked enemy is affected by Electro.

A common Childe Electrocharged team structure is:

*   **Hydro DPS:** Childe (Tartaglia).
*   **Electro Applicator:** Fischl or Beidou are excellent choices for off-field Electro application.
*   **Support/Buffer:** This slot often goes to an Anemo character like Kazuha or Sucrose. Kazuha can group enemies, shred resistances with Viridescent Venerer, and provide a significant Hydro DMG bonus. Sucrose offers similar benefits with crowd control and EM sharing, which directly buffs Electrocharged damage.
*   **Flex Slot:** This can be a healer like Bennett (though his Pyro application can interfere with consistent Hydro/Electro auras) or a shielder. Xiangling can also be used for high off-field Pyro damage, enabling Overload reactions alongside Electrocharged, though this requires careful management of elemental auras.

In these teams, Childe's rapid Hydro application in his melee stance allows for frequent Electrocharged procs. Fischl or Beidou ensure that Electro is consistently available to react with Childe's Hydro. The Anemo support groups enemies and buffs Hydro DMG, maximizing the effectiveness of both Childe's personal damage and the Electrocharged reaction damage. The ability to trigger Electrocharged, Swirl, and potentially other reactions simultaneously makes these teams very potent against groups of enemies.

### Other Notable Team Synergies

*   **Raiden Shogun:** While often associated with Hypercarry teams, Raiden Shogun can also fit into Electrocharged compositions. Her Elemental Skill, Eye of Stormy Judgment, provides consistent off-field Electro application. Her Elemental Burst, Secret Art: Musou Shinsetsu, deals significant Electro DMG and can trigger Electrocharged reactions if enemies are Hydro-infused. Pairing her with a Hydro applicator like Xingqiu or Yelan, and potentially an Anemo support, can create a strong Electrocharged core.
*   **Kokomi:** As a dedicated Hydro healer with strong Hydro application, Kokomi is an excellent enabler for Electrocharged teams. Her Elemental Skill, Джун, applies Hydro consistently in an AoE, and her Elemental Burst enhances her Normal Attacks, allowing her to trigger Electrocharged reactions herself while healing. Pairing her with Fischl, Beidou, and an Anemo unit creates a highly synergistic team focused on sustained Electrocharged damage and healing.

The effectiveness of Electrocharged teams relies heavily on maintaining the Hydro and Electro auras on enemies. Characters with consistent off-field elemental application are paramount. The ability to chain reactions and deal periodic damage makes Electrocharged a valuable reaction for both single-target and AoE scenarios, especially when paired with characters that can amplify elemental damage or provide crowd control.
