# Bloom, Hyperbloom, and Burgeon

Bloom is a Dendro Elemental Reaction that occurs when Dendro and Hydro elements interact. This interaction spawns a Dendro Core at the point of impact. These Dendro Cores are the foundation for the entire Bloom reaction tree, acting as precursors to further reactions.

## Bloom Reaction Mechanics

When a Dendro attack hits an enemy affected by Hydro, or a Hydro attack hits an enemy affected by Dendro, a Dendro Core is generated. Each Dendro Core has a base duration of 6 seconds. Within this duration, the Dendro Core can be triggered by Pyro or Electro, or it will explode on its own.

### Pure Bloom (Dendro Core Self-Detonation)

If a Dendro Core is not triggered by Pyro or Electro within its 6-second lifespan, it will explode automatically. This self-detonation deals Dendro DMG in a small AoE around the core. The damage of this explosion scales with the Elemental Mastery (EM) of the character who triggered the initial Bloom reaction. The base damage multiplier for a Pure Bloom explosion is 1.3 * (Character Level + 100) / (Character Level + 1400) * (1 + EM * 0.0009). This damage cannot crit and is not affected by CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG. Pure Bloom is generally the least utilized branch of the Bloom reaction tree due to its relatively low damage output compared to its more specialized counterparts and the inherent risk of self-damage when multiple cores detonate simultaneously. Teams focused on Pure Bloom often involve significant Dendro and Hydro application with minimal Pyro or Electro presence, aiming to maximize the number of Dendro Cores generated and detonated naturally. However, the AoE is small, and the damage can be inconsistent.

### Hyperbloom

Hyperbloom is triggered when a Dendro Core is hit by an Electro attack. Upon activation, the Dendro Core transforms into a Sprawling Shot, a homing projectile that seeks out the nearest enemy and deals Dendro DMG. The damage of the Sprawling Shot scales with the Elemental Mastery of the character who triggered the Electro hit on the Dendro Core. The base damage multiplier for a Hyperbloom Sprawling Shot is 3 * (Character Level + 100) / (Character Level + 1400) * (1 + EM * 0.0009). Similar to Pure Bloom, Hyperbloom damage cannot crit.

**Raiden Shogun's Premier Role in Hyperbloom:** Raiden Shogun, specifically with her Elemental Burst active, is considered the premier Hyperbloom trigger for several reasons. Her Burst, "Secret Art: Musou Shinsetsu," deals Electro DMG with her normal attacks and plunges. Crucially, the Electro DMG from her normal and charged attacks during her Burst can trigger Hyperbloom. Furthermore, her Ascension Passive Talent, "Dream of the High Purveyor," significantly boosts the damage of Hyperbloom reactions triggered by her by 30% if the triggering character's Elemental Mastery is above 200. This synergy, combined with her ability to provide consistent Electro application and her personal damage output, makes her an exceptional choice for Hyperbloom teams. Her Elemental Skill, "Opening: Prelude to Action," also provides off-field Electro application, further enhancing her viability.

**EM Investment for Hyperbloom:** To maximize Hyperbloom damage, the triggering character should aim for high Elemental Mastery. A common target for the Hyperbloom trigger is 800-1000 EM. This can be achieved through artifact substats, the main stat on the EM sands, and potentially an EM goblet and circlet if needed. Weapons like the Dragon's Bane or Magic Guide (for 4-star options) or Elegy for the End (for 5-star support) can also contribute significantly to EM. The character level is also important, with Level 90 being ideal for maximizing the base damage multiplier.

**Team Compositions for Hyperbloom:**
*   **Raiden Hyperbloom:** Raiden Shogun (Electro Trigger), Nahida (Dendro Applicator), Xingqiu (Hydro Applicator), Kazuha/Sucrose (Anemo Support/EM Buff). This team focuses on maximizing Raiden's EM and damage output while ensuring consistent Dendro and Hydro application. Kazuha or Sucrose can group enemies and provide EM buffs.
*   **Nilou Bloom Core:** While primarily a Bloom team, Nilou's passive allows for enhanced Dendro Cores that explode faster and deal more damage. If an Electro character like Kuki Shinobu or Raiden Shogun is included, they can trigger Hyperbloom from these enhanced cores.
*   **Standard Hyperbloom:** Kuki Shinobu (Electro Trigger), Nahida (Dendro Applicator), Xingqiu/Yelan (Hydro Applicators), Flex (e.g., another Hydro/Dendro for application, or an Anemo unit for grouping/buffs). Kuki Shinobu is a strong alternative trigger due to her consistent off-field Electro application from her Elemental Skill and her ability to heal.

### Burgeon

Burgeon is triggered when a Dendro Core is hit by a Pyro attack. Upon activation, the Dendro Core explodes in a larger AoE than Pure Bloom, dealing Pyro DMG. The damage of the Burgeon explosion scales with the Elemental Mastery of the character who triggered the Pyro hit on the Dendro Core. The base damage multiplier for a Burgeon explosion is 2 * (Character Level + 100) / (Character Level + 1400) * (1 + EM * 0.0009). Like Pure Bloom and Hyperbloom, Burgeon damage cannot crit.

**Self-Damage Mechanic:** A significant aspect of Burgeon is its potential for self-damage. When a Burgeon explosion occurs, the triggering character and any other characters within the AoE of the explosion will take 10% of the Burgeon damage as Pyro DMG. This makes careful positioning and team building crucial to avoid overwhelming self-inflicted damage, especially in challenging content. Characters with high HP or damage mitigation abilities can help offset this risk.

**EM Investment for Burgeon:** Similar to Hyperbloom, the Pyro character triggering Burgeon should prioritize Elemental Mastery. A target of 800-1000 EM is generally recommended for maximizing damage. Weapons like the Dragon's Bane or Sacrificial Fragments can be beneficial. Level 90 is also ideal for the triggering character.

**Team Compositions for Burgeon:**
*   **National Burgeon:** Xiangling (Pyro Trigger), Xingqiu (Hydro Applicator), Bennett (Pyro Support/Healer), Nahida/Dendro Traveler (Dendro Applicator). This team leverages the strong Hydro and Dendro application from Xingqiu and Nahida/Dendro Traveler, with Xiangling triggering Burgeon. Bennett provides healing and ATK buffs, which can mitigate some self-damage and boost Xiangling's personal damage.
*   **Thoma Burgeon:** Thoma (Pyro Trigger), Nahida (Dendro Applicator), Xingqiu/Yelan (Hydro Applicators), Kazuha/Sucrose (Anemo Support/EM Buff). Thoma's Elemental Burst can trigger Burgeon consistently with its Pyro-infused attacks, and his shield can offer some protection against self-damage.
*   **Dehya Burgeon:** Dehya (Pyro Trigger), Nahida (Dendro Applicator), Xingqiu/Yelan (Hydro Applicators), Kazuha/Sucrose (Anemo Support/EM Buff). Dehya's off-field Pyro application from her Elemental Skill can trigger Burgeon, though it might be less consistent than other options. Her interruption resistance and damage mitigation also help with self-damage.

## Dendro Core Generation and Management

The efficiency of any Bloom-based reaction heavily relies on the consistent and rapid generation of Dendro Cores. This requires strong Dendro and Hydro application. Characters like Nahida, Dendro Traveler, Collei, and Alhaitham excel at Dendro application, while Xingqiu, Yelan, and Barbara provide consistent Hydro application.

The number of Dendro Cores that can exist simultaneously is capped at 5. If more Dendro Cores are generated while the cap is reached, the oldest Dendro Core will explode prematurely. This mechanic can be exploited to control the timing of reactions or can lead to unintended detonations if not managed carefully.

The damage calculation for Dendro Cores is based on the EM of the character who *triggered the initial Bloom reaction* (Dendro + Hydro). However, for Hyperbloom and Burgeon, the damage scales with the EM of the character who *triggers the Electro or Pyro hit on the Dendro Core*, respectively. This distinction is crucial for optimizing team builds.

## Nilou's Bloom Enhancement

Nilou's unique passive talent, "Court of Blooming lotuses," significantly alters the Bloom reaction when she is in the party. If her team consists only of Dendro and Hydro characters, her passive allows Dendro Cores to be replaced by the "Bountiful Core." Bountiful Cores have a larger AoE, deal 100% more damage than standard Dendro Cores, and explode after 1 second, regardless of external triggers. Critically, Bountiful Cores cannot trigger Hyperbloom or Burgeon, nor will they trigger Pure Bloom self-detonation. This makes Nilou teams exclusively focused on enhanced Bloom damage and immune to the self-damage associated with Burgeon. The EM investment for the Bloom trigger in Nilou teams is still important, but the focus shifts to maximizing the number of Bountiful Cores generated through consistent Hydro and Dendro application.
