# Freeze Reaction

The Freeze reaction is a powerful Elemental Reaction in Genshin Impact that occurs when Hydro and Cryo elements come into contact. It immobilizes enemies, preventing them from moving or attacking, and can be a cornerstone of many effective team compositions, particularly in Spiral Abyss. The core mechanic of Freeze is the application of the "Frozen" status effect to enemies.

## Frozen Status Mechanics

When a Hydro-applied enemy is hit by a Cryo attack, or vice-versa, the Freeze reaction triggers. This applies the "Frozen" status to the enemy, rendering them immobile and unable to perform any actions, including attacking, moving, or using abilities. The duration of the Frozen status is not fixed and depends on the "Gauge Strength" of the elemental aura on the enemy.

Enemies have an Elemental Gauge, a hidden system that dictates how much elemental aura they possess. Each elemental application has a specific Gauge Strength value. For example, a single instance of Hydro application from a Normal Attack might have a Gauge Strength of 2. A Cryo application might also have a Gauge Strength of 2. When these elements interact, they deplete each other's Gauge.

The Frozen status has a base duration. However, this duration is extended if the enemy has a strong elemental aura remaining when Freeze is applied. For instance, if an enemy has a strong Hydro aura (e.g., from multiple Hydro applications in quick succession), a subsequent Cryo application will result in a longer Frozen duration. Conversely, a weak aura will lead to a shorter duration. The game mechanics are designed such that consistent application of both Hydro and Cryo is crucial for maintaining the Frozen state. The exact formula for duration scaling is complex and involves the remaining Gauge Strength of the dominant aura, but the practical implication is that more frequent and potent elemental applications lead to longer freezes.

The Frozen status can be broken prematurely by dealing damage to the Frozen enemy. Specifically, any attack that deals Physical damage or has the "Shatter" property will break the Frozen state.

## Shatter Interaction

The Shatter mechanic is intrinsically linked to the Freeze reaction. When a Frozen enemy is hit by a heavy attack, such as Claymore attacks, certain Plunging Attacks, or Geo damage, the Frozen status is immediately broken, and the enemy is dealt Physical DMG. This damage scales with the attacker's ATK and the level of the character triggering Shatter, with a base multiplier of 1.5 at level 1, increasing to 3.0 at level 90. Importantly, Shatter damage is considered a form of Physical DMG and does not benefit from Elemental DMG Bonuses.

This interaction means that Freeze teams generally avoid using Claymore characters or Geo damage dealers unless they are specifically designed to benefit from Shatter, such as Klee or Diluc in certain niche setups. For most Freeze compositions, the goal is to maintain the Frozen state for as long as possible, making Shatter an undesirable outcome.

## Synergies and Bonuses

Several mechanics and set bonuses significantly amplify the effectiveness of Freeze teams:

### 4-Piece Blizzard Strayer

The 4-piece bonus of the Blizzard Strayer artifact set is arguably the most powerful synergy for Freeze teams. It grants a **40% CRIT Rate bonus** when the character's Elemental Skill or Elemental Burst is active and they are in the presence of an enemy affected by Cryo. Furthermore, it grants an additional **40% CRIT Rate bonus** when the enemy is Frozen. This means that a character equipped with 4-piece Blizzard Strayer can achieve a **total of 80% CRIT Rate bonus** against Frozen enemies while their Skill or Burst is active, drastically reducing the need for CRIT Rate substats on artifacts and allowing players to focus on CRIT DMG and other offensive stats. This bonus is unconditional and applies to all attacks, including those that do not trigger Freeze.

### Cryo Resonance: "Shattering Ice"

Cryo Resonance, achieved by having at least two Cryo characters in the party, provides the "Shattering Ice" bonus. This grants **15% increased CRIT Rate against enemies that are Frozen or affected by Cryo**. This bonus stacks with the Blizzard Strayer set bonus, further reducing the CRIT Rate investment needed. Combined with the 4-piece Blizzard Strayer, this can lead to extremely high CRIT Rates, making it easy to achieve 100% CRIT Rate against Frozen enemies.

## Perma-Freeze Team Requirements

"Perma-freeze" refers to a team composition designed to keep enemies Frozen for the vast majority of combat encounters. Achieving perma-freeze requires consistent and rapid application of both Hydro and Cryo. This necessitates characters with strong off-field elemental application capabilities.

The core requirement for a perma-freeze team is a **high uptime on both Hydro and Cryo auras**. This means the Hydro applicator must be able to apply Hydro frequently enough to maintain an aura on enemies, and the Cryo applicator must be able to consistently reapply Cryo to trigger Freeze and refresh the Frozen status before it expires.

Key considerations for perma-freeze teams include:

*   **Elemental Application Rate:** Characters with fast and frequent elemental applications are essential. This often means relying on Elemental Skills and Bursts that apply their element continuously or in rapid pulses.
*   **Gauge Strength Management:** Understanding how elemental gauges work is crucial. Applying Hydro with a strong gauge strength (e.g., Mona's Burst, Ayato's Skill) followed by a Cryo character with consistent application (e.g., Rosaria's Burst, Ganyu's Charged Attacks) can maintain the Frozen state effectively.
*   **Energy Recharge (ER):** Many characters who enable perma-freeze rely on their Elemental Bursts for consistent elemental application. Ensuring they have sufficient ER to use their Burst off cooldown is vital. ER requirements can vary greatly depending on the team composition and enemy types, but often range from 160% to over 200% for some characters.
*   **Crowd Control:** Freeze itself is a form of crowd control, but some teams may also incorporate Anemo characters with abilities like Venti's Burst to group enemies, making them easier to freeze and keep frozen.

## Trivializing Mobile Enemies

The Freeze reaction is particularly effective at neutralizing enemies that are otherwise difficult to hit due to their mobility. Enemies like Fatui Agents, Abyss Heralds, and various agile monsters can be rendered completely harmless once Frozen. This allows characters with slower attack speeds or those who rely on precise aiming to deal damage without interruption.

For example, characters like Ganyu, who relies on charged shots that require aiming, can freely aim and fire without fear of being interrupted or having their shots miss due to enemy movement. Similarly, characters who need to stand still to perform their damage rotations, such as Ayaka, can do so safely while enemies are immobilized. The ability to control enemy positioning and prevent them from closing the distance or evading attacks significantly simplifies combat encounters, especially in challenging content like the Spiral Abyss.

## Best Hydro and Cryo Applicators for Freeze Teams

The effectiveness of a Freeze team hinges on the quality of its Hydro and Cryo applicators.

### Top Hydro Applicators:

*   **Mona:** Her Elemental Burst, "Astral Reflection," applies a Hydro "Omen" status that amplifies incoming damage and also applies a strong Hydro aura. Her Elemental Skill, "Mirror Reflection of Doom," also applies Hydro periodically. Mona is excellent for enabling Freeze, especially against groups, and her Omen provides a significant damage buff. Her ER requirements can be high, often needing 200%+ without external energy support.
*   **Kokomi:** Her Elemental Skill, "Jellyfish," provides consistent off-field Hydro application and healing. Her Elemental Burst allows for on-field Hydro application and enhanced healing/damage. Kokomi's consistent Hydro application is ideal for maintaining the Hydro aura needed for Freeze, and her healing makes Freeze teams very survivable. Her ER needs are typically around 180-200%.
*   **Ayato:** His Elemental Skill allows for rapid Hydro-infused Normal Attacks, providing strong on-field Hydro application. While primarily an on-field damage dealer, his Hydro application is potent enough to enable Freeze when paired with a strong Cryo applicator.
*   **Xingqiu:** His Elemental Burst, "Guhua Sword: Raincutter," provides consistent off-field Hydro application through his Rain Swords, which trigger alongside the active character's Normal Attacks. Xingqiu is a staple for many Hydro-related reactions due to his reliability and damage contribution. His ER requirements are typically 180-200%.
*   **Yelan:** Similar to Xingqiu, Yelan's Elemental Burst provides off-field Hydro application that scales with her HP. Her coordinated attacks trigger frequently, making her an excellent Hydro applicator for Freeze. Her ER requirements are also high, often 200%+.

### Top Cryo Applicators:

*   **Ayaka:** Her Elemental Burst, "Kamisato Art: Senho," unleashes a devastating wave of ice that deals continuous Cryo DMG in a large area, applying Cryo rapidly. Her Elemental Skill also applies Cryo. Ayaka is often the primary damage dealer in Freeze teams due to her high Burst damage and ability to reapply Cryo consistently. Her ER needs are typically 130-150% when paired with another Cryo unit.
*   **Ganyu:** Her Charged Shots, especially after her Elemental Skill dissipates, apply Cryo to multiple enemies. Her Elemental Burst also creates an AoE Cryo field that deals continuous damage. Ganyu can act as both a Cryo applicator and a primary damage dealer in Freeze teams.
*   **Rosaria:** Her Elemental Burst, "Rites of Rebirth," creates a field of chilling icicles that deal Cryo DMG and apply Cryo periodically. Her Elemental Skill also applies Cryo. Rosaria is a strong off-field Cryo applicator and provides CRIT Rate buffs to the team. Her ER needs are around 150-180%.
*   **Kaeya:** His Elemental Burst, "Glacial Waltz," summons rotating icicles that deal Cryo DMG. While his application is not as consistent as others, it can be sufficient in certain Freeze compositions, especially when paired with strong Hydro application. His ER needs are around 180-200%.
*   **Shenhe:** While primarily a Cryo support, Shenhe's Elemental Burst provides Cryo DMG buffs and applies Cryo to enemies within its area. Her Elemental Skill also applies Cryo. She excels in Freeze teams focused on maximizing Cryo damage.

The synergy between these characters, combined with the bonuses from Blizzard Strayer and Cryo Resonance, makes Freeze a potent and reliable reaction for controlling the battlefield and dealing consistent damage.
