# Fischl

## Lore

Fischl, the Prinzessin der Verurteilung, is a prominent figure in Mondstadt, known for her eccentric persona and her loyal companion, Oz. Her true identity is Amy, a shy and imaginative girl who works at the Adventurers' Guild. The Fischl persona emerged as a coping mechanism for Amy's social anxieties and her desire for a more exciting life. This elaborate alter ego, complete with a dramatic backstory and a regal demeanor, allowed her to interact with others more confidently, albeit in a theatrical manner. Her stories often involve fantastical elements and a self-proclaimed dominion over the night.

Her relationship with Mona Megistus, the Astrologist, is particularly noteworthy. Initially, Mona was intrigued by Fischl's seemingly genuine, albeit peculiar, claims of royalty and her unique way of speaking. Mona, with her own penchant for the dramatic and her deep understanding of the esoteric, found a kindred spirit in Fischl. While Mona initially believed Fischl's persona to be a genuine manifestation of her destiny, she eventually came to understand the underlying insecurity and the creative outlet it provided for Amy. Despite this revelation, their friendship remained strong, with Mona often indulging Fischl's theatrics and offering her unique brand of support. Mona's astrological insights occasionally touch upon Amy's true nature, hinting at a deeper understanding that transcends Fischl's facade.

Oz, Fischl's raven companion, is more than just a pet; he is a manifestation of Fischl's Electro Vision. While the exact circumstances of its manifestation are not fully detailed, it is understood that Oz is intrinsically linked to Fischl's Electro abilities. He acts as her confidant, her advisor, and the conduit for her powers on the battlefield. Oz's presence is a constant reminder of Fischl's dual nature – the timid Amy and the formidable Prinzessin. His ability to speak and interact with the world further blurs the line between Fischl's persona and reality, solidifying his role as a crucial element of her identity and power. The Electro Vision itself was granted to Amy during a moment of intense emotional distress, likely related to her struggles with social interaction and her desire to protect herself and her imagined world.

## Kit Mechanics

Fischl's combat effectiveness hinges on her ability to deal consistent off-field Electro damage, primarily through her companion, Oz. Her Elemental Skill, **Nightrider**, allows her to summon Oz to the field. Oz will continuously attack nearby enemies with Electro damage. The duration of Oz's presence is 10 seconds, and his Elemental Skill has a cooldown of 25 seconds, resulting in a significant uptime of 100% if timed correctly with her Elemental Burst.

When Fischl uses her Elemental Skill, she can reposition Oz. This is crucial for optimizing his targeting and ensuring he remains within range of enemies. A key mechanic is that Oz's attacks are considered Elemental Skill damage, which benefits from various talents and artifacts that boost Skill DMG. Furthermore, Oz's attacks can trigger Electro-related Elemental Reactions.

Her Elemental Burst, **Midnight Phantasmagoria**, transforms Fischl into Oz for a short duration. In this state, she can move freely and her Normal Attacks are converted to Electro damage, dealing damage to enemies she passes through. Upon exiting this state, Oz remains on the field, attacking enemies for the duration of his original summon. This Burst serves not only as an offensive tool but also as a way to reposition Oz or extend his presence on the field if his initial summon was poorly timed. The duration of the Burst is 1.5 seconds, and it has a cooldown of 20 seconds.

A significant power spike for Fischl comes at her Constellation Level 6, **Evernight Raven**. At C6, Oz's attacks gain an additional effect: when Fischl triggers an Electro-related Elemental Reaction (like Aggravate, Superconduct, Overload, Electro-Charged, or Quicken), Oz will unleash a coordinated attack that deals Electro damage equal to 30% of Fischl's ATK. This effect can occur once every 0.5 seconds. This Constellation fundamentally changes Fischl's role, turning her into an incredibly potent off-field Electro applicator and damage dealer, especially in reaction-focused teams, as it allows Oz to trigger additional damage instances on every reaction. This makes her C6 a highly sought-after upgrade.

## Builds

Fischl's build path heavily depends on her role and Constellation level.

### Weapons

*   **The Stringless (5-star Bow):** This bow is often considered Fischl's Best-in-Slot (BiS) for an Elemental Skill-focused build, especially at lower Constellations. Its sub-stat provides Elemental Mastery, which directly buffs the damage of Elemental Reactions. Its passive increases Elemental Skill DMG by 24% and Elemental Burst DMG by 24% at R5. This synergy makes it excellent for maximizing Oz's damage output.
*   **Polar Star (5-star Bow):** A strong option for general damage, offering CRIT Rate as its sub-stat and a passive that buffs Skill, Burst, and Normal/Charged Attack damage based on stacks.
*   **Aqua Simulacra (5-star Bow):** Provides a significant CRIT DMG sub-stat and a passive that boosts DMG, making it a powerful generalist option.
*   **Skyward Harp (5-star Bow):** Offers CRIT Rate and a passive that adds extra physical damage to attacks, which can be beneficial if Fischl is used in a physical damage composition.
*   **Mouun's Moon (4-star Bow):** Can be a strong choice for pure damage if Fischl is built with high ATK, as its passive scales with the team's total ATK.
*   **Mitternachts Waltz (4-star Bow):** A craftable option that can be decent if Fischl is frequently using her Elemental Skill and Burst, as its passive buffs Normal Attack DMG after using Skill and Skill DMG after using Normal Attack.
*   **Sacrificial Bow (4-star Bow):** A good F2P option, especially if aiming for higher uptime on Oz or if needing to reset cooldowns. The Energy Recharge sub-stat is also beneficial.
*   **Rust (4-star Bow):** Primarily for physical damage builds, as its passive significantly boosts Normal Attack DMG but reduces Charged Attack DMG.

### Artifacts

*   **4-piece Golden Troupe:** This is Fischl's current BiS artifact set for most off-field damage roles, especially at C6. Its 2-piece bonus grants 80 Elemental Mastery, and its 4-piece bonus increases Elemental Skill DMG by 35%. This set directly amplifies Oz's damage output and reaction damage.
*   **4-piece Thundering Fury:** This set is particularly strong for Fischl at C6, especially in teams that frequently trigger Electro reactions. Its 2-piece bonus grants 15% Electro DMG Bonus. Its 4-piece bonus reduces Elemental Skill cooldown by 1 second when triggering an Electro-related reaction, with a cooldown of 0.8 seconds. This allows for more frequent Oz summons and potentially more C6 coordinated attacks.
*   **2-piece Thundering Fury + 2-piece ATK% (e.g., Gladiator's Finale, Shimenawa's Reminiscence):** A viable option if a full 4-piece set is not available.
*   **2-piece Thundering Fury + 2-piece Noblesse Oblige:** Useful if Fischl is the sole Electro applicator and the team benefits from the Burst DMG bonus and potential ATK buff from Noblesse.
*   **4-piece Noblesse Oblige:** Can be used if Fischl is primarily supporting a team with her Burst and the team benefits from the ATK buff, though this is less common for her optimal damage output.

### Main Stats and Sub-stats

*   **Sands:** ATK% or Elemental Mastery (EM) is preferred. EM is generally better for reaction-focused builds (Aggravate, Quickbloom) and at C6. ATK% is more straightforward for general damage and physical builds.
*   **Goblet:** Electro DMG Bonus is generally the best for maximizing Oz's Electro damage. ATK% can be used if Electro DMG Bonus is unavailable or if focusing on physical damage.
*   **Circlet:** CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG is preferred, aiming for a 1:2 ratio.
*   **Sub-stats Priority:** CRIT Rate/CRIT DMG > ATK% > Elemental Mastery (especially for reaction teams) > Energy Recharge (ER). ER needs can vary from 120% to 160% depending on the team's energy generation and Fischl's rotation.

## Team Compositions

Fischl excels in teams that benefit from consistent off-field Electro application and damage. Her versatility allows her to fit into various archetypes.

### Aggravate Teams

These teams focus on triggering the Aggravate reaction (Dendro + Electro + Quicken). Fischl's high off-field Electro application makes her a premier choice.

*   **Example Comp:** Fischl (Off-field Electro DPS), Keqing/Cyno (On-field Electro DPS), Nahida/Dendro Traveler/Collei (Dendro Applicator), Kazuha/Sucrose/Jean (Anemo Support/Crowd Control).
*   **Role:** Fischl provides consistent Electro application for Quicken and Aggravate. Her C6 significantly boosts damage by triggering extra coordinated attacks on Aggravate procs.

### Quickbloom Teams

These teams combine the Quicken aura with the Bloom reaction, leading to Hyperbloom or Burgeon. Fischl's Electro application can trigger Hyperbloom if Hydro is present.

*   **Example Comp:** Fischl (Off-field Electro), Nahida/Dendro Traveler (Dendro), Xingqiu/Yelan (Hydro), Kuki Shinobu/Raiden Shogun (Electro for Hyperbloom trigger).
*   **Role:** Fischl applies Electro to maintain the Quicken aura and contribute to Hyperbloom triggers. Her damage is amplified by Aggravate if the Quicken aura is maintained.

### Superconduct Physical Teams

While less common now with the rise of Dendro, Fischl can still function in physical damage teams by triggering Superconduct.

*   **Example Comp:** Fischl (Off-field Electro), Eula/Razor (On-field Physical DPS), Diona/Rosaria (Cryo for Superconduct), Zhongli/Albedo (Geo Support).
*   **Role:** Fischl applies Electro to trigger Superconduct, which reduces enemy Physical RES by 40%. Her C6 can add significant damage to this reduction.

### National Variants

Fischl can sometimes be slotted into certain "National" team variations, especially those that want an off-field Electro applicator and benefit from her personal damage.

*   **Example Comp:** Xiangling (Pyronado), Bennett (Burst Support), Xingqiu (Hydro), Fischl (Off-field Electro).
*   **Role:** Fischl provides Electro for Electro-Charged reactions and additional off-field damage. Her C6 can be very potent here, adding damage to every Electro-Charged proc.

## Best Off-Field Electro Applicator

Fischl is widely regarded as one of the best, if not the best, off-field Electro applicators in Genshin Impact for several key reasons.

1.  **Oz's Consistent Targeting:** Oz's AI is designed to prioritize attacking nearby enemies. While not perfect, it generally provides reliable and consistent Electro application to single targets or small groups.
2.  **High Damage Ceiling:** Oz's damage scales well with ATK, Electro DMG Bonus, CRIT stats, and artifact sets like Golden Troupe. This means she not only applies Electro but also deals significant personal damage.
3.  **Elemental Skill Focus:** Her primary damage source, Oz, is tied to her Elemental Skill. This allows for artifact sets and weapons that specifically boost Skill DMG, further enhancing her output.
4.  **C6 Power Spike:** At Constellation Level 6, Fischl's utility skyrockets. The ability for Oz to trigger a coordinated Electro attack on *every* Electro-related reaction is game-changing. This means in Aggravate teams, Oz attacks every time Aggravate triggers, effectively doubling her Electro application and damage contribution in those scenarios. This level of synergy with reactions is unmatched by most other off-field Electro units.
5.  **Synergy with Reactions:** Her Electro application is frequent enough to reliably enable reactions like Quicken, Aggravate, Electro-Charged, and Superconduct. In Quickbloom teams, she helps maintain the Quicken aura while also contributing to Hyperbloom triggers.
6.  **Energy Generation:** While not her primary strength, Fischl can generate a decent amount of Electro particles, especially with her Burst, helping to fuel her own rotation and sometimes contributing to team energy needs.
7.  **Versatility:** Her ability to fit into so many different team archetypes (Aggravate, Quickbloom, Physical, even some Vaporize/Melt variations with specific setups) solidifies her position as a top-tier off-field Electro unit.

Compared to other off-field Electro applicators like Beidou (requires Electro-Charged to activate her Burst's full potential and has a higher energy cost), Yae Miko (requires field time to set up totems and has a higher energy cost), or Kuki Shinobu (primarily a healer with Electro application tied to her healing), Fischl offers a more consistent, damage-oriented, and less demanding off-field Electro presence, especially at C6.
