# Bennett

## Lore

Bennett is a young, energetic adventurer hailing from Mondstadt, renowned for his unparalleled and almost supernatural bad luck. Despite his misfortune, which ranges from minor inconveniences to life-threatening situations, Bennett possesses an indomitable spirit and an unwavering optimism. He is the sole member of his adventuring team, "Benny's Adventure Team," as all his previous companions have been sidelined by his persistent bad luck, often resulting in injuries or other unfortunate circumstances. This has led to him being the only active member, though he often recruits temporary help for his expeditions.

His affiliation with the Adventurers' Guild is a cornerstone of his identity. The Guild, recognizing his passion and resilience despite his constant mishaps, has supported him. His adventures, though frequently ending in disaster for him, have paradoxically led to the discovery of numerous treasures and rare materials for the Guild, making him an unintentional asset. His motivation stems from a deep desire to be a proper adventurer, to explore the world, and to achieve legendary status, a dream he pursues with relentless enthusiasm, often against overwhelming odds. His cheerful disposition and refusal to be discouraged by his perpetual misfortune make him a beloved, albeit cautionary, figure in Mondstadt. He views his bad luck not as a curse, but as a challenge to overcome, a testament to his adventurous heart.

## Kit Mechanics

Bennett's kit revolves around his Elemental Burst, **Fantastic Voyage**. This ability creates an **Inspiration Field** that provides two primary benefits to active characters within its radius: healing and an ATK bonus.

### Fantastic Voyage (Elemental Burst)

The healing provided by Fantastic Voyage scales with Bennett's **Base ATK** and his **HP**. It heals the active character to 70% of their Max HP if their HP is below 70%, and applies a **Pyro DMG Bonus** if their HP is above 70%. The healing is a significant burst, capable of fully restoring a character's HP quickly.

The ATK bonus is the most impactful aspect of his Burst for many teams. It is a flat addition to the active character's ATK, calculated as a percentage of Bennett's **Base ATK** plus any flat ATK bonuses from weapons. This means that weapons with high Base ATK are crucial for maximizing Bennett's buffing potential. The ATK bonus is **not** affected by Bennett's ATK% substats or artifacts, only his Base ATK.

**Threshold Behavior (C0 vs C1):**

*   **Constellation 0 (C0):** At C0, the ATK bonus from Fantastic Voyage is capped. The maximum ATK bonus is applied only when the active character's HP is above 70%. If the character's HP drops below 70%, the ATK bonus is removed until their HP is raised back above 70%. This can be a slight inconvenience, as it requires careful HP management to maintain the buff.
*   **Constellation 1 (C1) - "Unstoppable Excursion":** This constellation significantly improves Bennett's utility. It removes the HP restriction for the ATK bonus. Once Fantastic Voyage is cast, the ATK bonus is applied regardless of the active character's HP. Furthermore, C1 increases the base ATK multiplier of Fantastic Voyage, providing a larger ATK buff. This makes C1 Bennett a substantial upgrade, allowing for consistent ATK buffs without HP management concerns.

### Elemental Skill: Passion Overload

Bennett's Elemental Skill, **Passion Overload**, is a Pyro-infused sword attack. It can be charged to unleash different effects:

*   **Tap:** A quick Pyro slash dealing Pyro DMG.
*   **Hold (Level 1):** A stronger Pyro slash dealing more DMG and applying a Pyro Infusion to Bennett's weapon for a short duration.
*   **Hold (Level 2):** A powerful two-stage Pyro slash dealing significant Pyro DMG. The second hit launches enemies. This also applies a Pyro Infusion to Bennett's weapon for a short duration.

The Pyro Infusion from Passion Overload is crucial for enabling Pyro-based reactions when Bennett is the on-field character, especially in teams that benefit from his Pyro application.

## Builds

Bennett's build prioritizes **Energy Recharge (ER)** and **Base ATK** for his Burst, with **HP%** being secondary for increased healing. Artifact sets and weapon choices are critical for optimizing his role as a support.

### Weapons

The best weapons for Bennett are those with high Base ATK, as this directly increases the ATK buff provided by Fantastic Voyage.

*   **Aquila Favonia (5-Star):** This is Bennett's **Best-in-Slot (BiS)** weapon due to its exceptionally high Base ATK (961 at level 90) and its secondary stat, which provides Physical DMG Bonus (less relevant for his support role) and a passive that heals and grants an ATK bonus. The high Base ATK is the primary reason for its BiS status.
*   **Mistsplitter Reforged (5-Star):** Another excellent option with high Base ATK (674 at level 90) and Crit DMG. Its passive provides Elemental DMG Bonus, which is less beneficial for Bennett himself but the high Base ATK remains paramount.
*   **Skyward Blade (5-Star):** Offers a good Base ATK (608 at level 90) and ER substat, making it a solid choice for meeting ER requirements while contributing to his buff.
*   **Favonius Sword (4-Star):** This is a highly recommended **F2P-friendly** and often BiS option for many teams. It has a moderate Base ATK (454 at level 90) but its passive generates Elemental Particles on a CRIT hit, significantly aiding team-wide energy regeneration. This ER utility often outweighs the lower Base ATK compared to 5-star options, especially in teams that struggle with energy.
*   **Prototype Rancour (4-Star):** A craftable option with decent Base ATK (565 at level 90) and a Physical DMG Bonus substat. It can be used if other options are unavailable, but Favonius Sword is generally preferred for its ER utility.
*   **The Alley Flash (4-Star):** Offers a high Base ATK (624 at level 90) but lacks a useful secondary stat for Bennett. Its passive is also less impactful for his support role.
*   **Summit Shaper (5-Star):** While having high Base ATK, its passive requires a shield to be active to gain the ATK bonus, making it less ideal for Bennett unless paired with a shielder.

### Artifacts

*   **4-Piece Noblesse Oblige:** This is Bennett's **BiS** artifact set for most teams.
    *   **2-Piece Bonus:** Increases Elemental Burst DMG by 20%.
    *   **4-Piece Bonus:** Upon casting an Elemental Burst, increases all party members' ATK by 20% for 12 seconds. This 20% ATK buff stacks additively with Bennett's Fantastic Voyage ATK buff, providing a massive overall ATK increase to the team.
*   **4-Piece The Exile:** A good **budget/early-game** option.
    *   **2-Piece Bonus:** Energy Recharge increased by 20%.
    *   **4-Piece Bonus:** Upon casting an Elemental Burst, all party members gain 2 Energy. This is excellent for teams struggling with energy, though the ATK buff is less potent than Noblesse Oblige.
*   **2-Piece Tenacity of the Millelith + 2-Piece Noblesse Oblige:** A hybrid set that can be used if you have good substats. Tenacity provides HP% for healing, while Noblesse boosts Burst DMG.

**Main Stats:**

*   **Sands:** ER% or HP% (HP% if ER needs are met by weapon/substats)
*   **Goblet:** HP% (for healing) or Pyro DMG Bonus (if focusing on his personal damage, which is rare)
*   **Circlet:** HP% or Healing Bonus (for healing) or CRIT Rate (if using Favonius Sword to proc its passive)

**Substats:**

1.  Energy Recharge (until threshold is met)
2.  HP%
3.  CRIT Rate (especially for Favonius Sword)
4.  Flat HP

## Energy Recharge Threshold

Bennett's Elemental Burst, Fantastic Voyage, has an energy cost of 60. To ensure he can consistently cast his Burst every rotation, especially without Favonius Sword or significant ER from teammates, a substantial amount of Energy Recharge is required.

*   **General Threshold:** **170% - 190% ER** is the typical range for Bennett.
    *   This range ensures he can generate enough particles from his skill and potential off-field particle generation to fuel his Burst consistently, assuming he is using his Elemental Skill at least once per rotation and potentially hitting enemies.
    *   If Bennett is the sole Pyro character on the team, or if the team has low energy generation, the higher end of this range (closer to 190% or even slightly above) might be necessary.
    *   If using **Favonius Sword**, the ER requirement can be lowered, often to around **150% - 170%**, as the weapon's passive provides significant energy for the whole team. This allows for more HP% or ATK% substats on artifacts.
    *   If Bennett is paired with another strong Pyro battery (like Xiangling with The Catch or a 5-star Pyro character), his ER needs can also be reduced.

## Constellation 6 Warning

Bennett's Constellation 6 (C6) significantly alters his playstyle and can be detrimental to certain team compositions.

*   **Constellation 6 - "G强度" (G-Strength):** Upon casting Fantastic Voyage, all party members equipped with a Sword, Claymore, or Polearm will have their **Physical DMG DMG Bonus increased by 15%** and their attacks will be imbued with **Pyro**.
    *   **The Pyro Infusion:** This is the most significant and often problematic aspect of C6. It infuses the active character's weapon with Pyro. This infusion **cannot be overridden by other elemental infusions**.
    *   **Impact on Teams:**
        *   **Physical DPS:** Characters like Eula or Razor rely heavily on their Physical DMG. The Pyro infusion from C6 Bennett will convert their Physical DMG to Pyro DMG, drastically reducing their damage output. Therefore, **C6 Bennett should NOT be used in teams centered around Physical DPS**.
        *   **Electro-Charged/Freeze/Melt/Vaporize Teams:** While Pyro infusion can be beneficial for some reactions, it can also interfere with desired reaction chains. For example, in an Electro-Charged team, the Pyro infusion might cause Overload reactions prematurely, disrupting the intended damage patterns. In Melt teams, it can lead to inconsistent Melt procs if not managed carefully.
        *   **Teams Benefiting from Pyro:** For teams that inherently want Pyro application or Pyro DMG bonuses (e.g., some Vaporize teams with a Hydro DPS, or teams focused on Overload), C6 can be beneficial. However, the loss of Physical DMG for melee characters is a major drawback.

**Recommendation:** It is generally advised to **keep Bennett at C1 or C5** unless you are absolutely certain you will only use him in teams that benefit from or are unaffected by the C6 Pyro infusion. Many players choose to lock Bennett's constellations at C1, which is already a significant upgrade, or C5, which provides the full ATK buff from his Burst without the problematic Pyro infusion.

## Why Bennett Appears in Nearly Every Meta Team

Bennett's ubiquitous presence in top-tier teams stems from a combination of factors:

1.  **Massive ATK Buff:** His Fantastic Voyage provides one of the largest flat ATK bonuses in the game, scaling directly with his Base ATK. This buff significantly boosts the damage output of nearly any DPS character.
2.  **Consistent Healing:** The substantial healing ensures that the active character stays alive and, crucially at C1+, maintains the ATK buff without needing to worry about HP levels.
3.  **Pyro Resonance:** As a Pyro character, he enables Pyro Resonance with another Pyro character in the party, granting an additional 25% ATK.
4.  **Energy Generation:** While not his primary role, his Elemental Skill can generate particles, and with Favonius Sword, he becomes a potent battery for the team.
5.  **Flexibility:** His kit is relatively straightforward and his buffs are universally applicable, making him slot into a vast array of team archetypes, from reaction-based teams to mono-elemental teams.
6.  **Low Investment, High Return:** Even with modest investment (a decent Base ATK weapon and ER), Bennett provides immense value.

## How to Use Bennett Correctly

1.  **Prioritize Burst:** Bennett's primary function is his Elemental Burst. Ensure you have enough Energy Recharge to cast it off cooldown.
2.  **Weapon Choice:** Equip him with the highest Base ATK weapon you have. Favonius Sword is often preferred for its ER utility.
3.  **Artifact Set:** 4-piece Noblesse Oblige is the standard for maximizing team ATK.
4.  **Positioning:** Cast his Burst near the active DPS character to ensure they receive the ATK buff and healing. The Inspiration Field has a noticeable radius.
5.  **Skill Usage:** Use his Elemental Skill ("Passion Overload") to generate Energy Particles, especially before casting his Burst if his energy is low. Be mindful of the Pyro infusion if you are not intending to apply Pyro.
6.  **HP Management (C0):** If you are using a C0 Bennett, try to keep your active character's HP above 70% to maintain the ATK buff. This is less of a concern at C1+.
7.  **Constellation Management:** Be extremely cautious with his constellations. C1 is a significant upgrade. C6 is generally discouraged unless you understand its implications for your specific teams. Many players stop at C1 or C5.
8.  **Team Synergy:** Understand which teams benefit most from his buff. He excels in teams where the DPS character's damage scales heavily with ATK, which is almost every DPS character. Avoid using him with Physical DPS characters if he is C6.

By understanding these mechanics and build priorities, players can effectively utilize Bennett to significantly boost their team's overall damage and survivability, making him an indispensable character in the vast majority of Genshin Impact's endgame content.
