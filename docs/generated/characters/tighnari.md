# Tighnari

## Lore

Tighnari is the Forest Watcher of Avidya Forest, a position that places him at the forefront of environmental conservation and the management of the forest's diverse flora and fauna. Unlike many scholars who remain within the hallowed halls of Sumeru's Akademiya, Tighnari chose a more hands-on approach, dedicating himself to the practical application of knowledge for the preservation of the natural world. This decision stems from a deep-seated disillusionment with the political machinations and often detached academic pursuits that characterize the Akademiya. He views the pursuit of knowledge as a means to an end – the betterment of the world and its inhabitants, rather than an end in itself.

His early life was marked by a keen intellect and a natural aptitude for scholarly endeavors, leading him to be recognized within the Akademiya. However, Tighnari grew increasingly critical of the institution's internal power struggles and its tendency to prioritize theoretical advancements over tangible societal benefit. He witnessed firsthand how the pursuit of knowledge could be corrupted by ambition and self-interest, leading to policies and actions that harmed the environment and its people. This led him to eventually leave the traditional academic path, seeking to make a more direct and meaningful impact as the Forest Watcher.

Tighnari shares a close friendship with Cyno, the General Mahamatra. Their relationship is one of mutual respect and understanding, often characterized by Cyno's stern demeanor being softened by Tighnari's more pragmatic and sometimes wry observations. Cyno, tasked with upholding the Akademiya's laws and maintaining order, often finds himself dealing with the consequences of academic overreach or negligence, situations Tighnari is acutely aware of and often works to mitigate through his conservation efforts. Their shared experiences, though from different spheres, have forged a strong bond.

Tighnari's resistance to the Akademiya's abuses is not merely passive; he actively works to protect the forest and its inhabitants from the detrimental effects of unchecked academic curiosity or exploitation. He understands the delicate balance of the ecosystem and is fiercely protective of it. This often puts him at odds with researchers or expeditions that fail to respect the natural order or prioritize their own gains over the well-being of the forest. His role as Forest Watcher allows him to directly intervene, educate, and, when necessary, enforce protective measures. His perspective is that true wisdom lies not just in accumulating knowledge, but in understanding its consequences and applying it responsibly.

## Kit Mechanics

Tighnari is a Dendro Bow user whose kit is designed around rapid-fire, high-damage Charged Attacks, amplified by his Elemental Skill and Burst. His Normal Attacks deal Dendro DMG, but his true damage potential lies in his **Charged Attack**, which can be charged to unleash a **Wreath Arrow**. A fully charged Wreath Arrow deals Dendro DMG. However, Tighnari's unique mechanic comes into play after using his **Elemental Skill, Vijnana-Phala Mine**.

### Vijnana-Phala Mine (Elemental Skill)

Upon activation, Tighnari throws a Vijnana Stormdisk into the air, dealing AoE Dendro DMG in a frontal area and creating a Vijnana-Khanda Field. This field taunts enemies within its AoE, drawing their aggro. More importantly, the Vijnana-Khanda Field significantly reduces the charge time of Tighnari's Wreath Arrows. Normally, a Wreath Arrow requires a full charge to become a Wreath Arrow. After using Vijnana-Phala Mine, Tighnari can fire **three** Wreath Arrows with no charge time required. These special arrows are called **Clusterbloom Arrows**. Each Clusterbloom Arrow, upon hitting an enemy, will create a secondary explosion that deals Dendro DMG to enemies in a small AoE. The duration of the Vijnana-Khanda Field is 4 seconds, and the cooldown of Vijnana-Phala Mine is 12 seconds. This 8-second gap between the Skill's effect ending and its cooldown resetting is crucial for Tighnari's damage rotation, as it necessitates careful management of his Charged Attacks.

### Fashioner's Tanglevine Shaft (Elemental Burst)

Tighnari's Elemental Burst, Fashioner's Tanglevine Shaft, unleashes a volley of six Tanglevine Arrows that home in on enemies, dealing Dendro DMG. Upon hitting an enemy, each Tanglevine Arrow generates one **Fashioner's Tanglevine Shaft** that can track a target and deal further Dendro DMG. The key aspect of this Burst is its interaction with Tighnari's passive talent, **Scholarly Insight**. This talent states that for every point of Elemental Mastery Tighnari has, his Wreath Arrow DMG is increased by 0.06%, and his Fashioner's Tanglevine Shaft DMG is increased by 0.12%. This makes Elemental Mastery a highly valuable stat for Tighnari, directly boosting both his primary damage source (Clusterbloom Arrows) and his Burst. The Burst's energy cost is 60, and its cooldown is 15 seconds.

### Passive Talents

*   **Scholarly Insight**: As mentioned, this talent significantly buffs Tighnari's damage based on his Elemental Mastery. The scaling is substantial, making EM a priority stat.
*   **Unwavering Aim**: This talent further enhances Tighnari's Charged Attack damage. When firing a Wreath Arrow, his CRIT Rate is increased by 20% for 5 seconds. This buff is particularly useful for ensuring consistent critical hits with his primary damage source.
*   **Perceptive Instructions**: This passive talent is crucial for team building. It states that after Tighnari's Vijnana-Phala Mine hits an opponent, his Elemental Mastery will be increased by 60 for 6 seconds. Additionally, when he is affected by the Quicken, Aggravate, or Spread reactions, his CRIT Rate will be increased by 4% for 6 seconds. This talent synergizes incredibly well with Dendro-focused reaction teams, especially Aggravate.

Tighnari's playstyle revolves around using his Elemental Skill to create an opening for rapid Clusterbloom Arrows, followed by his Elemental Burst, and then weaving in as many regular Charged Attacks as possible before his Skill's cooldown resets. The short duration of the Skill's buff (4 seconds) combined with its 12-second cooldown means that Tighnari is not a character who can constantly maintain his optimal damage output. He excels in quickswap teams where he can enter the field, unleash his damage, and then swap out, allowing other characters to apply their buffs and debuffs. His reliance on Charged Attacks, amplified by his Skill, makes him a unique Dendro DPS character.

## Builds

Tighnari's build focuses on maximizing his Dendro DMG, CRIT stats, and Elemental Mastery due to his passive talents.

### Best-in-Slot Weapon

*   **Hunter's Path**: This 5-star bow is Tighnari's signature weapon and his BiS. It provides a high base ATK and a significant CRIT Rate substat (up to 44.1% at Lv. 90). Its passive grants an Elemental DMG Bonus based on 12% of the wearer's Elemental Mastery. Furthermore, after a Wreath Arrow hits an opponent, it will grant the "Path" effect for 12 seconds, increasing the wearer's Elemental Mastery by 40. This effect can be triggered once every 12 seconds. The synergy with Elemental Mastery is unparalleled, directly boosting both his Charged Attacks and Burst damage.

### Strong Alternative Weapons

*   **Aqua Simulacra**: Another strong 5-star option, providing a substantial CRIT DMG substat (up to 88.2% at Lv. 90). Its passive grants HP increase and a significant DMG Bonus, especially when near enemies. While it doesn't directly boost EM, the raw DMG and CRIT DMG it provides are exceptional.
*   **Polar Star**: This 5-star bow offers a good CRIT Rate substat (up to 36.8% at Lv. 90) and a passive that buffs Elemental Skill and Burst DMG, and Charged Attack DMG after hitting enemies multiple times. It requires a bit more setup to maximize its passive but offers high damage potential.
*   **The Stringless**: A 4-star option that is excellent for Tighnari. It provides Elemental Mastery as its substat (up to 221 at Lv. 90) and its passive increases Elemental Skill and Burst DMG by 24% and CRIT DMG of those hits by 48%. This directly buffs his Burst and the Clusterbloom Arrows generated by his Skill, making it a very potent choice.
*   **Slingshot**: A 3-star weapon that can be surprisingly effective on Tighnari, especially at lower investment or as a budget option. It provides a high CRIT Rate substat (up to 31.2% at Lv. 90). Its passive grants a 60% DMG Bonus to attacks that hit enemies within 30m, but the DMG is reduced by 10% if the hit is within 10m. Tighnari's Charged Attacks often hit at a range where this bonus is active, making it a viable budget choice.

### Artifact Sets

*   **4-piece Deepwood Memories**: This is Tighnari's best-in-slot artifact set.
    *   **2-piece bonus**: Grants 15% Dendro DMG Bonus.
    *   **4-piece bonus**: After an Elemental Skill hits an opponent, the opponent's Dendro RES is decreased by 30% for 8 seconds. This effect can be triggered even if the wearer is not on the field. This set is crucial for maximizing Tighnari's damage and the damage of other Dendro characters in the team.
*   **4-piece Wanderer's Troupe**: This set is a strong alternative, especially if you have good substats.
    *   **2-piece bonus**: Grants 80 Elemental Mastery.
    *   **4-piece bonus**: Increases Charged Attack DMG by 35% if the character uses a Bow. This directly buffs Tighnari's primary damage source.
*   **2-piece Deepwood Memories + 2-piece Wanderer's Troupe**: A good hybrid option if you cannot complete a full 4-piece set. This grants 15% Dendro DMG Bonus and 80 Elemental Mastery.

### Stat Priorities

*   **Sands**: Elemental Mastery or ATK%. Elemental Mastery is generally preferred due to Tighnari's passive scaling.
*   **Goblet**: Dendro DMG Bonus.
*   **Circlet**: CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG. Aim for a 1:2 ratio.
*   **Substats**: CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, Elemental Mastery, ATK%. Energy Recharge is generally not a high priority unless you struggle with Burst uptime, but his Burst is not always essential for his damage rotation.

Tighnari's ideal CRIT ratio is around 70% CRIT Rate and 140% CRIT DMG, but this can be adjusted based on weapon and artifact substats. Given the 20% CRIT Rate buff from his passive talent, Unwavering Aim, you can afford to run slightly less CRIT Rate on your artifacts if you consistently trigger it.

## Team Compositions

Tighnari excels in Dendro-based reaction teams, particularly those that leverage the Aggravate reaction (Electro + Dendro). His role is typically that of a quickswap DPS, dealing significant damage during his on-field window and then swapping out.

### Aggravate Teams

These teams focus on triggering the Aggravate reaction (Quicken -> Electro DMG, then Aggravate -> Dendro DMG + Electro DMG bonus) to boost Tighnari's damage.

*   **Tighnari / Yae Miko / Fischl / Nahida**: This is arguably Tighnari's strongest team.
    *   **Nahida**: Provides consistent Dendro application and significant Elemental Mastery buffs through her A4 passive and her own EM scaling. Her Burst also increases the active character's DMG Bonus based on the number of different elements in the party.
    *   **Yae Miko**: Offers strong off-field Electro DMG with her totems, which are excellent for triggering Aggravate consistently. Her Burst also provides burst damage.
    *   **Fischl**: Provides excellent off-field Electro DMG with Oz, especially when Tighnari is on-field. Her A4 passive, "Sparkling Steps," further buffs Aggravate damage.
    *   **Flex Slot (e.g., Zhongli, Kazuha, Baizhu)**:
        *   **Zhongli**: Provides an unbreakable shield, universal RES shred (including Dendro RES for enemies), and comfort.
        *   **Kazuha**: Can group enemies and provide a significant Dendro DMG Bonus through his A4 passive (if he swirls Dendro).
        *   **Baizhu**: Offers healing, a small shield, and Dendro application, further enabling Dendro reactions.

*   **Tighnari / Kuki Shinobu / Fischl / Nahida**: A more accessible Aggravate team.
    *   **Kuki Shinobu**: Provides healing and consistent Electro application through her ring. Her Electro application is generally slower than Yae Miko or Fischl, but her healing is invaluable.
    *   **Fischl**: As above, provides strong off-field Electro DMG.
    *   **Nahida**: As above, provides Dendro application and EM buffs.

### Other Dendro Teams

While Aggravate is his primary focus, Tighnari can also function in other Dendro teams.

*   **Tighnari / Dendro Traveler / Fischl / Kuki Shinobu**: A more budget-friendly option.
    *   **Dendro Traveler**: Provides consistent Dendro application and buffs through their Burst.
    *   **Fischl & Kuki Shinobu**: Provide Electro application for Quicken and Aggravate.

Tighnari's damage output is heavily reliant on his Charged Attacks, specifically the Clusterbloom Arrows generated by his Elemental Skill. His Elemental Burst provides additional damage and further benefits from Elemental Mastery. Therefore, team compositions that can consistently apply Electro to enemies to trigger Aggravate, while also providing buffs to Dendro DMG, CRIT stats, or Elemental Mastery, will yield the best results. The presence of a strong Dendro applicator like Nahida or Dendro Traveler is also crucial for maintaining the Quicken aura.

## Tighnari as a Charged Attack Focused DPS

Tighnari's entire kit is designed around empowering and amplifying his Charged Attacks. His Elemental Skill, Vijnana-Phala Mine, is the cornerstone of this design. Upon activation, it creates a Vijnana-Khanda Field that lasts for 4 seconds. During this duration, Tighnari can fire up to three **Clusterbloom Arrows** with no charge time. These are not standard Charged Attacks; they are special projectiles that deal Dendro DMG and, upon impact, create a secondary explosion that deals AoE Dendro DMG. This mechanic allows Tighnari to unleash a burst of damage in a very short window.

The damage from these Clusterbloom Arrows is calculated based on his Charged Attack multipliers, but with the added bonus of the AoE explosion. Furthermore, his passive talent, **Scholarly Insight**, directly increases the damage of his Wreath Arrows (which Clusterbloom Arrows are a special form of) based on his Elemental Mastery. This makes Elemental Mastery a critical stat for him, directly boosting his primary damage source.

His other passive talent, **Unwavering Aim**, further reinforces his Charged Attack focus by granting a 20% CRIT Rate bonus for 5 seconds after firing a Wreath Arrow. This ensures that his rapid-fire Clusterbloom Arrows have a higher chance of landing critical hits, maximizing their damage potential.

While his Elemental Burst, Fashioner's Tanglevine Shaft, provides additional damage, it is often used in conjunction with his Skill to maximize damage during his on-field time. The Burst's damage also scales with Elemental Mastery via the Scholarly Insight talent.

The core gameplay loop for Tighnari involves:
1.  Using his Elemental Skill to create the Vijnana-Khanda Field.
2.  Rapidly firing three Clusterbloom Arrows.
3.  Using his Elemental Burst.
4.  Firing as many regular Charged Attacks as possible before the Skill's effect ends or his on-field time is up.

The short duration of the Vijnana-Khanda Field (4 seconds) and the longer cooldown of Vijnana-Phala Mine (12 seconds) mean that Tighnari is not designed for sustained on-field presence. He is a quickswap DPS who enters the field, unleashes a powerful burst of Dendro damage primarily through his empowered Charged Attacks, and then swaps out to allow other characters to perform their roles. This playstyle is highly effective in reaction-based teams like Aggravate, where consistent Dendro application and Electro application can be managed by other team members while Tighnari is off-field. His reliance on specific mechanics to empower his Charged Attacks, rather than raw ATK scaling or sustained auto-attacks, defines him as a Charged Attack focused DPS.
