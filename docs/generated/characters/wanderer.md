# Wanderer (Scaramouche)

## Lore

The Wanderer's existence is a tragic tapestry woven from divine ambition, mortal craftsmanship, and profound abandonment. He was initially conceived by the Electro Archon, Baal (Beelzebul), as a prototype vessel, a being intended to house her Gnosis and potentially succeed her. This creation, crafted from the finest materials and imbued with immense Electro power, was designed to be devoid of emotion and attachment, a perfect, unfeeling puppet. However, Baal, upon witnessing the nascent being's capacity for emotion and the potential for it to be corrupted or misused, deemed the project a failure and discarded him.

This discarded creation was discovered by a kind craftsman named Niwa, who resided in the Tatarasuna region of Inazuma. Niwa, seeing the potential and perhaps the loneliness within the being, took him in and gave him the name "Kunikuzushi," meaning "country-destroyer." Niwa raised Kunikuzushi, providing him with a semblance of a normal life and even forging him a new body, a more refined vessel that would eventually become the Wanderer's current form. Despite Niwa's affection, Kunikuzushi's inherent nature, a product of divine creation, remained volatile. He possessed immense power and a deep-seated fear of abandonment, stemming from his rejection by Baal. This fear manifested as cruelty, a hardened shell he used to push others away before they could abandon him.

His early life was marked by a series of betrayals and disillusionments. Niwa himself was eventually forced to betray Kunikuzushi due to political machinations and the demands of the Shogunate. This final abandonment shattered Kunikuzushi, solidifying his belief that attachments were inherently painful and that power was the only true safeguard. He adopted the moniker "Scaramouche," a name associated with a boastful and cowardly character in commedia dell'arte, reflecting his self-loathing and his intention to become a formidable, untouchable figure.

As the Sixth of the Eleven Fatui Harbingers, Scaramouche reveled in his power and the fear he instilled. He was known for his ruthlessness and his ability to manipulate others. His ultimate ambition was to ascend to godhood, believing that by controlling the very fabric of reality, he could erase the pain of his past and achieve true invincibility. This ambition led him to Sumeru, where he sought to usurp the Dendro Archon, Nahida, and seize control of the Irminsul, the repository of all knowledge and history in Teyvat. His plan involved using the power of the Divine Tree to rewrite reality and become a god.

However, his scheme was thwarted by the combined efforts of the Traveler and Nahida. In a climactic battle, Scaramouche was defeated. Following his defeat, Nahida, in her wisdom, erased Scaramouche and his entire history from the Irminsul. This act stripped him of his memories, his identity as Scaramouche, and his connection to the Fatui. He was reborn as a nameless wanderer, a blank slate with no recollection of his past cruelties or his divine origins.

The Wanderer, now without the burden of his past, embarked on a journey of self-discovery. He adopted the name "Wanderer" and began to meditate on the nature of identity, particularly in the absence of continuous memory. He grappled with the concept of self when his past actions and experiences were no longer accessible. His journey is one of introspection, seeking to understand who he is without the weight of his history, and attempting to forge a new path, one potentially free from the cycle of pain and abandonment that defined his previous existence. He carries a deep-seated unease and a tendency towards cynicism, remnants of his past, but his current path is one of seeking meaning and purpose in a world where his very existence was once erased.

## Combat Mechanics

The Wanderer is a 5-star Anemo Catalyst user who excels as an on-field Anemo DPS, primarily due to his unique ability to fly and attack from the air. His gameplay revolves around his Elemental Skill, **"Windfavored,"** which allows him to enter a state of perpetual flight and enhances his Normal and Charged Attacks.

### Elemental Skill: Windfavored

Upon activation, **Windfavored** consumes Kuugo's Stamina (a separate stamina bar that regenerates over time and is replenished by collecting Anemo particles or defeating enemies) to lift the Wanderer into the air. In this state, his Normal and Charged Attacks are converted into Anemo damage and deal increased damage. The most crucial aspect of this state is that it allows him to hover and move freely in the air, dodging enemy attacks and repositioning himself with ease.

The **Windfavored** state has a base duration of 6 seconds, with a cooldown of 10 seconds. However, its duration can be significantly extended. Collecting Anemo Elemental Particles (from his own or other Anemo characters' skills) or defeating enemies while **Windfavored** is active restores Kuugo's Stamina, allowing him to stay airborne for much longer. This stamina management is key to maximizing his DPS uptime. The initial lift-off also deals Anemo DMG in a small AoE.

### Elemental Skill: Kuugo Sakuretsu

While in the **Windfavored** state, the Wanderer's Normal Attacks become **"Gale Blade"** and his Charged Attacks become **"Stagnant Air."**
**Gale Blade:** Fires Anemo projectiles that deal Anemo DMG. Each hit has a chance to restore Kuugo's Stamina.
**Stagnant Air:** Consumes Kuugo's Stamina to perform an AoE Anemo DMG attack.

The damage scaling for these attacks is significantly higher than his base Normal and Charged Attacks. The key to maximizing his damage output lies in chaining Normal Attacks within the **Windfavored** state, as they are his primary damage source.

### Elemental Burst: Kyougen: Shounen Dash

The Wanderer's Elemental Burst, **Kyougen: Shounen Dash**, is a powerful AoE Anemo attack that can only be used while in the **Windfavored** state. When activated, he unleashes a swift, multi-hitting strike, dealing significant Anemo DMG in a large area.

The Burst's damage is substantial, and it’s crucial to use it strategically to maximize its impact. It consumes all remaining Kuugo's Stamina, so it's often best used when his stamina is nearly depleted or when a significant burst of damage is needed. The Burst also has a unique interaction: if the Wanderer has **not** consumed Kuugo's Stamina when activating his Elemental Skill, the Burst will deal additional damage. This encourages players to manage his stamina carefully, sometimes opting to use the Burst before his stamina is fully depleted to gain this bonus. The Burst has a cooldown of 15 seconds and generates 4 Anemo particles.

### Passive Talents

*   **First Ascension Passive: Jade Reborn:** When **Windfavored** is active, hitting enemies with Normal or Charged Attacks increases the DMG dealt by Normal and Charged Attacks. This effect can stack up to 4 times and lasts for 8 seconds. This passive directly buffs his primary damage source while airborne.
*   **Fourth Ascension Passive: The Soaring Blade:** When **Windfavored** is active, if the Wanderer's Kuugo's Stamina is depleted by using a Charged Attack, his DMG Output is increased, and his resistance to interruption is enhanced. This encourages aggressive use of Charged Attacks to maximize damage and survivability.
*   **Utility Passive: Precognition: Windfall:** When crafting Weapon Ascension Materials, the Wanderer has a 10% chance to refund a portion of the materials used.

The Wanderer's kit is designed for sustained aerial combat. Players must master stamina management to maintain his **Windfavored** state for as long as possible, weaving Normal and Charged Attacks with his Burst for maximum damage. His ability to dodge and reposition in the air makes him highly survivable, especially when paired with characters who can provide shields or healing.

## Builds

The Wanderer's build focuses on maximizing his Anemo DMG, CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, and ATK%. His unique playstyle as an on-field Anemo DPS benefits greatly from artifacts and weapons that enhance these stats.

### Weapons

*   **Signature Weapon: Tulaytullah's Remembrance:** This 5-star Catalyst is his BiS (Best-in-Slot). It provides a high base ATK and CRIT DMG substat, and its passive significantly boosts Normal Attack DMG. It also grants ATK% and Normal Attack Speed buffs based on the duration of his Elemental Skill, perfectly synergizing with his kit. The passive provides stacks that increase Normal Attack DMG by 4.8%/6%/7.2%/9.6%/12% for each point of Kuugo's Stamina remaining, up to 30 stacks. It also increases Normal Attack Speed by 10% for 12 seconds.
*   **Strong Alternatives:**
    *   **Lost Prayer to the Sacred Winds:** A 5-star Catalyst that provides CRIT Rate substat and increases Elemental DMG Bonus. Its passive grants increased Movement Speed and Elemental DMG Bonus when the character is on the field, making it a strong option for sustained DPS.
    *   **Skyward Atlas:** Another 5-star Catalyst with a high base ATK and ATK% substat. Its passive provides a DMG Bonus and a chance to summon a cloud that attacks enemies, further boosting his damage output.
    *   **Memory of Dust:** A 5-star Catalyst that provides ATK% substat and increases Shield Strength. Its passive grants an ATK Bonus when shielded, making it excellent in teams with strong shielder support.
*   **Good 4-Star Options:**
    *   **The Widsith:** A 4-star Catalyst that offers CRIT DMG substat. Its passive provides random buffs, including ATK%, Elemental DMG Bonus, or Elemental Mastery, which can significantly boost his damage during its uptime.
    *   **Mappa Mare:** A 4-star Catalyst craftable in Inazuma. It provides Elemental Mastery substat and its passive grants an Elemental DMG Bonus after triggering an Elemental Reaction. This is a good option if you plan to incorporate him into reaction-based teams.
    *   **Dodoco Tales:** A 4-star Catalyst from the Midsummer Courtyard event. It provides ATK% substat and its passive increases Charged Attack DMG and Normal Attack DMG after using an Elemental Skill.
*   **Free-to-Play Option:**
    *   **Magic Guide:** A 3-star Catalyst that provides Elemental Mastery substat. While its base ATK is low, its passive increases DMG against enemies affected by Hydro, Electro, Cryo, or Pyro, making it a decent budget option for reaction teams.

    *   2-piece: Anemo DMG Bonus +15%.
    *   4-piece: Normal Attack SPD increased by 10%. Charged Attack DMG increased by 30% for 5s after using Elemental Skill. This set's 4-piece bonus directly buffs his Normal and Charged Attacks while airborne, making it his BiS.

    *   **4-piece Viridescent Venerer:** While typically used for Anemo support, the 4-piece set's Anemo RES shred is incredibly valuable for any Anemo DPS. If you have a good set, it can be competitive, especially in teams where its utility is maximized.
    *   **2-piece Gladiator's Finale / 2-piece Shimawa's Reminiscence:** These sets provide ATK% bonuses (18% each). A 2pc/2pc combination can be a good temporary option if you don't have a complete Desert Pavilion Chronicle set.
    *   **2-piece Desert Pavilion Chronicle / 2-piece Gladiator's Finale:** This combination provides Anemo DMG Bonus and ATK%, offering a solid stat boost.

### Artifact Main Stats

*   **Sands:** ATK% or Elemental Mastery (if focusing on reactions)
*   **Goblet:** Anemo DMG Bonus
*   **Circlet:** CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG (whichever is needed to balance your ratio)

### Substats Priority

CRIT DMG > CRIT Rate > ATK% > Elemental Mastery (for reactions) > Energy Recharge (low priority, as his Burst is not his primary damage source and he generates particles)

A good CRIT ratio to aim for is 1:2 (e.g., 70% CRIT Rate / 140% CRIT DMG). Given his high base ATK and ATK% scaling, prioritizing ATK% is crucial.

## Team Compositions

The Wanderer's unique aerial DPS playstyle benefits from teams that can provide buffs, healing, shielding, and elemental application for Swirl reactions. His Anemo damage can be amplified through reactions like Swirl with Hydro, Pyro, Electro, and Cryo.

### Core Synergies

*   **Anemo Support/Battery:** Characters like **Jean** (healing, Anemo particles, VV shred with C4), **Sayu** (healing, VV shred), or **Sucrose** (EM sharing, VV shred, Anemo particles) can provide essential support. Jean is particularly strong as she offers healing and can equip Viridescent Venerer to shred enemy Anemo RES.
*   **Elemental Applicators:** Characters who can consistently apply elements for Swirl reactions are vital.
    *   **Hydro:** **Yelan** and **Xingqiu** are excellent choices, providing off-field Hydro application for consistent Swirls and Hydro-infused Normal Attacks. Their damage reduction and minor healing also add survivability.
    *   **Pyro:** **Xiangling** (off-field Pyro DMG with Pyronado), **Bennett** (ATK buff, healing), **Thoma** (shielding, Pyro application). Bennett is almost mandatory for his massive ATK buff.
    *   **Electro:** **Fischl** (off-field Electro DMG with Oz), **Yae Miko** (off-field Electro DMG).
    *   **Cryo:** **Rosaria** (CRIT Rate buff), **Kaeya** (off-field Cryo DMG).
*   **Shielder/Healer:** Since the Wanderer is often in the air and can be vulnerable to certain attacks or crowd control, a shielder or healer is highly recommended.
    *   **Shielders:** **Zhongli** (impenetrable shield, universal RES shred), **Layla** (Cryo shield, CRIT Rate buff), **Thoma** (Pyro shield, can be useful for Pyro Swirls). Zhongli is the premier choice for his unparalleled shielding and RES shred.
    *   **Healers:** **Bennett** (ATK buff and healing), **Jean** (healing, Anemo RES shred), **Kokomi** (Hydro application, healing).

### Example Team Compositions

1.  **Wanderer, Faruzan, Bennett, Zhongli:** This is arguably his strongest team.
    *   **Faruzan (C6 recommended):** Provides immense Anemo DMG buffs and Anemo RES shred. She acts as his Anemo battery.
    *   **Bennett:** Offers a massive ATK buff and healing.
    *   **Zhongli:** Provides an unbreakable shield and universal RES shred, allowing the Wanderer to attack uninterrupted.
    *   **Synergy:** Faruzan buffs Anemo damage, Bennett buffs ATK, and Zhongli provides survivability and RES shred. This team maximizes the Wanderer's raw Anemo damage output.

2.  **Wanderer, Faruzan, Yelan, Kazuha/Sucrose:** A Swirl-focused team.
    *   **Faruzan:** Anemo buffs and RES shred.
    *   **Yelan:** Off-field Hydro application for Hydro Swirls and personal damage.
    *   **Kazuha/Sucrose:** Can provide additional Elemental DMG buffs (Kazuha) or EM sharing (Sucrose) and Viridescent Venerer RES shred if Faruzan isn't using it. Kazuha's grouping can also be beneficial.

3.  **Wanderer, Bennett, Xiangling, Thoma:** A more aggressive, reaction-heavy team.
    *   **Bennett:** ATK buff and healing.
    *   **Xiangling:** Off-field Pyro DMG with Pyronado, enabling Pyro Swirls.
    *   **Thoma:** Provides a Pyro shield and can enable Pyro Swirls. This team can be potent but requires careful management of elemental application and positioning.

4.  **Wanderer, Jean, Xingqiu, Yelan:** A double Hydro, double Anemo team.
    *   **Jean:** Healing, Anemo RES shred (VV), and Anemo particles.
    *   **Xingqiu & Yelan:** Consistent off-field Hydro application for Hydro Swirls and their own damage.
    *   **Synergy:** This team offers excellent healing, survivability, and consistent Hydro Swirls, making the Wanderer's aerial attacks very effective.

The Wanderer's unique aerial combat style, combined with his strong Anemo damage potential, makes him a versatile and powerful on-field DPS character when supported by the right team. Mastering his stamina management and understanding his synergy with elemental reactions are key to unlocking his full potential.
