# Venti

## Lore

Venti is the current Anemo Archon of Mondstadt, known to the world as the God of Freedom. However, his true form is that of a nameless wind spirit who existed long before the Archon War. In the ancient past, Mondstadt was a land ruled by a tyrannical dragon, and the wind spirit, through his nascent powers, aided a young bard in his quest to liberate the city. This bard, who fought with courage and a song, eventually fell in battle. In tribute to this fallen hero and to embody the spirit of freedom he represented, the wind spirit took on the bard's likeness and the name "Venti." He then ascended to godhood, becoming the Anemo Archon.

For centuries, Venti ruled Mondstadt, often in a more hands-off manner, preferring to observe and guide indirectly through his people's own will. He adopted the persona of a carefree, wine-loving bard, rarely revealing his true divine nature. This facade allowed him to interact with mortals on a more personal level and understand their desires and struggles. During this time, he formed a deep bond with Dvalin, one of the Four Winds, who served as his loyal protector and companion. Dvalin was instrumental in Venti's early reign, helping to maintain peace and order in Mondstadt.

However, Venti's long period of relative peace and his trust in humanity led to a significant vulnerability. When the Fatui, under the command of the Eleventh Harbinger, Tartaglia, infiltrated Mondstadt, Venti was caught off guard. The Tsaritsa's primary target was the Gnosis, the divine artifact that allowed Archons to channel their power and connect with Celestia. In the ensuing confrontation, the Eighth Harbinger, Signora, brutally extracted Venti's Gnosis, leaving him severely weakened and vulnerable. This event marked a turning point for Venti, forcing him to confront the reality of external threats and the limitations of his passive approach to governance. The loss of his Gnosis diminished his divine power significantly, though he retained his Archon status and his connection to the Anemo element. He has since been on a journey to reclaim his Gnosis and understand the Fatui's ultimate goals, all while maintaining his bardic persona to avoid further direct confrontation. His relationship with Dvalin remains strong, with the dragon often acting as a guardian for Mondstadt in Venti's stead, especially during his periods of absence or perceived weakness.

## Kit Mechanics

Venti's kit revolves around crowd control and elemental support, making him one of the premier Anemo characters for managing groups of enemies.

### Elemental Skill: Skyward Sonnet

**Skyward Sonnet** is Venti's Elemental Skill. When tapped, it deals Anemo DMG in a small AoE and launches an enemy hit into the air, creating a small wind current. This wind current can lift Venti himself, allowing for aerial traversal and evasion.

When held, Venti summons a larger wind domain that continuously pulls enemies towards its center, dealing Anemo DMG over time. This held version has a longer charge time but offers superior crowd control. The skill has a base cooldown of 6 seconds for the tap version and 8 seconds for the hold version. The damage scales with Venti's ATK stat and his Elemental Mastery.

### Elemental Burst: Wind's Grand Ode

**Wind's Grand Ode** is Venti's Elemental Burst, a powerful ability that creates a large Stormeye at the target location. This Stormeye sucks in enemies, dealing Anemo DMG continuously for 8 seconds. The Stormeye can absorb Hydro, Pyro, Cryo, or Electro elements. Once it absorbs an element, it deals additional DMG of that element alongside the Anemo DMG.

The initial cast of Wind's Grand Ode has a cooldown of 15 seconds and requires 60 Energy. The absorption mechanic is crucial for Venti's team utility. If the Stormeye absorbs an element, it will continuously deal damage of that element for its duration. For example, if it absorbs Hydro, it will deal both Anemo and Hydro DMG. This allows Venti to trigger elemental reactions like Swirl with other elements present on enemies.

A significant mechanic of Venti's Burst is its Energy refund. If the Stormeye absorbs an element, all party members who have that element infused in their attacks (e.g., Hydro-infused attacks from Xingqiu or Mona) will receive an Energy refund. This refund is 15 Energy for each character that has the absorbed element. This makes Venti exceptionally valuable for teams that rely on elemental bursts, as he can help battery characters of the absorbed element.

The damage of Skyward Sonnet and Wind's Grand Ode scales with Venti's ATK stat. However, the Swirl damage and the additional elemental damage from absorption scale with Venti's Elemental Mastery. This duality in scaling means that building Venti requires balancing ATK for his personal damage and EM for his reaction damage and utility.

## Builds

Venti's build focuses on maximizing his support capabilities, primarily through his crowd control and Swirl damage.

### Best-in-Slot Weapon: Elegy for the End

**Elegy for the End** is Venti's signature 5-star Bow and his Best-in-Slot support weapon. Its passive provides a significant amount of Elemental Mastery (221 at level 90) and, more importantly, grants the "Sigil of Remembrance" effect. Upon hitting enemies with Elemental Skills or Bursts, Venti gains stacks. Once he accumulates 4 stacks, he triggers the "Flowing Sands" effect, which grants the entire party a significant boost to Elemental Mastery (100) and ATK (20%). This effect has a cooldown of 20 seconds and can only be triggered once every 120 seconds. The substantial EM boost directly enhances Venti's Swirl damage and the damage of reactions triggered by his Burst, while the party-wide buffs significantly increase the overall damage output of the team.

### Strong F2P Weapon: Favonius Warbow

The **Favonius Warbow** (4-star) is an excellent Free-to-Play (F2P) option for Venti. Its secondary stat provides Energy Recharge (ER), which is crucial for Venti to consistently use his Elemental Burst. The passive ability of the Favonius Warbow generates a small amount of Energy particles upon scoring a critical hit. This significantly reduces Venti's reliance on external ER sources and helps him and the team maintain Burst uptime. A well-built Venti with Favonius Warbow can often reach sufficient ER thresholds without needing ER on artifacts, allowing for more offensive stats.

### Artifacts: 4-Piece Viridescent Venerer

The **4-piece Viridescent Venerer** artifact set is the undisputed Best-in-Slot for Venti and virtually all Anemo characters focused on Swirl reactions.

*   **2-Piece Bonus:** +15% Anemo DMG Bonus.
*   **4-Piece Bonus:** Increases Swirl DMG by 60%. Decreases opponent's Elemental RES to the element infused in the Swirl by 40% for 10 seconds.

The 4-piece bonus is the primary reason this set is essential. It dramatically amplifies Venti's Swirl damage and provides a powerful debuff that increases the damage dealt by all party members to enemies affected by the Swirl. This debuff is particularly potent when Venti absorbs an element in his Burst, as it will reduce the resistance to both Anemo and the absorbed element.

**Artifact Main Stats:**

*   **Sands:** Elemental Mastery or Energy Recharge. If using Favonius Warbow and have sufficient ER substats, EM is preferred. If struggling with Burst uptime, ER Sands is necessary.
*   **Goblet:** Elemental Mastery. While Anemo DMG Bonus is an option for personal damage, EM provides greater overall team damage through Swirl.
*   **Circlet:** Elemental Mastery. Crit Rate can be considered if using Favonius Warbow to ensure consistent particle generation, but EM generally offers more team utility.

**Substats Priority:**

1.  Energy Recharge (until comfortable Burst uptime, typically 180-200% without Favonius, lower with Favonius)
2.  Elemental Mastery
3.  CRIT Rate (especially for Favonius Warbow)
4.  ATK%

## Team Compositions

Venti excels in teams that benefit from grouping enemies and triggering elemental reactions. His weakness lies against enemies that cannot be easily pulled or are too large to be affected by his crowd control.

### Freeze Teams

Venti is a staple in Freeze compositions due to his ability to group enemies, allowing Cryo and Hydro application to hit them simultaneously and consistently.

*   **Core:** Venti, Hydro Applicator (e.g., Mona, Xingqiu, Kokomi), Cryo Damage Dealer (e.g., Ayaka, Ganyu).
*   **Flex Slot:** Another Cryo character for resonance and Cryo application (e.g., Diona, Shenhe) or an Anemo character for VV shred if Venti isn't holding it (though Venti is usually the primary VV holder).
*   **Example:** Ayaka / Ganyu / Mona / Venti. Venti groups enemies, Mona applies Hydro, and Ayaka/Ganyu freeze and shatter them. Venti's Burst can absorb Hydro from Mona, further enhancing the freeze and dealing Hydro DMG.

### Elemental Reaction Teams

Venti's ability to absorb elements and trigger widespread Swirls makes him effective in teams that rely on multiple elemental reactions.

*   **Example 1 (Electro-Charged):** Beidou / Fischl / Xingqiu / Venti. Venti groups enemies, allowing Electro and Hydro to continuously trigger Electro-Charged. Beidou and Fischl provide off-field Electro damage and energy, while Xingqiu provides Hydro application and damage reduction. Venti's Burst can absorb Electro or Hydro, amplifying the reaction damage.
*   **Example 2 (Mono-Element with Swirl):** This typically involves a strong main DPS of one element and Venti to swirl that element and debuff enemies. For instance, a Pyro DPS like Diluc or Hu Tao could benefit from Venti swirling Pyro, reducing enemy Pyro RES. However, Venti's Burst absorption is less impactful here unless another element is present.

### Venti's Weakness: Large and Boss Enemies

Venti's primary drawback is his inability to effectively control large enemies or bosses. His Skyward Sonnet and Wind's Grand Ode can only lift or pull smaller to medium-sized enemies. Against enemies like Ruin Graders, Geovishap Hatchlings, Abyss Lectors, or any boss-type enemy, Venti's crowd control is rendered useless. In such scenarios, his contribution shifts solely to his Swirl damage and potential elemental absorption for reaction triggers, but his signature utility is significantly diminished. Therefore, teams featuring Venti often require a strong damage dealer capable of handling single targets or situations where enemies are not grouped.

## Talent Priority

1.  **Elemental Burst (Wind's Grand Ode):** This is Venti's most impactful talent, providing the majority of his utility and damage through crowd control and Swirls.
2.  **Elemental Skill (Skyward Sonnet):** While less impactful than his Burst, his Skill still provides Anemo damage and utility, especially the hold version for smaller groups.
3.  **Normal Attack:** Venti's Normal Attacks deal minimal damage and are rarely used in his support role. They can be leveled last.
