# Neuvillette

## Lore

Neuvillette is the Chief Justice of Fontaine, a figure of immense authority and unwavering impartiality. He presides over the Court of Fontaine with a stoic demeanor, his judgments often perceived as absolute and final. However, beneath this facade of judicial rigor lies a profound and ancient being: the current incarnation of the Dragon Sovereign of Water. This revelation positions Neuvillette as a primordial entity, predating the current divine order established by the Seven Archons and the Heavenly Principles. His existence is intrinsically tied to the very essence of the Hydro element and the natural laws of Teyvat, making him a unique and powerful figure outside the established divine hierarchy.

His relationship with Furina, the former Hydro Archon, is complex and deeply rooted in shared history and mutual understanding, albeit often masked by their public roles. Neuvillette was aware of Furina's true nature and the immense burden she carried for centuries, acting as a silent observer and, at times, a subtle supporter. He witnessed her performance as the "god" of Justice, understanding the sacrifices she made and the loneliness she endured. This shared experience, particularly the weight of their respective responsibilities and the eventual downfall of the Hydro Archon's divine authority, forged a bond of respect and empathy.

A significant aspect of Neuvillette's lore is his connection to "grief-induced rain." This phenomenon is not merely atmospheric; it is a manifestation of his deep-seated sorrow and the immense power he wields. When Neuvillette experiences profound grief or emotional turmoil, the skies above Fontaine weep with him, a literal downpour reflecting his inner state. This was most notably observed during the period of Furina's suffering and the subsequent events that led to the dissolution of the Hydro Archon's divine authority. The rain serves as a poignant symbol of his empathy and the overwhelming nature of his emotions, a stark contrast to his usual composed demeanor. His existence as a Dragon Sovereign also implies a deep connection to the natural world and its elemental forces, a power far exceeding that of mortal beings or even Archons. This ancient lineage grants him an inherent understanding of the world's fundamental principles, which he applies not only in his judicial capacity but also in his personal life, often grappling with the transient nature of mortal existence compared to his own long lifespan.

## Kit Mechanics

Neuvillette's combat kit revolves around his unique **Charged Attack: Equitable Judgment**. This powerful attack consumes HP to unleash a torrent of water, dealing Hydro DMG in a straight line. The core mechanic for optimizing his damage lies in the collection of **Sourcewater Droplets**. These droplets are generated through his Elemental Skill, **Ousia-Aligned Skill: Separation of Rain and Sea**, and his Elemental Burst, **Ousia-Aligned Burst: Tide of Oblivion**.

The **Separation of Rain and Sea** (Elemental Skill) deals AoE Hydro DMG and creates three Sourcewater Droplets. Its cooldown is 12 seconds. The **Tide of Oblivion** (Elemental Burst) unleashes a massive wave that deals AoE Hydro DMG and generates six Sourcewater Droplets. Its cooldown is 18 seconds and it costs 70 Energy.

When Neuvillette performs his Charged Attack: Equitable Judgment, he can absorb nearby Sourcewater Droplets. Each droplet absorbed restores 3% of his Max HP and, more importantly, generates one stack of the "Past Draconic Glories" passive. This passive significantly enhances his Charged Attack by increasing its damage based on the number of stacks. Crucially, absorbing droplets also reduces the HP cost of his Charged Attack, making it more sustainable. The maximum number of stacks for Past Draconic Glories is 3, achieved by absorbing 3 droplets. Each stack increases the damage of Equitable Judgment by 160%/170%/180% of its original damage. The HP cost of the Charged Attack is also reduced by 30%/40%/50% based on the number of droplets absorbed.

Neuvillette's first Ascension Passive, **Discipline of the Fleeting Current**, further amplifies his damage potential. When Neuvillette's HP increases or decreases, he gains 1 stack of "Raging Tide" for 4 seconds, up to a maximum of 4 stacks. Each stack increases his Charged Attack DMG by 4%. This passive synergizes extremely well with his HP-fluctuating kit and the droplet absorption mechanic, which can both heal and consume HP.

His second Ascension Passive, **The Stillness of the Water**, is a cornerstone of his self-sufficiency. It grants him a 15% Hydro DMG Bonus. Additionally, for each 1,000 Max HP Neuvillette possesses above 30,000 HP, his Charged Attack DMG Bonus is increased by 3%. This bonus can reach up to 45% (for 60,000 HP). This passive incentivizes building HP on Neuvillette and allows him to achieve high damage output without relying on external buffs or reactions.

Neuvillette's kit is designed for him to be a primary on-field Hydro DPS. He generates his own damage multipliers through his Charged Attack, which is further enhanced by his passives and the Sourcewater Droplets. The ability to heal himself through droplet absorption, combined with the HP scaling of his damage, makes him remarkably self-sufficient. He does not require specific reaction partners to deal significant damage, although certain team compositions can further amplify his output.

## Builds

Neuvillette's builds heavily prioritize HP, Crit Rate, and Crit DMG due to his scaling and passives.

### Signature Weapon

*   **Tome of the Eternal Flow**: This is Neuvillette's Best-in-Slot (BiS) weapon. It provides a substantial Crit DMG substat. Its passive grants HP% and further increases Charged Attack DMG based on HP. When HP increases or decreases, it grants stacks that boost ATK (which converts to HP for Neuvillette) and Energy Recharge. This weapon perfectly complements his kit by boosting his HP, Crit DMG, and Charged Attack damage.

### Strong F2P Options

*   **Sacrificial Jade (Battle Pass Weapon)**: This 4-star weapon offers a Crit Rate substat, which is highly valuable for reaching optimal crit ratios. Its passive increases Elemental Skill DMG and grants HP% when the Elemental Skill hits an opponent. The HP% bonus is particularly beneficial for Neuvillette's scaling. It has a limited duration of 10 seconds per 30-second cooldown, requiring careful management.
*   **Prototype Amber**: A craftable 4-star weapon that provides HP% as its substat. Its passive generates HP for the entire party upon using an Elemental Burst, and also regenerates Energy. While not directly boosting his damage, the HP and Energy regeneration contribute to his sustain and Burst uptime, indirectly supporting his performance.

### Artifact Sets

*   **4-piece Marechaussee Hunter**: This is Neuvillette's BiS artifact set. It provides a 15% Hydro DMG Bonus for 2 pieces. The 4-piece bonus grants a 36% Crit Rate increase when HP changes. This set synergizes perfectly with Neuvillette's kit, as his HP constantly fluctuates due to his Charged Attack and the effects of his passives and potential teammates like Furina. The massive Crit Rate boost allows players to focus more on Crit DMG and HP substats.
*   **4-piece Heart of Depth**: This set provides a 15% Hydro DMG Bonus for 2 pieces and increases Normal and Charged Attack DMG by 30% for 4 pieces after using an Elemental Skill. While good, it lacks the Crit Rate synergy of Marechaussee Hunter and is generally outclassed.
*   **2-piece combinations**: Players can also use 2-piece sets that provide HP% (like Vourukasha's Glow or Tenacity of the Millelith) or Hydro DMG Bonus (like Heart of Depth or Nymph's Dream) if a full 4-piece Marechaussee Hunter set is not yet available.

### Stat Priorities

*   **Sands**: HP%
*   **Goblet**: HP%
*   **Circlet**: Crit Rate or Crit DMG (depending on weapon and substats to achieve a 1:2 ratio)
*   **Substats**: Crit DMG > Crit Rate > HP% > Energy Recharge (aim for around 120-140% ER depending on team).

## Team Compositions

Neuvillette's self-sufficient nature allows for flexible team building, but certain compositions maximize his potential.

### Premier Team

*   **Neuvillette / Furina / Kazuha / Jean**: This is widely considered Neuvillette's premier team.
    *   **Furina**: Provides a significant universal DMG Bonus through her Fanfare stacks, which are generated by her Elemental Burst and are easily stacked by Neuvillette's HP-draining Charged Attack and healing from other team members. She also provides off-field Hydro application.
    *   **Kazuha**: Offers Elemental DMG buffs for Hydro and crowd control. His A4 passive grants a Hydro DMG Bonus to the team after Swirling Hydro.
    *   **Jean**: Provides healing to counteract Furina's HP drain and activate Marechaussee Hunter's Crit Rate bonus. She also groups enemies and can act as an Anemo healer with Viridescent Venerer, shredding Hydro RES.

### Budget Variants and Other Synergies

*   **Neuvillette / Furina / Sucrose / Diona**: A more accessible version of the premier team. Sucrose provides Elemental Mastery buffs and crowd control, while Diona offers healing and a shield for survivability.
*   **Neuvillette / Furina / Zhongli / Jean**: Zhongli provides a strong shield, universal RES shred, and interruption resistance, further enhancing Neuvillette's comfort and damage. Jean still provides essential healing.
*   **Neuvillette / Fischl / Beidou / Kazuha**: A "mono-hydro" adjacent team that focuses on Electro-Charged and Overload reactions, with Fischl and Beidou providing significant off-field Electro DMG and application. Kazuha can Swirl Electro and Hydro for buffs. This team relies less on HP fluctuations for Marechaussee Hunter stacks if healing is limited.
*   **Neuvillette / Hydro Support / Anemo Support / Flex**: Teams focusing on Hydro resonance (two Hydro characters for HP boost) and Anemo units for grouping and RES shred are generally effective. Flex slots can include characters for additional healing, shielding, or off-field damage.

Neuvillette's self-sufficient playstyle means he can function effectively even with less optimized teams. The core loop involves using his Elemental Skill and Burst to generate Sourcewater Droplets, then absorbing them with his Charged Attack to unleash devastating Equitable Judgment. Teams that can provide consistent buffs, RES shred, and manage HP fluctuations will yield the highest damage ceilings.
