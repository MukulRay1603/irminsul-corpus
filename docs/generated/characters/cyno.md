# Cyno

## Lore

Cyno holds the esteemed title of General Mahamatra within the Sumeru Akademiya, a position of immense authority responsible for enforcing the Akademiya's laws and apprehending those who transgress them. This role places him at the forefront of maintaining order within Sumeru City and its surrounding regions, often involving direct confrontation with individuals who have fallen afoul of the scholarly institution's stringent regulations. His duties are not merely ceremonial; he is an active participant in investigations and arrests, demonstrating a formidable combat prowess that complements his legal authority. The weight of his responsibilities is palpable, as he is tasked with upholding a system that, while designed for the pursuit of knowledge, can sometimes be perceived as rigid and unforgiving.

His childhood was spent alongside Tighnari, the current Forest Watcher of Avidya Forest. Their formative years were marked by a shared upbringing, though the specifics of their early life together are not extensively detailed. This shared history, however, has fostered a deep bond between them, characterized by mutual respect and understanding. Tighnari, with his more pragmatic and nature-focused perspective, often serves as a foil to Cyno's more structured and law-oriented approach. Despite their differing philosophies, their friendship remains a cornerstone of Cyno's personal life, providing him with a grounding influence amidst the pressures of his Mahamatra duties.

Cyno's Story Quest, "Testament," delves deeply into the inherent tension between justice and law as embodied by his role. He grapples with the moral complexities of his position, questioning whether strict adherence to the Akademiya's laws always aligns with true justice. This internal conflict is exacerbated by his personal experiences and his understanding of human nature, which often falls into shades of gray rather than the black and white of legal statutes. The quest highlights his struggle to reconcile his duty to uphold the law with his own sense of morality, revealing a character who is not simply a cold enforcer but a thoughtful individual wrestling with profound ethical dilemmas. He is shown to be capable of empathy and understanding, even towards those he must apprehend, suggesting a nuanced view of culpability and consequence.

A defining characteristic of Cyno, often brought up in his interactions and lore, is his reputation for telling terrible jokes. These jokes are frequently delivered with a deadpan seriousness, making them even more awkward or amusing depending on the listener's disposition. This peculiar habit serves as a coping mechanism and a way for him to disarm tense situations, though it often has the opposite effect. His humor is characterized by puns and wordplay that are often so convoluted or poorly timed that they elicit groans rather than laughter. Despite their poor reception, Cyno persists in sharing them, revealing a quirky and somewhat endearing side to his otherwise stern demeanor. This trait humanizes him, showcasing a desire for connection and levity, even if his execution is unconventional.

## Kit Mechanics

Cyno's combat effectiveness revolves around his Elemental Burst, "Sacred Rite: Wolf's Swiftness," which transforms him into the "Pactsworn Pathclearer" state. This state lasts for 18 seconds and significantly enhances his combat capabilities. During this period, his Normal Attacks are converted to Electro DMG, and his Elemental Skill, "Secret Rite: Chasmic Soulfarer," undergoes a transformation. When Cyno uses his Elemental Skill in this state, it gains the "Spirit-Channeling: Endsea" effect. This effect causes the skill to deal Electro DMG in a larger area in front of him and, crucially, reduces its cooldown by 3.4 seconds. This allows for a rapid succession of Elemental Skills within his Burst duration, significantly increasing his Electro application and damage output.

The Pactsworn Pathclearer state also grants Cyno the "Endless Waltz" passive. When Cyno's Normal Attacks hit opponents during his Burst, he gains a "Pactsworn Pathclearer" stack. Each stack increases the DMG of his Normal Attacks. These stacks are the primary way Cyno scales his damage within his Burst. The more stacks he accumulates, the more potent his Normal Attacks become. The maximum number of stacks Cyno can accumulate is 12. The duration of his Burst can be extended by landing his transformed Elemental Skill, "Spirit-Channeling: Endsea," during the Pactsworn Pathclearer state. Specifically, if the skill hits an opponent, the duration of Sacred Rite: Wolf's Swiftness is extended by 4 seconds. This mechanic is vital for maximizing Cyno's uptime in his enhanced state and is a key factor in optimizing his damage potential.

Furthermore, Cyno's Ascension 4 Passive, "Authority-Based Execution," plays a crucial role in both damage amplification and extending his Burst duration. This passive grants a bonus to his Normal Attack DMG based on his Elemental Mastery (EM). The bonus is calculated as 0.3% of his Elemental Mastery for Normal Attacks and 0.2% of his Elemental Mastery for his Elemental Skill. This makes Elemental Mastery a highly valuable stat for Cyno, especially in reaction-based teams. More importantly, when Cyno's Pactsworn Pathclearer stacks reach certain thresholds, his Burst duration is extended. At 30, 60, and 100 Pactsworn Pathclearer stacks, the duration of Sacred Rite: Wolf's Swiftness is extended by 2, 4, and 6 seconds, respectively. This means that by consistently hitting opponents with his Normal Attacks and Elemental Skills during his Burst, Cyno can significantly prolong his enhanced state, further increasing his overall damage output and Electro application. The interplay between his Elemental Skill's cooldown reduction, the Pactsworn Pathclearer stacks, and the A4 passive extension creates a dynamic and rewarding combat loop for players who can master its rhythm.

## Best in Dendro Reaction Teams

Cyno excels in Dendro-infused reaction teams, primarily Aggravate and Quickbloom, due to his sustained Electro application and his kit's synergy with Elemental Mastery. His long-duration Elemental Burst allows him to consistently trigger these reactions over an extended period, making him a premier on-field Electro DPS for these compositions.

### Aggravate Teams

In Aggravate teams, Cyno's role is to consistently apply Electro to enemies affected by the Quicken aura, triggering the Aggravate reaction which deals additional Electro DMG. The key to these teams is ensuring a stable Quicken aura is maintained, which is achieved by balancing Dendro and Electro application. Cyno's rapid Electro application during his Burst, combined with his transformed Normal Attacks, makes him ideal for this.

A prime example of an Aggravate team featuring Cyno is:
*   **Cyno / Nahida / Fischl / Baizhu**

In this composition:
*   **Nahida** provides consistent Dendro application through her Elemental Skill and Burst, enabling the Quicken aura and also buffing Cyno's Elemental Mastery with her A4 passive. Her Dendro application is crucial for maintaining the Quicken status on enemies.
*   **Fischl** offers off-field Electro DMG through her Oz, which also contributes to maintaining the Quicken aura and triggering Aggravate. Her A4 passive, "Lightning-Scion Arts," further amplifies her damage when Aggravate is triggered.
*   **Baizhu** provides healing and a Dendro shield, ensuring Cyno's survivability during his long Burst duration. His Elemental Burst also grants interruption resistance and a small amount of healing. Crucially, his A4 passive grants a DMG bonus to reactions based on his Max HP, which can benefit Cyno's Aggravate damage.

The rotation typically involves Nahida applying Dendro, Fischl deploying Oz, Baizhu using his Burst for healing and shielding, and then Cyno entering his Burst state to unleash his enhanced Normal Attacks and Elemental Skills, triggering multiple Aggravate reactions.

### Quickbloom Teams

Quickbloom teams are a variation of Aggravate that also incorporates the Bloom reaction. In these teams, Dendro and Electro are applied to create Quicken, and then Hydro is introduced to trigger Bloom. The goal is to have Cyno trigger the Spread reaction (a sub-reaction of Quicken) with his Electro DMG, while also benefiting from the Hyperbloom reaction that occurs when Dendro Cores are hit by Electro. However, in Cyno's case, his primary focus is often on Aggravate and Spread due to his on-field presence. The "Quickbloom" designation typically refers to teams where Bloom is a secondary reaction, and the primary focus remains on Quicken/Aggravate/Spread.

A Quickbloom variant of the above team could look like:
*   **Cyno / Nahida / Xingqiu / Kuki Shinobu**

In this setup:
*   **Cyno** remains the on-field Electro DPS.
*   **Nahida** provides Dendro application.
*   **Xingqiu** provides consistent off-field Hydro application with his Elemental Burst, enabling Bloom. He also offers damage reduction and minor healing.
*   **Kuki Shinobu** provides healing and can trigger Hyperbloom if Dendro Cores are generated and hit by her Electro. However, in a Cyno-centric Quickbloom team, the focus is often on Cyno triggering Spread and Aggravate, with Bloom and potentially Hyperbloom as secondary benefits.

The synergy lies in the ability to trigger multiple Dendro-related reactions. Cyno's Electro application can trigger Aggravate on Quicken-affected enemies, and if Hydro is present to create Dendro Cores, those cores can be detonated by Electro to create Dendro projectiles that deal damage. While Cyno himself doesn't directly trigger Hyperbloom by hitting cores, his Electro application contributes to the overall reaction ecosystem.

## Builds

### Weapons

Cyno's signature weapon, **Staff of the Scarlet Sands**, is his Best-in-Slot (BiS). This polearm provides a significant amount of CRIT Rate (36.8% at Lv. 90) and its passive grants an ATK bonus based on Cyno's Elemental Mastery. Furthermore, it converts a portion of his Elemental Mastery into bonus Normal Attack DMG when his Elemental Burst is active, directly synergizing with his kit and his A4 passive. This weapon significantly boosts his damage output in reaction-based teams.

Strong F2P and alternative options include:
*   **Deathmatch:** A Battle Pass weapon that provides CRIT Rate and a DMG bonus based on the number of opponents. It's a reliable stat stick.
*   **Missive Windspear:** An event weapon that offers ATK% and an Elemental Mastery bonus after triggering an Elemental Reaction. It's a good free-to-play option if obtained.
*   **Kitain Cross Spear:** A 3-star weapon that provides Elemental Mastery and regenerates Energy for the user. While its base ATK is low, the EM and Energy Recharge can be beneficial, especially for ER management.
*   **White Tassel:** A 3-star weapon obtainable from chests in Liyue. It provides CRIT Rate and a bonus to Normal Attack DMG, making it a surprisingly effective budget option for Cyno's Normal Attack-focused Burst.

### Artifacts

The premier artifact set for Cyno is **4-piece Thundering Fury**. This set provides Electro DMG Bonus and reduces the cooldown of his Elemental Skill when triggering Electro-related reactions. The 4-piece bonus is particularly potent for Cyno as it allows him to use his Elemental Skill more frequently within his Burst duration, leading to more "Spirit-Channeling: Endsea" activations, more Pactsworn Pathclearer stacks, and potentially longer Burst durations due to the A4 passive extension. The reduction in Elemental Skill cooldown directly translates to more damage and better reaction uptime.

Alternative artifact sets include:
*   **4-piece Gilded Dreams:** This set provides Elemental Mastery and ATK bonuses based on the team's elemental composition. It's a strong option, especially if the team has diverse elemental types, as it significantly boosts Cyno's EM, which scales his damage through his A4 passive.
*   **4-piece Gladiator's Finale:** While generally less optimal than Thundering Fury or Gilded Dreams for Cyno in reaction teams, the 2-piece ATK bonus and 4-piece Normal Attack DMG bonus can still provide a decent damage increase if the other sets are unavailable.

For artifact main stats:
*   **Sands:** Elemental Mastery is generally preferred due to his A4 passive and synergy with reactions. ATK% can be viable if running weapons with low base ATK or if EM is excessively high from other sources.
*   **Goblet:** Electro DMG Bonus.
*   **Circlet:** CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG, depending on substats and weapon.

Substat priority: CRIT Rate/DMG > Elemental Mastery > Energy Recharge > ATK%.

## ER Management

Cyno's Elemental Burst has a high energy cost of 80 and a duration of 18 seconds. To ensure he can consistently use his Burst every rotation, Energy Recharge (ER) management is crucial. The exact ER requirement can vary significantly based on team composition, weapon choice, and artifact substats, but a general guideline is as follows:

*   **Solo Electro:** 180-200%+ ER
*   **With Electro Resonance (e.g., Fischl):** 160-180%+ ER
*   **With Favonius weapon user:** 140-160%+ ER

The presence of an off-field Electro character like Fischl, who can generate particles and trigger her A4 passive, significantly reduces Cyno's personal ER needs. Additionally, a character wielding a Favonius weapon can generate additional particles for the team. The 4-piece Thundering Fury set's ability to reduce Elemental Skill cooldowns can also indirectly help with ER by allowing more frequent skill uses, which can generate more particles if the skill hits enemies. However, the primary method of ER generation for Cyno is through his own character particles and those generated by teammates. Players should aim to have enough ER to comfortably burst every rotation without sacrificing too much offensive substat stats. If ER requirements are too high, consider using an ER weapon like Staff of the Scarlet Sands (which has EM but its passive can be very strong) or prioritizing ER substats.
