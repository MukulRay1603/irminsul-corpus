# Ayaka

## Lore

Kamisato Ayaka, the "Frostflake Heron," is the eldest daughter of the Yashiro Commission's former head and the younger sister of Kamisato Ayato. As a prominent figure within the Yashiro Commission, one of the three major organizations governing Inazuma, Ayaka shoulders significant responsibilities. Her upbringing has been steeped in tradition and duty, shaping her into a poised and elegant individual who embodies the ideals of the Kamisato clan. This public persona, however, often masks a deeper emotional landscape.

Ayaka's life is characterized by a delicate balance between her public obligations and her private desires. The strictures of her role within the Yashiro Commission, particularly during the tumultuous period of the Vision Hunt Decree, demanded a level of stoicism and adherence to protocol that often left her feeling isolated. She is deeply committed to her clan's legacy and the well-being of Inazuma, but the weight of these responsibilities can be isolating. Her public appearances are meticulously crafted, showcasing grace and unwavering dedication, yet beneath this polished exterior lies a yearning for genuine connection and a vulnerability she rarely reveals.

Her relationship with her brother, Kamisato Ayato, is one of deep respect and mutual reliance. While Ayato handles the more overt political machinations of the Yashiro Commission, Ayaka often serves as a calming influence and a moral compass. Their shared commitment to Inazuma's prosperity and stability forms the bedrock of their bond. She trusts his judgment implicitly, even when his methods are unconventional, and he, in turn, cherishes her unwavering support and inherent goodness.

Thoma, the "Housekeeper," is a crucial figure in Ayaka's life, acting as a loyal retainer and a close confidant. He provides a much-needed bridge between Ayaka's sheltered upbringing and the wider world. Thoma's pragmatic approach and genuine care offer Ayaka a sense of comfort and stability. He understands the pressures she faces and offers unwavering support, often acting as a buffer against the more demanding aspects of her duties. Their interactions reveal a warmth and sincerity that contrasts with the formality of her other relationships, hinting at a deeper emotional connection. Thoma's presence allows Ayaka to express a more relaxed and authentic side of herself, a rare glimpse into the woman behind the Frostflake Heron.

Ayaka's internal struggles often stem from the conflict between her duty to uphold tradition and her personal aspirations. She grapples with the expectations placed upon her as a noble of Inazuma and the desire for a more personal, less constrained existence. This internal conflict is a recurring theme in her story quests, where players witness her navigating complex social situations and confronting her own limitations. Her journey is one of self-discovery, learning to reconcile her public image with her private self, and finding strength not just in her lineage but in her own evolving identity.

## Kit Mechanics

Kamisato Ayaka's combat prowess revolves around her exceptional Cryo application and damage, primarily delivered through her Elemental Skill and Burst, augmented by her unique Elemental Infusion via her Alternate Sprint.

### Alternate Sprint: Kamisato Art: Senka

Upon activation, Ayaka performs a dash, leaving behind an ice-illusory clone that explodes after a short duration, dealing Cryo DMG to nearby enemies. This clone's explosion applies Cryo to enemies within its radius. Crucially, during the dash itself, Ayaka is considered to be in the **Cryo-infused state**. This infusion lasts for a short duration after the dash concludes, allowing her Normal and Charged Attacks to deal Cryo DMG. The initial dash consumes stamina, and if it is held, Ayaka will continuously consume stamina to maintain the dash.

### Normal Attacks: Kamisato Art: Marobashi

Ayaka performs up to five rapid strikes. Her **Charged Attack** consumes stamina to unleash two swift sword strikes. When her Normal and Charged Attacks deal Cryo DMG, they benefit from her Elemental Infusion. The damage scaling for her Normal Attacks is as follows:
*   **1-Hit Combo:** 59.4% ATK
*   **2-Hit Combo:** 55.7% ATK + 55.7% ATK
*   **3-Hit Combo:** 46.6% ATK + 46.6% ATK + 67.2% ATK
*   **4-Hit Combo:** 40.8% ATK + 40.8% ATK + 40.8% ATK + 76.4% ATK
*   **5-Hit Combo:** 44.2% ATK + 44.2% ATK + 44.2% ATK + 44.2% ATK + 97.7% ATK
*   **Charged Attack:** 143% ATK

### Elemental Skill: Kamisato Art: Hyouka

Ayaka performs a swift slash, dealing Cryo DMG to opponents in front of her. This skill also applies Cryo to enemies hit. The skill has a cooldown of 8 seconds and generates 4-5 Elemental Particles. The damage multiplier for this skill is 212% ATK at Talent Level 6.

### Elemental Burst: Kamisato Art: Soumetsu

Ayaka unleashes a Frostflake Seki no To, which blooms from her very being and grows. This Frostflake Seki no To will continuously advance forward, dealing Cryo DMG to everything it touches. The Seki no To is essentially a powerful, fast-moving snowstorm. At its initial activation, it deals Cryo DMG in a small AoE. As it moves, it releases **Cryo DMG** through a series of sword strikes. The final hit of the Seki no To explodes, dealing Cryo DMG in a larger AoE. The key mechanic here is that the sword strikes within the Seki no To deal damage independently of Ayaka herself, allowing for consistent Cryo application and damage even if Ayaka is not directly attacking. The Burst has a duration of 5 seconds and a cooldown of 20 seconds.

*   **Initial DMG:** 105% ATK
*   **Seki no To DMG:** 42% ATK per tick
*   **Explosion DMG:** 126% ATK

A significant aspect of Ayaka's kit is her **Cryo Infusion** from her Alternate Sprint. After using her dash, her Normal and Charged Attacks are converted to Cryo DMG for a short period. This infusion is crucial for enabling her damage output, especially when paired with artifacts like Blizzard Strayer and characters that provide buffs or debuffs based on Cryo.

### Stamina Management

Ayaka's Alternate Sprint consumes stamina. While it provides valuable Cryo infusion and mobility, overuse can lead to depleted stamina, leaving her vulnerable and unable to perform Charged Attacks or dodge effectively. Players must learn to balance the use of her sprint for infusion and repositioning with the need to conserve stamina for offensive maneuvers and defensive actions. Short dashes followed by attacks are generally more efficient than prolonged sprinting.

## Builds

Ayaka's build focuses on maximizing her Cryo DMG, Crit Rate, Crit DMG, and ATK, with Energy Recharge being a crucial secondary stat to ensure consistent Burst uptime.

### Best-in-Slot Weapon

*   **Mistsplitter Reforged (5-Star Sword):** This is Ayaka's signature weapon and her BiS. It provides a massive Crit DMG substat (44.1% at Lv. 90) and its passive grants an Elemental DMG Bonus. At Stack Level 1, it grants 12% Elemental DMG Bonus. At Stack Level 2, it grants 24% Elemental DMG Bonus. At Stack Level 3 (achieved when the wielder's Elemental DMG is higher than 100% of their Base ATK, and by triggering an Elemental Burst), it grants a further 28% Elemental DMG Bonus. This synergy with Cryo DMG makes it unparalleled for her.

### Strong Free-to-Play / Craftable Options

*   **Amenoma Kageuchi (4-Star Sword):** This craftable sword from Inazuma is an excellent F2P option. It offers an ATK% substat (45.9% at Lv. 90) and its passive grants an Energy Recharge bonus after using an Elemental Skill. More importantly, after the Elemental Skill hits an opponent, it gains 1 Sure-Hit DMG stack. When the wielder's Energy reaches 100%, all stacks are consumed to recover 6 Energy per stack. This significantly helps with Ayaka's Burst uptime, especially if her ER is slightly lower. It's important to note that the passive requires hitting enemies with the skill to gain stacks.
*   **Prototype Rancour (4-Star Sword):** Another craftable option, this sword provides a high Physical DMG Bonus substat (which is less ideal for Ayaka's Cryo focus) but offers a significant ATK% bonus through its passive when Normal or Charged Attacks hit. At Refinement 5, hitting an opponent with a Normal or Charged Attack increases ATK by 16% and DEF by 12% for 6s. This effect can occur once every 5s. While not optimal for her Cryo damage, it provides a solid ATK boost.

### Artifact Sets

*   **4-Piece Blizzard Strayer:** This is Ayaka's premier artifact set.
    *   **2-Piece Bonus:** Grants 15% Cryo DMG Bonus.
    *   **4-Piece Bonus:** When the character attacks an opponent affected by Cryo, their Crit Rate is increased by 20%. If the opponent is Frozen, Crit Rate is further increased by an additional 20%. This set is incredibly powerful for Ayaka in Freeze compositions, as it allows players to focus less on Crit Rate substats and more on Crit DMG, ATK%, and ER.
*   **2-Piece Blizzard Strayer + 2-Piece Noblesse Oblige:** This combination provides a 15% Cryo DMG Bonus and a 20% Elemental Burst DMG Bonus. This is a strong alternative if a perfect 4-piece Blizzard Strayer set is difficult to obtain, or if the team composition benefits more from the Burst DMG.
*   **4-Piece Noblesse Oblige:** While less optimal for Ayaka's personal damage, this set can be used if Ayaka is the sole Burst damage dealer in a team that heavily relies on her Burst's damage and the subsequent ATK buff for other teammates. The 4-piece bonus grants all party members a 20% ATK buff for 12s after using her Elemental Burst.

### Stat Priorities

*   **Sands:** ATK% or Energy Recharge (if ER requirements are not met by substats/weapon).
*   **Goblet:** Cryo DMG Bonus.
*   **Circlet:** Crit DMG or Crit Rate (depending on substats and whether the opponent is Frozen).
*   **Substats:** Crit DMG > Crit Rate > ATK% > Energy Recharge.

### Energy Recharge Threshold

Ayaka's Elemental Burst, Kamisato Art: Soumetsu, is her primary source of damage and has a 20-second cooldown. To ensure she can use her Burst every rotation, a certain amount of Energy Recharge is necessary.

*   **Base ER:** Without any external buffs or specific weapons, Ayaka generally needs around **130-140% ER**.
*   **With Cryo Resonance (Shenhe/Rosaria):** If running with another Cryo character, Cryo Resonance provides a 15% Crit Rate bonus against enemies affected by Cryo, and potentially more if Frozen. This can slightly lower the Crit Rate needed from substats.
*   **With Favonius Weapons:** If teammates are using Favonius weapons (e.g., Favonius Lance on Shenhe/Rosaria, Favonius Codex on Kokomi), the team's overall energy generation increases, potentially lowering Ayaka's individual ER requirement.
*   **With Amenoma Kageuchi:** This craftable sword's passive helps recover energy, potentially reducing the ER requirement by 10-15%.
*   **Against Single Target:** Against fewer enemies, particle generation can be lower, potentially requiring slightly higher ER.
*   **Against Multiple Targets:** Against multiple enemies, her skill and burst can hit more targets, generating more particles, potentially lowering the ER requirement.

A general guideline is to aim for **130-140% ER** on Ayaka herself. If running with a dedicated Cryo battery like Shenhe or Rosaria, this can be reduced to **120-130%**. If Ayaka is the sole Cryo character and lacks ER from substats or weapons, she might need up to **150-160% ER**. It is always recommended to test in-game to find the sweet spot for your specific team composition and playstyle.

## Team Compositions

Ayaka excels in Freeze compositions, where her Cryo application and Burst damage are amplified by the Frozen status effect.

### Premier Freeze: Kokomi + Kazuha + Shenhe

This is arguably Ayaka's strongest and most synergistic team.
*   **Ayaka:** Main DPS.
*   **Shenhe:** Dedicated Cryo support. Her Elemental Skill provides a significant flat Cryo DMG Bonus to Ayaka's attacks and Burst. Her Burst also debuffs enemies' Cryo RES. Shenhe's A4 passive further buffs Cryo DMG based on her own DEF.
*   **Kazuha:** Crowd control and Elemental DMG buffer. He groups enemies with his Burst and Skill, making them easier for Ayaka to hit with her Burst. His A4 passive provides a significant Elemental DMG Bonus to the element he swirls (Cryo in this case).
*   **Kokomi:** Hydro applicator and healer. Her Elemental Skill provides consistent Hydro application off-field for freezing. Her healing ensures Ayaka and the team stay healthy, and her TTDS (Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers) can provide an ATK buff if equipped.

**Rotation Example:** Shenhe EQ -> Kazuha E(D)Q -> Kokomi E -> Ayaka Dash + NAs/CA + Q.

### Ayaka + Mona + Venti + Diona (Classic Freeze)

A classic and highly effective Freeze team, particularly strong before Shenhe's release.
*   **Ayaka:** Main DPS.
*   **Mona:** Hydro applicator and debuffer. Her Elemental Skill applies Hydro and taunts enemies. Her Elemental Burst applies the Omen debuff, significantly increasing DMG taken by enemies.
*   **Venti:** Premier crowd control. His Elemental Burst groups enemies into a vortex, allowing Ayaka's Burst to hit all of them simultaneously. He also shreds Cryo RES if he swirls Cryo.
*   **Diona:** Healer, shielder, and Cryo battery. Her Elemental Skill provides Cryo application and a shield. Her Burst heals the team and provides Cryo application. She can also provide energy for Ayaka.

**Rotation Example:** Diona E(hold)Q -> Venti EQ -> Mona EQ -> Ayaka Dash + NAs/CA + Q.

### Other Viable Compositions:

*   **Ayaka + Ganyu + Shenhe + Kokomi:** A double Cryo team that maximizes Cryo Resonance and allows Ganyu to contribute significant off-field Cryo damage with her Burst.
*   **Ayaka + Rosaria + Kazuha + Barbara:** A more accessible Freeze team. Rosaria acts as a Cryo battery and provides Crit Rate buffs. Barbara offers healing and Hydro application.
*   **Ayaka + Xingqiu + Kazuha + Diona:** While Xingqiu provides excellent Hydro application, his single-target focus can make freezing less consistent against multiple enemies compared to Mona or Kokomi. However, his damage reduction and minor healing are beneficial.

When building Ayaka teams, the core principle is to ensure consistent Hydro application for freezing, strong Cryo buffs and debuffs, crowd control to group enemies, and sufficient healing or shielding. The synergy between Ayaka's Burst and crowd control abilities is paramount for maximizing her damage output.
