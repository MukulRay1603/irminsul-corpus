# Yae Miko

## Lore

Yae Miko, the Guuji of Grand Narukami Shrine, is a Kitsune of immense age and power, boasting a lineage stretching back millennia. She is the longest-standing companion and confidante of the Electro Archon, Raiden Shogun (Ei), a relationship forged over centuries of shared existence and quiet observation. Yae Miko's true nature as a Kitsune, a fox spirit from Japanese folklore, imbues her with a playful, often mischievous, yet profoundly wise demeanor. She possesses an unparalleled understanding of Inazuma's history, its people, and the intricate workings of the divine, having witnessed its evolution firsthand.

For centuries, while Ei meditated in the Plane of Euthymia, Yae Miko served as the de facto guardian of Inazuma, a role that extended far beyond her clerical duties. She managed the affairs of the Grand Narukami Shrine, a significant spiritual and political institution, and acted as a crucial intermediary between the divine and the mortal realms. Her centuries of watching over Inazuma were marked by a unique blend of detached amusement and genuine concern. She observed the follies and triumphs of humanity with a keen eye, often intervening subtly to steer events or offer guidance, though she rarely revealed the full extent of her knowledge or power.

The emotional dynamic between Yae Miko and Ei is exceptionally complex, characterized by a deep, unspoken bond forged through shared loneliness and mutual respect. Yae Miko is one of the few beings who can genuinely understand Ei's burdens, her isolation, and the weight of her responsibilities as an Archon. She often teases Ei, a testament to their familiarity and her ability to break through the Archon's stoic facade. Yet, beneath the playful banter lies a profound loyalty and a shared history that binds them irrevocably. Yae Miko understands Ei's desire for eternity and the sacrifices she has made, and she has consistently supported Ei's vision, even when it meant navigating difficult truths or making difficult choices. Her existence is intertwined with Ei's, and her presence provides a grounding force for the eternally meditating Archon, a reminder of the world she is protecting.

Yae Miko's involvement in major Inazuman events, such as the Vision Hunt Decree and the subsequent rebellion, showcased her strategic acumen and her ability to manipulate situations from the shadows. While she outwardly appeared to be a neutral observer or even a supporter of the decree, her actions were often aimed at mitigating its harshest effects and paving the way for a more balanced resolution. She played a pivotal role in guiding the Traveler, providing crucial information and assistance, demonstrating that her ultimate allegiance lies with the well-being of Inazuma and its people, rather than blind adherence to any single ideology. Her Kitsune nature allows her to see the bigger picture, understanding that true eternity might not be stagnation, but rather a dynamic balance that allows for growth and change.

## Kit Mechanics

Yae Miko's combat kit revolves around her unique Electro application and damage-dealing capabilities, primarily through her Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst.

### Elemental Skill: Great Illusory Art

Great Illusory Art allows Yae Miko to dash forward, leaving behind a **Sesshou Sakura** totem. This dash has a short cooldown of 4 seconds and consumes 30 Energy. She can have up to **3 Sesshou Sakura** active simultaneously. Each totem has its own independent cooldown of 10 seconds.

The Sesshou Sakura totems periodically strike a nearby enemy with **Electro damage**. The damage dealt by these totems scales with Yae Miko's **ATK** and **Elemental Mastery**. Crucially, the totems possess a **chaining mechanic**: when a Sesshou Sakura strikes an enemy, it can trigger additional strikes on other nearby enemies. The number of enemies a totem can strike increases based on the number of totems deployed.
*   **1 Sesshou Sakura:** Strikes 1 enemy.
*   **2 Sesshou Sakura:** Strikes 1 enemy, with a chance to strike a second nearby enemy.
*   **3 Sesshou Sakura:** Strikes 1 enemy, with a chance to strike up to 2 additional nearby enemies.

Furthermore, the damage dealt by Sesshou Sakura totems is **Electro DMG**. This damage can trigger elemental reactions like Overload, Electro-Charged, Superconduct, Aggravate, and Hyperbloom. The totems will prioritize striking enemies within their range. If there are no enemies, they will strike the ground, applying Electro.

A key aspect of Yae Miko's kit is the **leveling mechanic** of her Sesshou Sakura. The damage of each totem increases based on the number of totems deployed:
*   **Level I (1 Totem):** Base damage.
*   **Level II (2 Totems):** Damage increased by 20%.
*   **Level III (3 Totems):** Damage increased by 40%.

This means that deploying all three totems is essential for maximizing their damage output. The totems have a duration of 14 seconds, and their placement is crucial for optimal coverage and chaining. Players should aim to place them strategically to cover areas where enemies are likely to gather or move. The totems will automatically target enemies within their range, but their effectiveness is amplified by enemy density and Yae Miko's own positioning.

### Elemental Burst: Secret Art: Tenko Kenshin

Yae Miko's Elemental Burst, Secret Art: Tenko Kenshin, unleashes a devastating strike of **Electro damage** in a large AoE. The initial damage of the Burst is significant and scales with Yae Miko's **ATK** and **Elemental Mastery**.

The true power of her Burst lies in its synergy with her Elemental Skill. Upon casting Secret Art: Tenko Kenshin, Yae Miko **detonates all active Sesshou Sakura totems**. Each detonated totem deals additional **Electro damage** to enemies within its range. The damage of this detonation is also influenced by Yae Miko's ATK and Elemental Mastery.

The Burst has a base energy cost of **80**, and its cooldown is **22 seconds**. This high energy cost necessitates careful Energy Recharge management.

A unique aspect of the Burst is its **"Tenko Rejuvenation"** effect. For each level of Sesshou Sakura that was active when the Burst was cast, Yae Miko gains a **"Tenko Electro Blossom"**. These blossoms deal additional Electro damage. The damage of these blossoms scales with Yae Miko's **Elemental Mastery**. This mechanic further incentivizes maintaining three Sesshou Sakura totems before using her Burst.

The Burst animation is relatively quick, allowing Yae Miko to return to the field or switch to another character efficiently. The combination of the initial Burst damage and the subsequent totem detonations provides a massive burst of Electro damage, making her an excellent sub-DPS or burst damage dealer.

### Totem Placement Strategy

Effective Sesshou Sakura totem placement is paramount for maximizing Yae Miko's damage. The totems have a fixed range and will target enemies within that radius. Players should aim to deploy them in a way that covers the most area and allows for maximum enemy engagement.

Common strategies include:
*   **Corner Placement:** Placing totems in the corners of the combat arena can ensure they cover a wide area and are less likely to be destroyed by enemy attacks.
*   **Strategic Spacing:** Spacing totems evenly can provide overlapping coverage and increase the chances of chaining attacks.
*   **Proximity to Enemies:** While totems target automatically, placing them closer to where enemies are expected to be can ensure consistent targeting.
*   **Burst Synergy:** Remember to have all three totems active before casting her Elemental Burst to maximize the detonation damage and gain the Tenko Rejuvenation stacks.

The totems themselves have a small amount of HP and can be destroyed by enemy attacks, especially in challenging content like the Spiral Abyss. Their placement should consider enemy attack patterns and AoE damage.

## Builds

Yae Miko's build focuses on maximizing her Electro damage, which scales with both ATK and Elemental Mastery, and ensuring sufficient Energy Recharge for her high-cost Elemental Burst.

### Signature Weapon

*   **Kagura's Verity:** Yae Miko's signature 5-star catalyst is her Best-in-Slot (BiS) weapon. It provides a significant **CRIT DMG** substat, which is highly valuable. Its passive, **"The Ibaraki Kintou"**, grants an Elemental DMG Bonus upon using an Elemental Skill. At R1, this bonus starts at 12% and increases by 24% for each stack, up to 3 stacks. When the maximum number of stacks is reached, the Elemental Burst DMG is increased by 48%. This passive synergizes perfectly with Yae Miko's kit, as she uses her Elemental Skill multiple times to deploy totems before her Burst. The stacks are gained by casting her Elemental Skill, and the bonus applies to her Electro DMG and Burst damage.

### Strong Alternatives

*   **The Widsith:** A strong 4-star option, especially for players who don't have Kagura's Verity. It offers **CRIT DMG** as a substat. Its passive provides random buffs to the wielder: an ATK buff, an Elemental DMG Bonus, or Elemental Mastery. While the randomness can be a drawback, the potential for high damage output makes it a very competitive choice. The Elemental Mastery buff is particularly beneficial for Yae Miko in reaction-based teams.
*   **Skyward Atlas:** A standard 5-star option that provides a substantial **ATK%** substat. Its passive grants an Elemental DMG Bonus and has a chance to trigger a cloud that deals additional AoE damage. While not as specialized as Kagura's Verity, the raw stats and consistent damage bonus make it a solid choice.
*   **Lost Prayer to the Sacred Winds:** Another 5-star catalyst that offers **CRIT Rate** as a substat. Its passive grants increased Movement SPD and an Elemental DMG Bonus that increases over time while in combat. This provides a consistent damage boost, especially if Yae Miko stays on the field for extended periods.

### Budget/F2P Options

*   **Mappa Mare:** A 4-star craftable catalyst. It provides **Elemental Mastery** as a substat, which is excellent for reaction-based teams. Its passive grants an Elemental DMG Bonus after triggering an elemental reaction, stacking up to 4 times. This is a good F2P option for players focusing on Aggravate or Hyperbloom.
*   **Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers (TTDS):** A 3-star catalyst that offers a massive **HP%** substat but a very low base ATK. Its passive grants a significant ATK buff to the next character switched in. While it doesn't directly boost Yae Miko's personal damage, it's an incredibly powerful support tool for her teammates, making it a viable option in specific team compositions where her personal damage is less critical than her team's overall output.
*   **Prototype Amber:** While primarily a support catalyst, its passive provides HP and Energy Recharge, which can help with Yae Miko's ER needs and survivability. However, its damage-boosting capabilities are minimal.

### Artifact Sets

*   **4-piece Golden Troupe:** This is Yae Miko's current Best-in-Slot artifact set.
    *   2-piece: Increases Elemental Skill DMG by 20%.
    *   4-piece: Increases Elemental Skill DMG by 25% of the wearer's Elemental Mastery. This effect is maintained for 8s after casting an Elemental Skill. It can be triggered once every 12s.
    This set directly buffs her Sesshou Sakura totems, which are her primary source of damage outside of her Burst. The EM scaling component of the 4-piece bonus also synergizes well with her kit, especially in reaction-heavy teams.
*   **2-piece Thundering Fury / 2-piece ATK% Set (e.g., Gladiator's Finale, Shimenawa's Reminiscence):** A viable option if 4-piece Golden Troupe is not readily available. The 2-piece Thundering Fury increases Electro DMG by 15%, while the 2-piece ATK% sets provide a 18% ATK boost. This combination offers a good balance of stats.
*   **2-piece ATK% / 2-piece ATK%:** A straightforward and effective option, especially if focusing on raw ATK scaling.
*   **4-piece Emblem of Severed Fate:** While primarily for characters who rely on their Burst for damage, Yae Miko can utilize this set if her Energy Recharge needs are very high and her Burst is a significant portion of her damage. The ER bonus from the 2-piece and the Burst DMG bonus from the 4-piece can be beneficial, but it generally underperforms compared to Golden Troupe for her totem damage.

### Stat Priorities

*   **Sands:** ATK% or Elemental Mastery (EM) depending on team composition. If in a reaction-heavy team (e.g., Aggravate with high EM buffs), EM Sands can be superior. Otherwise, ATK% Sands is generally preferred.
*   **Goblet:** Electro DMG Bonus.
*   **Circlet:** CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG (aim for a 1:2 ratio).
*   **Substats:** CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, ATK%, Elemental Mastery, Energy Recharge.

### Energy Recharge (ER) Management

Yae Miko's Elemental Burst costs 80 Energy, making ER a crucial stat. The exact ER requirement varies significantly based on her team composition, weapon, and artifact set.

*   **General Target:** A common target for Yae Miko is around **140-160% ER** when she is the sole Electro character and has no ER-providing teammates.
*   **With Electro Resonance (e.g., with Raiden Shogun):** The ER requirement can be reduced to **120-140%**.
*   **With Favonius Weapons on Teammates:** If teammates are equipped with Favonius weapons, the requirement can be lowered further.
*   **4-piece Emblem of Severed Fate:** If using this set, the ER substat is more valuable as it directly contributes to Burst damage.

It is highly recommended to test Yae Miko's ER in practice to determine her personal needs within a specific team.

## Team Compositions

Yae Miko excels in various Electro-based team compositions, particularly those that benefit from consistent off-field Electro application and her high burst damage.

### Aggravate Teams

Yae Miko is a premier choice for Aggravate teams due to her ability to apply Electro consistently with her Sesshou Sakura totems, which can trigger the Aggravate reaction (Electro + Dendro) frequently.

*   **Core:** Yae Miko (Electro DPS/Sub-DPS), Nahida (Dendro Applicator/Buffer), Kazuha/Sucrose (Anemo Support/Crowd Control/DMG Buffer).
*   **Flex Slot:** Zhongli (Shielder/Universal RES Shred), Baizhu (Healer/Dendro Application/Interruption Resistance), Fischl (Off-field Electro DMG/Battery), another Electro character for Electro Resonance.
*   **Strategy:** Nahida applies Dendro, Yae Miko deploys totems for Electro application, and Kazuha groups enemies and buffs Electro DMG. The constant Electro application from Yae's totems triggers Aggravate, significantly boosting damage. Yae's Burst provides a large burst of damage that also benefits from Aggravate.

### Hyperbloom Trigger

While not the primary trigger, Yae Miko can function as a trigger for Hyperbloom (Dendro + Electro + Hydro -> Bloom -> Electro hits Bloom seed) if built with sufficient Elemental Mastery. However, characters like Kuki Shinobu or Raiden Shogun are generally preferred for this role due to their faster Electro application and better EM scaling.

*   **Example Comp:** Nahida (Dendro), Xingqiu/Yelan (Hydro), Yae Miko (Electro Trigger), Kazuha/Sucrose (Anemo Support).
*   **Strategy:** Nahida and Xingqiu/Yelan create Dendro Cores. Yae Miko's totems can hit these cores, but her Electro application might not be fast enough to consistently trigger Hyperbloom compared to dedicated triggers. If Yae Miko is built with high EM and her totems are hitting many cores, she can contribute significantly.

### Quicken Teams (General)

Beyond Aggravate, Yae Miko fits well into general Quicken teams, where the goal is to maintain the Quicken aura on enemies for consistent Electro and Dendro reactions.

*   **Example Comp:** Yae Miko (Electro), Nahida/Dendro Traveler (Dendro), Kuki Shinobu (Healer/Electro Resonance/Quicken Support), Kazuha/Sucrose (Anemo Support).
*   **Strategy:** This team focuses on maintaining the Quicken aura and then amplifying damage through Aggravate or Spread. Yae Miko provides consistent Electro application, while Kuki Shinobu offers healing and Electro Resonance.

### Mono-Electro Teams

Yae Miko can also shine in teams that focus on Electro damage amplification, especially with characters like Raiden Shogun.

*   **Example Comp:** Yae Miko (Electro Sub-DPS), Raiden Shogun (Electro DPS/Battery), Kazuha (Anemo Support), Bennett (ATK Buffer/Healer).
*   **Strategy:** This team maximizes Electro damage by having two strong Electro characters and an Anemo buffer. Raiden Shogun's Burst provides significant damage, and her ability to generate Electro particles helps with Yae Miko's ER needs. Bennett's ATK buff further amplifies their damage.

### Yae Miko as Burst DPS

In teams where her personal damage is the focus, Yae Miko can be paired with strong buffers and debuffers.

*   **Example Comp:** Yae Miko (Burst DPS), Bennett (ATK Buffer), Kazuha (DMG Buffer/RES Shred), Zhongli (Shielder/Universal RES Shred).
*   **Strategy:** This composition maximizes Yae Miko's damage output during her Burst window. Bennett provides a massive ATK buff, Kazuha groups enemies and buffs Electro DMG, and Zhongli offers a shield and reduces enemy Electro RES. Yae Miko would deploy her totems, then use her Burst for massive damage, potentially swapping out before the totems expire.
