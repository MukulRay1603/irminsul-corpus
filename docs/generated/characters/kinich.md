# Kinich

## Lore

Kinich is a prominent figure from the Scions of the Canopy, a tribe deeply intertwined with the Dragonlords of Natlan. He serves as a representative of his people, embodying their connection to these ancient beings and their role in the nation's defense. His most significant relationship is with his Dragonlord companion, Ajaw. Their dynamic is characterized by a constant, often humorous, bickering that belies a profound partnership and mutual reliance. This constant banter is not a sign of animosity but rather a testament to their long-standing bond, forged through shared experiences and a deep understanding of each other's strengths and weaknesses.

Kinich's primary responsibility lies in Natlan's defense, a duty he shares with Ajaw. As a Scion of the Canopy, he possesses unique abilities and knowledge that allow him to coordinate with and amplify Ajaw's draconic power. This partnership is crucial for repelling threats to Natlan, whether they be external invaders or internal disturbances. The Scions of the Canopy are known for their ability to commune with and ride Dragonlords, a skill passed down through generations. Kinich, as a skilled practitioner of this art, is a vital asset to Natlan's military might. His understanding of Ajaw's elemental affinities and combat patterns allows for devastating coordinated attacks. The lore hints at a history where the Scions and Dragonlords have stood as Natlan's ultimate bulwark, and Kinich upholds this tradition with unwavering dedication. His personal motivations often revolve around protecting his tribe and ensuring the continued peace and security of Natlan, even if it means enduring Ajaw's incessant commentary. The bickering between them can be seen as a ritualistic exchange, a way to maintain focus and synchronize their efforts before engaging in battle. This unique relationship highlights the symbiotic nature of their existence, where each relies on the other for survival and success.

## Kit Mechanics

Kinich's combat kit revolves around his unique state, **Canopy Hunter**, and his powerful Elemental Burst, **Scalespiker Cannon**.

### Canopy Hunter: Riding High Nightsoul

Upon activating his Elemental Skill, Kinich enters the **Canopy Hunter** state, during which he gains increased movement speed and his Normal and Charged Attacks are infused with Pyro DMG. This state is powered by **Nightsoul**, a unique resource that accumulates over time and through specific combat actions.

**Nightsoul Mechanic:**
Nightsoul is a gauge that fills up to a maximum of 100 points. It regenerates passively at a rate of 5 Nightsoul per second. Additionally, landing Pyro-infused Normal and Charged Attacks while in the Canopy Hunter state generates 3 Nightsoul per hit. Successfully dodging enemy attacks also grants 10 Nightsoul. When Nightsoul reaches 100, Kinich can perform a special **Nightsoul Strike**, a powerful charged attack that consumes all accumulated Nightsoul to deal significant Pyro DMG. The damage of this Nightsoul Strike scales with the amount of Nightsoul consumed.

**Canopy Hunter State Details:**
- **Duration:** 12 seconds.
- **Cooldown:** 18 seconds.
- **Normal Attack Infusion:** Pyro DMG.
- **Charged Attack:** Consumes stamina as usual, but also deals Pyro DMG.
- **Movement Speed Bonus:** 15%.

The strategic use of Nightsoul is paramount to maximizing Kinich's damage output. Players must balance aggressive attacking to generate Nightsoul quickly with defensive maneuvers to avoid damage and gain bonus Nightsoul. The choice of when to unleash the Nightsoul Strike—whether at lower Nightsoul for a quick burst or after accumulating the maximum for a devastating blow—depends on the combat situation and desired damage profile.

### Scalespiker Cannon

Kinich's Elemental Burst, **Scalespiker Cannon**, is a devastating coordinated attack with his Dragonlord companion, Ajaw. Upon activation, Ajaw unleashes a torrent of Pyro energy, dealing massive AoE Pyro DMG.

**Scalespiker Cannon Details:**
- **Energy Cost:** 70.
- **Cooldown:** 18 seconds.
- **Initial Hit:** Ajaw fires a concentrated blast of Pyro energy, dealing AoE Pyro DMG.
- **Follow-up Attacks:** After the initial hit, Ajaw continues to unleash smaller Pyro projectiles for a duration of 3 seconds, dealing additional AoE Pyro DMG.
- **Synergy with Canopy Hunter:** While in the Canopy Hunter state, activating Scalespiker Cannon will consume all remaining duration of Canopy Hunter, but the Burst's damage will be significantly amplified. The amplification scales with the remaining duration of Canopy Hunter.

This Burst is a powerful tool for both single-target elimination and crowd control. Its synergy with the Canopy Hunter state encourages players to time their Burst activation strategically, ideally when Canopy Hunter is nearing its end to maximize the damage amplification.

**Peak Patrol Song** is Kinich's signature 5-star Polearm.
- **Base ATK:** 674 (at Lv. 90)
- **Secondary Stat:** CRIT Rate 22.1% (at Lv. 90)

This weapon is BiS (Best in Slot) for Kinich due to its high base ATK, CRIT Rate substat, and powerful ATK and Pyro DMG bonuses that directly synergize with his kit. The "Dragon's Roar" effect provides a significant damage boost, especially during his Canopy Hunter state.

## Nightsoul Mechanic Explained

The **Nightsoul** mechanic is central to Kinich's combat loop, dictating the flow of his damage output and rewarding skillful play. It is a resource generated and consumed by Kinich, primarily during his **Canopy Hunter** state.

**Generation:**
-   **Passive Regeneration:** Nightsoul passively regenerates at a steady rate of 5 points per second. This ensures that even with minimal offensive action, Kinich will gradually build up his resource.
-   **Normal/Charged Attacks:** Landing Pyro-infused Normal and Charged Attacks while in the Canopy Hunter state generates 3 Nightsoul per hit. This incentivizes aggressive, continuous attacking to fuel the resource pool.
-   **Dodging:** Successfully dodging an enemy attack grants a significant bonus of 10 Nightsoul. This rewards defensive play and awareness, providing a way to quickly top up Nightsoul if interrupted or needing to reposition.

**Consumption and Usage:**
-   **Nightsoul Strike:** The primary way Nightsoul is consumed is through the **Nightsoul Strike**. This is a special charged attack that can only be performed when Kinich has accumulated Nightsoul. Upon activation, it consumes all currently held Nightsoul.
-   **Damage Scaling:** The damage dealt by the Nightsoul Strike is directly proportional to the amount of Nightsoul consumed. A Nightsoul Strike performed with 100 Nightsoul will deal significantly more damage than one performed with only 30 Nightsoul. This creates a strategic decision: unleash a smaller burst of damage with less Nightsoul for immediate impact, or wait to accumulate the maximum for a devastating blow.
-   **Canopy Hunter State:** The Canopy Hunter state lasts for 12 seconds and has a cooldown of 18 seconds. During this state, Kinich's Normal and Charged Attacks are infused with Pyro, making them ideal for generating Nightsoul. The goal is often to maximize Nightsoul generation within this window to unleash a powerful Nightsoul Strike before the state ends.

**Strategic Implications:**
The Nightsoul mechanic encourages a dynamic playstyle. Players must balance aggressive attacking to generate Nightsoul quickly with the need to dodge enemy attacks to gain bonus Nightsoul and avoid taking damage. The decision of when to unleash the Nightsoul Strike is crucial:
-   **Low Nightsoul:** A quick Nightsoul Strike can be used to finish off a weakened enemy or interrupt a dangerous attack.
-   **High Nightsoul:** Accumulating maximum Nightsoul allows for a significant damage spike, ideal for breaking enemy shields, dealing with elite enemies, or contributing to burst damage phases.

Furthermore, the synergy with Kinich's Elemental Burst, **Scalespiker Cannon**, adds another layer of strategy. Activating the Burst while in Canopy Hunter consumes the remaining duration of the state but amplifies the Burst's damage based on that remaining duration. This means players might choose to end their Canopy Hunter state early to empower their Burst, or prioritize building and unleashing a Nightsoul Strike before activating their Burst. The interplay between generating Nightsoul, unleashing the Nightsoul Strike, and timing the Elemental Burst creates a deep and engaging combat experience for Kinich players.
