# Furina

## Lore

Furina's existence is intrinsically tied to the tragic prophecy of the Hydro Nation, Fontaine, and the desperate plan devised by the former Hydro Archon, Focalors. For 500 years, the people of Fontaine were cursed to be judged by the Oceanids, and the Hydro Archon was tasked with gathering enough "Primordial Water" to absolve them. Focalors, however, recognized the impossibility of this task and the inherent injustice of a divine punishment for a crime committed by a foreign entity. Instead, she orchestrated a centuries-long deception.

Focalors split her divine essence into two: herself, the Archon who would eventually sacrifice herself, and Furina, a mortal vessel who would embody the Archon's persona and perform a grand play for 500 years. Furina, unaware of her true nature as a mortal puppet, believed herself to be the Hydro Archon. She lived a life of constant performance, projecting an image of unwavering confidence and divine authority, all while secretly harboring immense self-doubt and fear. This elaborate charade was designed to accumulate the faith and belief of the Fontainians, which Focalors intended to use as the "Primordial Water" to break the curse.

During the Archon Quests, the Traveler confronts Furina, exposing the truth of her existence. The revelation shatters Furina's carefully constructed persona. The weight of 500 years of deception, the immense pressure of her perceived divine responsibilities, and the realization that she was merely a tool, not a true Archon, lead to a profound emotional breakdown. She confesses her fears, her loneliness, and her exhaustion to the Traveler, revealing the immense psychological toll her role had taken.

Following this catharsis, Focalors enacts the final stage of her plan. She confronts the Primordial One and sacrifices her divine self, using her own divine essence and the accumulated faith of the Fontainians to create the "Primordial Water" needed to lift the curse. This act also dissolves the divine authority of the Hydro Archon, rendering Furina, now fully aware of her mortal status, the sole remaining entity of the original Hydro Archon's lineage.

With the curse lifted and her role as a false god concluded, Furina is freed from her 500-year performance. She chooses to live as an ordinary human, grappling with the trauma of her past and the newfound freedom of mortality. Her journey is one of immense suffering, deception, and ultimate liberation, transforming her from a seemingly arrogant and theatrical Archon into a complex character who finds solace and purpose in the mundane aspects of life, cherishing human connections and experiences.

## Kit Mechanics

Furina's combat kit revolves around her unique ability to summon Salon Members and her "Fanfare" mechanic, which buffs the entire party's damage. Her Elemental Skill, **"Salon Solitaire,"** is central to her offensive capabilities.

### Elemental Skill: Salon Solitaire

Upon activation, Furina summons **Salon Members** from the three Ousia-aligned factions: the **Singer of Many Waters** (a crab), the **Gentilhomme Usher** (a seahorse), and the **Swordsaint** (a jellyfish). These companions will automatically attack nearby enemies. The composition of the Salon Members summoned depends on Furina's Arkhe alignment. When aligned with Ousia, she summons the **Singer of Many Waters**, the **Gentilhomme Usher**, and the **Swordsaint**. When aligned with Pneuma, she summons the **Singer of Many Waters**.

The primary function of the Salon Members is to deal Hydro DMG. For every second, the **Singer of Many Waters** will launch a **Hydro Missile** at an enemy, dealing Hydro DMG. The **Gentilhomme Usher** will continuously attack enemies in front of Furina, dealing Hydro DMG. The **Swordsaint** will perform coordinated attacks with Furina's Normal Attacks, dealing Hydro DMG.

Crucially, the Salon Members' attacks consume HP from the active character. For each Salon Member attacking, the active character's HP is drained by **0.5%** every second. This HP drain is a key component for generating **Fanfare** stacks.

The Arkhe alignment of Furina's Elemental Skill can be switched by holding the Elemental Skill button. When aligned with Ousia, she enters the **Salon Solitaire** state, summoning the Salon Members as described above. When aligned with Pneuma, she enters the **Singer of Many Waters** state, summoning only the Singer of Many Waters, which heals the active character for a small amount over time. This Pneuma state is generally not used for damage output but for utility.

### Elemental Burst: Staging - Overture of the Sea

Furina's Elemental Burst, **"Staging - Overture of the Sea,"** provides a significant party-wide damage bonus. Upon activation, Furina deals Hydro DMG to all nearby opponents and enters the **"Universal Revelry"** state.

During the Universal Revelry state, for each **1%** increase or decrease in the **current HP** of characters in the party, Furina gains one stack of **Fanfare**. This effect can be triggered every **0.1 seconds**. A maximum of **300** Fanfare stacks can be acquired. The Fanfare stacks are capped at **300**, and the maximum bonus DMG from Fanfare is **+30%**.

The Fanfare stacks grant a **DMG Bonus** to all party members. The DMG Bonus granted by Fanfare increases based on the number of Fanfare stacks Furina has accumulated. The bonus is **1%** for every **10** Fanfare stacks. For example, with 100 Fanfare stacks, party members gain a 10% DMG Bonus. With 300 Fanfare stacks, they gain the maximum 30% DMG Bonus.

The Universal Revelry state lasts for **18 seconds**. The Fanfare stacks are cleared when the Universal Revelry state ends.

The HP drain from Salon Members and the HP fluctuations from other party members' abilities are the primary ways to generate Fanfare stacks. This symbiotic relationship between HP management and damage amplification is the core of Furina's offensive utility.

### Passive Talents

*   **"She Knows the Way"**: When the active character's HP is above **50%**, Salon Members will deal **10%** increased DMG. This bonus is increased by **250%** of the amount by which the active character's HP exceeds **50%**. This means that when the active character is at full HP, Salon Members gain a significant damage boost.
*   **"The Stillness, the Calm"**: When Furina's Pneuma-aligned summon (Singer of Many Waters) heals a character, it generates **2** Fanfare points every **2.5 seconds**. This passive allows for some Fanfare generation even when Furina is not actively draining HP, albeit at a much slower rate.
*   **"All the World's a Stage"**: Furina's own Fanfare gain is increased by **25%**. This means she gains Fanfare stacks more efficiently from HP changes.

## Builds

Furina's builds prioritize Crit Rate, Crit DMG, and Energy Recharge, with her damage output scaling heavily with her Fanfare stacks.

### Signature Weapon: Splendor of Tranquil Waters

The **Splendor of Tranquil Waters** is Furina's BiS (Best-in-Slot) weapon. It provides a substantial Crit DMG substat bonus. Its passive grants an increase to the wielder's Max HP, which directly boosts the damage of her Salon Members. Additionally, it increases the DMG dealt by the wielder's Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst. When the wielder's HP changes, their Elemental DMG Bonus is increased for a duration, and this effect can stack. This weapon synergizes perfectly with Furina's kit by amplifying her personal damage and her party-wide buffs.

### Strong F2P Options: Favonius Codex

For F2P players, the **Favonius Codex** is an excellent choice. While it doesn't directly boost Furina's damage stats as much as her signature weapon, its Energy Recharge substat is invaluable for ensuring consistent Burst uptime. The passive ability of Favonius Codex generates Elemental Particles upon scoring a CRIT hit, which significantly alleviates Furina's high Energy Recharge requirements. This allows for more consistent Fanfare generation and Burst uptime, which is crucial for maintaining her party-wide DMG buff.

### Artifact Sets

*   **4-piece Golden Troupe**: This set is Furina's BiS artifact set. The 2-piece bonus grants a **20%** Elemental Skill DMG Bonus. The 4-piece bonus provides an **80%** Elemental Skill DMG Bonus after the wearer's Elemental Skill hits an opponent. This bonus is further increased by **25%** of the wearer's Elemental Skill DMG Bonus. This set significantly amplifies the damage dealt by Furina's Salon Members, making her a potent off-field damage dealer.
*   **4-piece Heart of Depth**: This set offers a **15%** Hydro DMG Bonus with its 2-piece bonus and increases Normal and Charged Attack DMG by **30%** for 15 seconds after using an Elemental Skill. While the 4-piece bonus is less synergistic with Furina's primary role as an off-field damage dealer and buffer, the Hydro DMG Bonus is still beneficial.
*   **2-piece combinations**: Players can also opt for 2-piece combinations that boost Hydro DMG, Elemental Skill DMG, or Crit Rate, such as **2-piece Golden Troupe** and **2-piece Heart of Depth**, or **2-piece Noblesse Oblige** for Burst DMG.

### Stat Priorities

*   **Sands**: Energy Recharge or HP% (depending on team needs and weapon ER)
*   **Goblet**: Hydro DMG Bonus
*   **Circlet**: Crit Rate or Crit DMG (aim for a 1:2 ratio)
*   **Substats**: Crit Rate, Crit DMG, Energy Recharge, HP%

**Energy Recharge Threshold**: Furina's Elemental Burst has a high cost of **80** Energy. To ensure consistent Burst uptime without relying heavily on Favonius weapons or an ER battery, Furina typically requires **180-200%** Energy Recharge. This can be lowered to **140-160%** if she is paired with another Hydro character (like Yelan or Xingqiu) or if the team utilizes Favonius weapons extensively.

## Team Compositions

Furina excels in teams that can effectively manage HP fluctuations and benefit from her party-wide DMG bonus. Her unique mechanics necessitate a dedicated healer to counteract the HP drain from her Salon Members and to maximize Fanfare stack generation.

### Core Synergies

*   **HP Fluctuation Mechanics**: Characters who can either drain HP (like Hu Tao, Xiao) or heal allies (like Jean, Kuki Shinobu, Kokomi) synergize well with Furina. These mechanics allow for rapid Fanfare stack generation.
*   **On-Field DPS**: Furina is an off-field support and sub-DPS. She requires an on-field character to trigger her Salon Members' attacks and to benefit from her Burst's DMG bonus.
*   **Dedicated Healer**: As explained below, a dedicated healer is almost mandatory to sustain the party through the HP drain and to maximize Fanfare generation.

## Why Furina Requires a Dedicated Healer

Furina's kit is designed around **HP management**, specifically the **drain** caused by her **Salon Members** and the **fluctuation** required to generate **Fanfare** stacks. Her Elemental Skill, **Salon Solitaire**, causes her Salon Members to drain the active character's HP by **0.5%** per second for each member present. At maximum summons (three members), this amounts to a **1.5%** HP drain per second.

This constant HP drain, while necessary for maximizing Fanfare generation, can quickly deplete a character's health, especially during prolonged combat or when facing enemies with high damage output. Without a dedicated healer, the active character would be at risk of being defeated, rendering the team ineffective.

Furthermore, the **Fanfare** mechanic itself rewards HP fluctuations. Furina gains **Fanfare** stacks when the party's HP increases or decreases. While the Salon Members provide the primary HP drain, other team members' abilities can also contribute to HP changes. However, the consistent, significant drain from Furina's own skill necessitates a counter-measure.

A dedicated healer serves multiple crucial roles in a Furina team:

1.  **Sustained Healing**: They provide consistent healing to counteract the HP drain from Furina's Salon Members, keeping the active character alive and able to continue dealing damage.
2.  **Fanfare Generation**: Many healers have abilities that restore HP to the entire party. This HP restoration directly contributes to Furina's Fanfare stack generation, as HP increases also grant stacks. This creates a feedback loop where healing enables more damage.
3.  **Team Survivability**: Beyond Furina's drain, enemies also deal damage. A dedicated healer ensures the entire team remains healthy, preventing potential knockouts and allowing for uninterrupted combat.
4.  **Buffing**: Some healers, like Bennett or Jean, also provide valuable buffs (ATK, DMG Bonus) that further enhance the team's overall damage output.

Characters like Bennett, Kuki Shinobu, Jean, Kokomi, and Baizhu are excellent choices for dedicated healers in Furina teams. Their ability to provide substantial healing, often with party-wide effects, is essential for Furina to reach her full potential as a powerful damage buffer and sub-DPS. Without such a dedicated healer, the HP drain from Furina's kit would be too detrimental to manage effectively, severely limiting her team-building flexibility and overall combat effectiveness.
