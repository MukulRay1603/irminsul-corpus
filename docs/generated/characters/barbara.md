# Barbara

## Lore and Background

Barbara is the beloved Deaconess of the Church of Favonius in Mondstadt, a prominent figure known for her unwavering kindness, dedication to healing, and her dual life as a popular idol singer. She is the younger sister of Jean, the Acting Grand Master of the Knights of Favonius, though their familial relationship is not widely known due to Jean's desire to maintain a professional demeanor at work. Barbara's upbringing was steeped in the teachings of the Church, fostering a deep sense of compassion and a desire to alleviate the suffering of others. Her innate Hydro Vision further solidified her path as a healer, allowing her to channel divine water into restorative energies.

Her introduction to the Traveler, Lumine or Aether, often occurs early in their journey through Mondstadt, highlighting her role as a beacon of hope and a source of comfort in a city still recovering from the attack of Stormterror. Barbara's gentle nature and genuine concern for the Traveler's well-being make her an immediate ally and a symbol of Mondstadt's resilience. She is particularly instrumental in helping the Traveler understand the importance of healing and support in combat, a stark contrast to the more offensive-oriented characters they might encounter.

Beyond her duties as a deaconess, Barbara harbors a secret passion: she is a burgeoning idol singer. This persona, while initially kept private, eventually becomes a significant part of her character. Her performances are characterized by infectious energy and heartfelt lyrics, often dedicated to the people of Mondstadt, whom she deeply cherishes. This dual identity reflects her multifaceted personality – the diligent healer and the radiant performer, both driven by a desire to bring joy and relief to others. Her idol activities are a source of personal fulfillment and a unique way for her to connect with the populace on an emotional level, often leading to her signature "Barbara, Come On!" chants that energize her allies.

Her relationship with Jean is one of deep affection and mutual respect, though complicated by Jean's stoic public image. Barbara admires Jean's strength and leadership, while Jean, in turn, worries about Barbara's sometimes overzealous dedication and her tendency to push herself too hard. This dynamic adds a layer of familial warmth to the often-military structure of the Knights and the Church. Barbara's idol activities are a point of concern for Jean, who fears it might distract from her deaconess duties or expose her to undue scrutiny, yet she ultimately supports her sister's happiness. Barbara's journey is one of self-discovery, balancing her sacred duties with her personal aspirations, all while embodying the spirit of hope and healing that defines Mondstadt.

## Kit Mechanics

Barbara's Elemental Skill, **Let the Show Begin**, summons a Melody Loop around her, applying **Wet** status to nearby enemies and healing her active character over time. This healing scales with Barbara's **Max HP**. The Melody Loop has a duration of 15 seconds and a cooldown of 20 seconds, resulting in a 5-second downtime. While active, the Melody Loop continuously applies **Wet** to enemies within its radius, making it a consistent source of Hydro application for triggering Hydro-based reactions. Crucially, the Melody Loop also heals the active character for a percentage of Barbara's Max HP at intervals. This self-healing mechanic is a core aspect of her kit, allowing her to sustain her team through sustained combat. However, a significant drawback of the Melody Loop is that it also applies **Wet** to the active character, making them vulnerable to Cryo-infused attacks and Electro-Charged damage.

Barbara's Elemental Burst, **Shining Miracle**, instantly heals all party members for a large amount, also scaling with her **Max HP**. This burst provides a substantial emergency heal, capable of recovering a significant portion of a team's health in an instant. It has an Energy Cost of 70 and a cooldown of 20 seconds. The burst's potent healing makes Barbara an invaluable asset for teams that struggle with survivability or face heavy incoming damage.

At Constellation Level 6, **I've Come Back For You**, Barbara gains the ability to revive one fallen character once every 15 minutes. This revive effect is triggered automatically when a party member falls, restoring 100% HP to the revived character. This unique revive mechanic elevates Barbara from a pure healer to an emergency "undo" button, capable of saving a run from failure in challenging content like the Spiral Abyss. This C6 ability is a significant power spike and makes her a highly sought-after support, especially for players who are still building their roster or facing difficult encounters.

The application of **Wet** status by her Elemental Skill is a key utility. It enables consistent Hydro application for reactions such as Vaporize, Freeze, Electro-Charged, and Bloom. While the application rate is not as rapid as characters like Xingqiu or Yelan, it is sufficient for many team compositions, especially when coupled with characters who can consistently trigger reactions. The self-application of **Wet** to the active character, while a drawback in certain scenarios (particularly against Cryo enemies), can be managed by switching characters or utilizing other defensive measures.

## Builds and Equipment

Barbara's build primarily focuses on maximizing her healing output and utility. Her most iconic and accessible weapon is the **Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers (TTDS)**, a 3-star catalyst. When swapping to another character after Barbara uses her Elemental Skill, TTDS grants the new active character a significant ATK boost (up to 48% at R5). This makes Barbara an exceptional **support** character, focusing on buffing her team's damage rather than her own. To maximize the uptime of TTDS's buff, players should aim for a build that allows Barbara to quickly use her skill and swap out.

For artifact sets, **4-piece Ocean-Hued Clam** is her best-in-slot option for a dedicated healing build. This set provides a healing bonus and, more importantly, generates a "Sea-Dyed Foam" bubble that explodes after 3 seconds, dealing Physical DMG based on the amount healed. This allows Barbara to contribute significant damage even while focusing on healing, turning her healing into offensive potential. The bubble can store up to 30,000 HP worth of healing, making it highly effective with Barbara's high HP scaling.

Alternatively, **4-piece Maiden Beloved** can be used to further enhance her healing output, though it lacks the offensive component of Ocean-Hued Clam. A **2-piece Tenacity of the Millelith** combined with **2-piece Heart of Depth** or **2-piece Maiden Beloved** can also be a viable option, focusing on HP% and Hydro DMG Bonus respectively, though this split is less optimal than a full set.

When building Barbara, the primary stats to prioritize are **HP%** on the Sands, Goblet, and Circlet. This directly increases her healing and the effectiveness of her Elemental Skill and Burst. For substats, **HP%**, **Energy Recharge (ER)**, and **CRIT Rate/DMG** (if focusing on Ocean-Hued Clam damage) are desirable. Energy Recharge is particularly important to ensure she can consistently use her Elemental Burst for emergency heals and to maintain team sustain. ER requirements can vary depending on the team composition and whether an ER weapon is used, but aiming for around 180-220% ER is generally recommended for comfortable Burst uptime.

For weapon options beyond TTDS, **Prototype Amber** is a strong 4-star craftable option that provides HP% and generates Energy Particles on Burst use, further aiding team energy needs. **Favonius Codex** can also be useful for its ER substat and the Energy generation from its passive, though it requires CRIT Rate to proc. For 5-star options, **Kagura's Verity** or **Lost Prayer to the Sacred Winds** can increase her personal damage and healing, but they are generally overkill and detract from her primary support role, making TTDS the superior choice for most players.

## Role and Team Compositions

Barbara's primary role in a team is that of a **dedicated healer** and **Hydro applicator**. Her consistent healing, especially with her Elemental Skill's Melody Loop, allows teams to sustain themselves through prolonged combat. Her Elemental Burst provides a crucial emergency heal, capable of bringing a depleted team back to full health. At C6, she becomes an invaluable asset for her revive capability, effectively preventing run failures in challenging content.

Her **Hydro application** is consistent enough to enable various Hydro-based reactions. She excels in teams that benefit from the **Wet** status.

*   **Freeze Teams:** Barbara's Hydro application can enable consistent Freeze reactions when paired with Cryo characters like Ganyu, Ayaka, or Kaeya. Her healing ensures the team's survivability while the enemies are crowd-controlled.
    *   Example Comp: Ayaka (Cryo DPS), Ganyu (Cryo Sub-DPS/Support), Kazuha (Anemo Support), Barbara (Healer/Hydro Applicator).
*   **Electro-Charged Teams:** Her Hydro can react with Electro characters like Fischl, Beidou, or Raiden Shogun to trigger Electro-Charged, dealing continuous damage.
    *   Example Comp: Raiden Shogun (Electro DPS/Support), Beidou (Electro Sub-DPS), Kazuha (Anemo Support), Barbara (Healer/Hydro Applicator).
*   **Vaporize Teams (Less common):** While her Hydro application isn't as rapid as Xingqiu or Yelan, she can still enable Vaporize for Pyro characters like Hu Tao or Diluc, especially if paired with an Anemo unit for grouping and Swirl. However, characters with faster Hydro application are generally preferred for optimal Vaporize damage.
*   **Bloom Teams (Nilou Bloom):** In Nilou-focused Bloom teams, Barbara can be a viable Hydro applicator. Her healing is beneficial, and the self-application of Wet status can be managed.
    *   Example Comp: Nilou (Dendro/Hydro), Nahida (Dendro), Kuki Shinobu (Electro/Healer), Barbara (Healer/Hydro Applicator).

Barbara's strength lies in her accessibility and potent healing. She is a **budget Hydro applicator** and an **emergency revive specialist** (at C6). This makes her particularly valuable for players who do not have access to 5-star Hydro characters like Kokomi, Yelan, or Xingqiu, or who need an extra layer of safety in difficult content.

### Barbara vs. Sangonomiya Kokomi

While both Barbara and Sangonomiya Kokomi are 4-star Hydro healers, they have distinct strengths and weaknesses that make them preferable in different situations.

*   **Healing:** Both characters offer substantial healing. Kokomi's healing is generally more consistent and less restrictive due to her Skill's AoE Hydro application and healing, which does not apply Wet to the active character. Barbara's healing is potent but her Skill's self-application of Wet can be a liability against Cryo enemies or in situations where the active character needs to avoid Hydro.
*   **Hydro Application:** Kokomi's Elemental Skill, **Jellyfish**, provides continuous AoE Hydro application and healing. Barbara's Skill, **Melody Loop**, also provides AoE Hydro and healing, but its application is tied to her active character's Normal and Charged Attacks. Kokomi's Hydro application is generally considered more consistent and less dependent on the active character's attacking pattern.
*   **Utility:** Kokomi offers significant Hydro application for reactions and her Burst allows her to deal Hydro DMG and heal while on-field. Barbara's primary utility beyond healing is her **TTDS buff** and her **C6 revive**. The TTDS buff is a massive team-wide ATK boost that Kokomi cannot replicate. Her C6 revive is a unique and powerful safety net that Kokomi lacks.
*   **Team Synergy:** Kokomi excels in Hydro-centric teams like Freeze, Electro-Charged, and Bloom, often acting as a Hydro enabler and healer. Barbara shines in teams that can heavily benefit from the TTDS buff, or where her C6 revive is crucial for survival. She can also enable Freeze and Electro-Charged, but her Hydro application is less consistent than Kokomi's.
*   **Builds:** Kokomi typically builds for Hydro DMG Bonus and Crit DMG (though she cannot crit) to maximize her on-field damage during her Burst, alongside HP and Healing Bonus. Barbara's build is more flexible; she can be built purely for HP and healing to maximize her support and TTDS buff, or with Ocean-Hued Clam to contribute damage.

**When to prefer Barbara over Kokomi:**

*   When you need the **Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers (TTDS) ATK buff**. This buff is one of the strongest in the game and can significantly boost your team's damage output, making Barbara a superior choice in many DPS-focused teams.
*   When you need an **emergency revive**. Barbara's C6 revive is a game-changer for difficult content and can save runs that would otherwise be lost.
*   When you are **early in the game** and have access to TTDS and Barbara, but not Kokomi. Barbara is a very accessible and effective healer and support.
*   In teams where the **self-application of Wet status is not a major concern**, or can be managed effectively.

**When to prefer Kokomi over Barbara:**

*   When you need **consistent, unhindered Hydro application** for reactions like Freeze or Electro-Charged without the risk of applying Wet to your active character.
*   When you need an **on-field Hydro damage dealer** who can also heal, especially in teams that benefit from her Hydro infusion during her Burst.
*   In teams where the **TTDS buff is not as critical** as consistent Hydro application or on-field presence.
*   When facing **Cryo-heavy enemies** where the self-application of Wet by Barbara can be detrimental.
