# Wriothesley

## Lore

Wriothesley, the Duke of the Meropide Fortress, is a figure of immense authority and complex personal history within Fontaine. He governs the underwater prison, a place where criminals and those deemed undesirable by the nation are confined. His ascension to this position was not through inheritance or political maneuvering, but through a brutal demonstration of strength, defeating his predecessor in a trial by combat. This method of succession, while unconventional, cemented his rule and instilled a sense of order within the fortress, albeit one maintained through a stern and often unforgiving hand.

His own past is not free from darkness. Wriothesley spent time within the very prison he now commands, a period that profoundly shaped his worldview and his approach to justice. This personal experience with confinement and the harsh realities of the criminal underworld grants him a unique perspective, fostering a sense of empathy and understanding for those under his charge, even as he upholds the law. This self-awareness and acceptance of his own history are crucial to his character, allowing him to rule with a pragmatic and often surprisingly compassionate, albeit stern, demeanor. He believes in rehabilitation and second chances, but only for those who genuinely seek to atone and contribute to society.

Wriothesley's relationship with Furina, the former Hydro Archon, is particularly noteworthy. While many in Fontaine treated her with deference due to her divine status, or with suspicion and pity due to her perceived eccentricities, Wriothesley was one of the few who saw her as a person. He interacted with her on a more equal footing, recognizing her burdens and the immense pressure she carried. This genuine connection, forged in a shared understanding of isolation and the weight of responsibility, highlights a softer, more perceptive side to the formidable Duke. He values honesty and directness, and Furina’s willingness to engage with him without pretense resonated deeply. His interactions with her, particularly during the events surrounding the prophecy, reveal a man capable of loyalty and a quiet, unwavering support for those he deems worthy. He is a pragmatic ruler who understands the nuances of human nature, a trait honed by his own experiences and his position overseeing the less fortunate elements of Fontainian society.

## Kit Mechanics

Wriothesley's combat effectiveness is intrinsically linked to his unique HP management system, revolving around his **Gracious Tribute** state and his **Chilling Penalty** mechanic.

### Gracious Tribute State

Upon using his Elemental Skill, **Gracious Tribute**, Wriothesley enters a state where his Normal Attacks are converted into **Chilling Penalty** attacks. This state has a duration of 5 seconds and a cooldown of 10 seconds. Crucially, while in this state, Wriothesley's interruption resistance is significantly increased, making him more resilient to enemy attacks and allowing him to maintain his offensive pressure. The skill itself deals Hydro DMG and applies a debuff to enemies hit, increasing the DMG they take from Wriothesley's Normal and Charged Attacks. This debuff lasts for 5 seconds.

### Chilling Penalty Attacks

During the **Gracious Tribute** state, Wriothesley's Normal Attacks become **Chilling Penalty** attacks. These attacks consume a portion of his HP, specifically 5% of his Max HP per hit, to deal increased DMG. This HP consumption is the core of his gameplay loop, demanding careful management to maximize damage output without compromising his survivability. The damage increase from these attacks scales with his ATK and is further enhanced by the debuff applied by his Elemental Skill.

### HP Recovery Threshold and Passive

Wriothesley possesses a unique passive talent that rewards him for managing his HP effectively. When his HP drops below 60% or rises above 60%, he gains a stack of **"Debt Paid"**. Each stack increases his CRIT Rate by 3%. He can have up to 4 stacks, providing a maximum CRIT Rate bonus of 12%. This passive encourages a dynamic playstyle where players actively manipulate Wriothesley's HP to maintain optimal CRIT Rate.

Furthermore, Wriothesley has another passive talent that grants him HP recovery. When his HP is below 30%, he will regenerate 3% of his Max HP every 1 second for 5 seconds. This regeneration effect can occur once every 10 seconds. This mechanic provides a built-in safety net, allowing him to recover from dangerously low HP levels and re-engage in his HP-consuming attacks. The interplay between his HP-consuming attacks, the CRIT Rate bonus from fluctuating HP, and the emergency HP regeneration forms the foundation of his combat strategy.

The ideal HP management strategy for Wriothesley involves strategically using his Elemental Skill to enter **Gracious Tribute**, unleashing **Chilling Penalty** attacks to deal significant damage while consuming HP, and then allowing his HP to drop below 60% to gain **"Debt Paid"** stacks. Once his HP falls too low, the passive HP regeneration can kick in, or he can be healed by external sources to bring his HP back above 60%, potentially resetting the **"Debt Paid"** stacks and allowing for another cycle of HP consumption and damage amplification. This rhythm of HP depletion and recovery is key to maximizing his damage potential and survivability.

## Builds

Wriothesley's build prioritizes CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, ATK%, and Hydro DMG Bonus. His unique HP fluctuation mechanic makes certain artifact sets exceptionally powerful.

### Signature Weapon

*   **Cashflow Supervision**: This 5-star Catalyst is Wriothesley's signature weapon and his Best-in-Slot (BiS). It provides a substantial CRIT DMG substat. Its passive grants an ATK% bonus and, when HP changes, increases Normal Attack DMG. This directly synergizes with Wriothesley's kit, amplifying his **Chilling Penalty** attacks and rewarding his HP-draining playstyle. The passive's condition of HP changing is easily met by Wriothesley's own kit.

### Strong 4-Star Options

*   **The Widsith**: A strong 4-star option, especially when its passive procs favorably. It offers CRIT DMG as a substat and provides ATK%, Elemental DMG Bonus, or a DMG buff on a timed interval. While RNG-dependent, its potential damage output is very high.
*   **Ballad of the Fjords**: This event-exclusive weapon provides CRIT Rate as a substat and grants Elemental Mastery based on the elemental types in the party. While EM is not Wriothesley's primary scaling stat, the CRIT Rate and potential for a DMG bonus make it a viable option, especially in teams that can benefit from EM for reactions if Wriothesley is not the sole Hydro applicator.

### Free-to-Play (F2P) Options

*   **Mappa Mare**: This craftable Catalyst provides Elemental Mastery as a substat and gains Elemental DMG Bonus when triggering elemental reactions. While not ideal for pure damage, it can be a decent placeholder if other options are unavailable, especially in reaction-heavy teams.
*   **Flowing Purity**: Another craftable option that provides ATK% as a substat. Its passive grants an Elemental DMG Bonus after using an Elemental Skill, but it also consumes HP. This can synergize with his HP-draining playstyle but requires careful HP management.

### Artifact Sets

*   **4-Piece Marechaussee Hunter**: This is Wriothesley's **BiS artifact set**. It provides a 15% Hydro DMG Bonus on the 2-piece effect. The 4-piece effect grants a 36% CRIT Rate bonus when HP increases or decreases by 15% or more, up to 3 stacks. This set perfectly complements Wriothesley's kit by providing a massive CRIT Rate boost that he can easily maintain due to his HP-consuming attacks and the passive healing. This allows players to focus more on CRIT DMG and ATK% on substats.
*   **4-Piece Heart of Depth**: This set provides a 15% Hydro DMG Bonus on the 2-piece effect and a 30% Normal Attack DMG Bonus after using an Elemental Skill for 15 seconds on the 4-piece effect. It's a strong alternative if Marechaussee Hunter pieces are scarce, offering a significant boost to his Normal Attacks.
*   **2-Piece Gladiator's Finale / Shimenawa's Reminiscence + 2-Piece Heart of Depth / Marechaussee Hunter**: A viable option for a balanced stat distribution, combining ATK% or Normal Attack DMG with Hydro DMG Bonus.

**Stat Priorities:**

*   **Sands**: ATK%
*   **Goblet**: Hydro DMG Bonus
*   **Circlet**: CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG (aim for a 1:2 ratio)
*   **Substats**: CRIT DMG > CRIT Rate > ATK% > Energy Recharge (ER needs are generally low for Wriothesley, around 120-130% depending on the team).

    *   **Shenhe**: Offers a substantial Physical DMG Bonus (if Wriothesley's damage is considered physical for buffing purposes, though his is Hydro) and Cryo DMG Bonus, but more importantly, her Skill provides a flat DMG bonus to Normal Attacks and her Burst offers a RES shred. Her A4 passive also grants a Cryo DMG Bonus to allies, which can be indirectly beneficial if she's built for damage herself. *   **Wriothesley / Bennett / Kazuha / Layla**:

## Comfortable Playstyle

Wriothesley's playstyle is characterized by an aggressive, rhythm-based approach centered around his **Gracious Tribute** state. The core loop involves:

1.  **Activating Elemental Skill**: Use **Gracious Tribute** to enter the state where his Normal Attacks become **Chilling Penalty** attacks. This also applies the Hydro DMG vulnerability debuff to enemies.
2.  **Unleashing Chilling Penalty Attacks**: Perform a string of Normal Attacks. These attacks will consume HP but deal significantly increased damage. Aim to perform as many as possible within the 5-second duration of **Gracious Tribute**.
3.  **Managing HP**: Pay attention to Wriothesley's HP. Ideally, you want his HP to fluctuate around the 60% mark to maximize the stacks of his **"Debt Paid"** passive for increased CRIT Rate. If his HP drops too low (below 30%), his passive healing will activate, which can be a safety net but might interrupt the optimal HP fluctuation for buffs. If his HP goes too high, he might lose CRIT Rate stacks.
4.  **Utilizing Burst**: Wriothesley's Elemental Burst deals a large instance of Hydro DMG. It's best used when his HP is relatively low to maximize its damage potential, or strategically to finish off enemies or trigger powerful reactions.
5.  **Rotation and Cooldowns**: After the **Gracious Tribute** state ends, switch to support characters to reapply buffs, generate energy, and prepare for the next rotation. Wriothesley's Elemental Skill has a 10-second cooldown, which aligns well with the duration of many support abilities and artifact set effects.

The "comfortable" aspect of Wriothesley's playstyle comes from the inherent resilience provided by his increased interruption resistance during **Gracious Tribute** and his passive HP regeneration. While managing HP is crucial for optimization, his kit provides enough leeway to avoid being overly fragile. Players can find a rhythm in his attack strings and HP management, making his combat feel fluid and engaging. Teams that provide shields or consistent healing can further enhance comfort by mitigating the risks of HP loss, allowing players to focus more on offensive execution.
