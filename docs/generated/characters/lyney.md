# Lyney

## Lore

Lyney is a prominent figure in Fontaine, renowned as a master magician and a key member of the enigmatic House of the Hearth. This organization, often perceived as a troupe of traveling performers, operates with a hidden agenda, deeply involved in the political and social machinations of the nation. Lyney, alongside his adopted siblings Lynette and Freminet, forms the core of this theatrical family, each contributing their unique talents to the House's performances and clandestine operations.

Lyney's persona is that of a charismatic showman, captivating audiences with his illusions and stage presence. However, beneath this dazzling exterior lies a sharp intellect and a strategic mind. He is fiercely protective of his siblings, particularly Lynette, his constant companion and assistant, and Freminet, the quiet diver whose skills are crucial to their more covert endeavors. Their bond is not merely familial but a testament to their shared upbringing within the House of the Hearth, where loyalty and mutual reliance are paramount.

The House of the Hearth, led by the formidable Arlecchino, is deeply intertwined with the prophecy of the Hydro Nation and the impending judgment. Lyney, as one of Arlecchino's most trusted agents, plays a significant role in gathering information and influencing events from within the public eye. His performances often serve as a cover for his true objectives, allowing him to observe and interact with Fontainian society, including the Court of the First Sinner and the various factions vying for power. His understanding of human nature, honed through years of performance, makes him adept at manipulation and persuasion.

Lyney's involvement in the events surrounding the prophecy and the subsequent crisis in Fontaine highlights his complex moral compass. While he operates within the framework of the House of the Hearth's directives, his actions are often driven by a desire to protect those he cares about and, ultimately, to ensure a favorable outcome for his adopted family. His journey in Fontaine reveals the intricate web of alliances and betrayals that define the nation's current state, positioning him as a pivotal character in its unfolding narrative.

## Kit Mechanics

Lyney's combat kit revolves around his unique **Prop Surplus** stack system, which empowers his Charged Attack. He gains stacks by consuming a special summon, the **Grin-Malkin Hat**, which is triggered by his Elemental Skill, **Bewildering Lights**.

### Prop Surplus and Grin-Malkin Hat

When Lyney uses his Elemental Skill, **Bewildering Lights**, he deals Pyro DMG to enemies in front of him and summons a **Grin-Malkin Hat**. This hat taunts enemies and has a certain amount of HP that scales with Lyney's Max HP. Upon expiration or destruction, the hat explodes, dealing AoE Pyro DMG. Crucially, casting **Bewildering Lights** consumes any existing **Prop Surplus** stacks Lyney possesses and grants him a number of stacks equal to the number consumed. Each stack increases the DMG dealt by the summoned **Grin-Malkin Hat**.

The core loop involves Lyney performing his **Charged Attack** after casting his Elemental Skill. A Level 2 Charged Attack, when cast after consuming **Prop Surplus** stacks, becomes a **"Charged Attack: Fanfare"**. This special Charged Attack consumes all current **Prop Surplus** stacks to fire a **Spirit-O-Shot** arrow. The **Spirit-O-Shot** deals Pyro DMG, and its damage is significantly amplified by the number of **Prop Surplus** stacks consumed. Each stack consumed increases the DMG of the **Spirit-O-Shot** by a considerable amount, up to a maximum of 5 stacks. This means Lyney's primary damage source is his **Charged Attack: Fanfare**, which is directly tied to his **Prop Surplus** stack management and the effectiveness of his Elemental Skill.

The interaction between the Elemental Skill and the Charged Attack is fundamental. Lyney needs to cast his Elemental Skill to generate **Prop Surplus** stacks (by consuming them and re-gaining them), which then fuel his powerful **Charged Attack: Fanfare**. The **Grin-Malkin Hat** summoned by the Elemental Skill also provides utility through its taunt, drawing enemy aggro and protecting Lyney, allowing him to charge his attack more safely. The hat's explosion also contributes to overall damage, especially when multiple stacks are consumed, as the explosion's DMG is also enhanced.

### Charged Attack Focus

Lyney's entire offensive strategy is built around maximizing the damage of his **Charged Attack: Fanfare**. This requires careful management of **Prop Surplus** stacks. Ideally, Lyney wants to reach 5 stacks before unleashing his **Charged Attack: Fanfare** for the highest possible damage output. This is achieved by consuming stacks and re-gaining them through his Elemental Skill. For instance, if Lyney has 3 stacks, casting his Elemental Skill consumes these 3 stacks and grants him 3 new stacks. This process is key to building up to the maximum 5 stacks.

The damage scaling of the **Spirit-O-Shot** is substantial. At Talent Level 10, a base **Spirit-O-Shot** deals 306% ATK DMG. Each **Prop Surplus** stack consumed adds an additional 120% ATK DMG. Therefore, with 5 stacks, the **Spirit-O-Shot** alone deals (306% + 5 * 120%) = 906% ATK DMG. This immense multiplier, combined with Lyney's high base ATK and potential ATK buffs, makes his **Charged Attack: Fanfare** his primary damage dealer.

Furthermore, Lyney's Passive Talent, **"T শাহরুখ's Trickery,"** enhances his DMG based on the number of different Elemental Types in his party. This passive is crucial for understanding his team-building requirements. It grants a DMG Bonus to Lyney's attacks based on the number of Elemental Types present: 1 type grants 60% DMG Bonus, 2 types grant 120% DMG Bonus, and 3 types grant 200% DMG Bonus. This bonus is capped at 3 Elemental Types. This means that having only Pyro characters in the party (1 Elemental Type) grants him a 60% DMG Bonus, while having Pyro, Anemo, and Bennett (Pyro) would grant him the full 200% DMG Bonus. This mechanic strongly incentivizes Mono-Pyro or near-Mono-Pyro team compositions to maximize his damage potential.

His Elemental Burst, **"One-Man Show,"** is a supplementary damage tool. Lyney transforms into a Grin-Malkin Cat, dealing continuous Pyro DMG to enemies. At the end of the duration, he reappears, dealing AoE Pyro DMG and summoning a **Grin-Malkin Hat** that taunts enemies and explodes. This Burst can help generate additional **Prop Surplus** stacks if used strategically before casting his Elemental Skill, as the hat summoned by the Burst also contributes to stack generation.

## Mono Pyro Team Requirement

Lyney's kit is intrinsically designed to excel in **Mono Pyro** or heavily Pyro-focused team compositions due to his passive talent, **"T শাহরুখ's Trickery."** This talent grants him a significant Elemental DMG Bonus based on the number of different Elemental Types in his party. The bonus is as follows:

*   **1 Elemental Type:** 60% Elemental DMG Bonus
*   **2 Elemental Types:** 120% Elemental DMG Bonus
*   **3 Elemental Types:** 200% Elemental DMG Bonus

This bonus caps at 3 Elemental Types. This means that a team composed solely of Pyro characters (1 Elemental Type) would grant Lyney a 60% Pyro DMG Bonus. However, the most optimal scenario involves including characters of different elements that can synergize with Pyro and provide support without significantly diluting Lyney's damage focus.

The critical takeaway is that Lyney's damage output is heavily reliant on this passive. When he is placed in teams with multiple different elements (e.g., Pyro, Hydro, Electro, Anemo), the DMG Bonus from **"T শাহরুখ's Trickery"** is significantly reduced. For instance, a team with Lyney (Pyro), Yelan (Hydro), Fischl (Electro), and Kazuha (Anemo) would only grant him a 200% Pyro DMG Bonus. However, the intention behind his kit is to maximize this bonus.

The ideal scenario for Lyney is to have him as the sole Pyro character, with two other characters of different elements, and a fourth character who is also Pyro. This configuration allows him to reach the maximum 200% Elemental DMG Bonus while still benefiting from powerful support. For example, a team of Lyney (Pyro), Kazuha (Anemo), Bennett (Pyro), and Xiangling (Pyro) would have three Pyro characters and one Anemo character, resulting in two distinct elemental types (Pyro and Anemo). This composition would grant Lyney the maximum 200% Elemental DMG Bonus.

The reason for this strict requirement is that Lyney's personal damage multipliers are already high, but the **"T শাহরুখ's Trickery"** passive acts as a massive multiplier on top of his already potent **Charged Attack: Fanfare**. Without this bonus, his damage ceiling drops considerably, making him less competitive compared to other Pyro DPS units. Therefore, team compositions that deviate from a Mono Pyro or near-Mono Pyro structure will result in a noticeable decrease in Lyney's overall damage output. This makes him a niche but exceptionally powerful DPS within his specific team archetypes.

## Builds

Lyney's builds focus on maximizing his ATK, CRIT Rate, and CRIT DMG, with a secondary emphasis on Pyro DMG Bonus. His reliance on his Charged Attack means that artifacts and weapons that enhance this aspect of his kit are highly valuable.

### Signature Weapon: Bridal Remorse

**Bridal Remorse** is Lyney's signature 5-star bow and his Best-in-Slot (BiS) weapon. It provides a significant base ATK and a substat of CRIT Rate, which is extremely beneficial for Lyney. Its passive ability, **"A Father's Present,"** grants an Elemental DMG Bonus upon hitting an opponent. This bonus increases further when Lyney's attacks hit opponents affected by Pyro. Additionally, it grants stacks of **"Prop Surplus"** when Lyney's Charged Attacks hit opponents, up to 5 stacks. Each stack increases his ATK by a percentage. This weapon synergizes perfectly with Lyney's kit, directly boosting his Charged Attack damage and providing substantial ATK buffs.

### Alternative 5-Star Weapon: The First Great Magic

**The First Great Magic** is another strong 5-star bow option for Lyney. It offers a high base ATK and a CRIT DMG substat. Its passive grants an Elemental DMG Bonus based on the number of other party members with the same Elemental Type as the wielder. Furthermore, it grants ATK% based on the number of party members with different Elemental Types. While this weapon can provide significant buffs, its passive is more flexible and can be beneficial in teams that are not strictly Mono Pyro. However, for optimal Lyney performance, **Bridal Remorse** remains superior due to its direct synergy with his **Prop Surplus** stacks and Charged Attack focus.

### Strong 4-Star Options

*   **Mouun's Moon** (4-star): This bow provides a high ATK% substat and its passive increases Elemental Burst DMG based on the total Energy Recharge of the party. While Lyney's Burst is not his primary damage source, the substantial ATK boost can still be beneficial.
*   **Thundering Pulse** (5-star, if available): While a 5-star, it can be obtained through standard banner or limited banner wishes. It offers a high ATK% substat and a significant DMG Bonus to Normal Attacks, which indirectly benefits Lyney's Charged Attack.
*   **Prototype Crescent** (4-star, Craftable): This bow offers an ATK% substat and its passive grants bonus ATK when hitting weak points. If Lyney can consistently hit weak points, this can be a strong F2P option.

### Artifact Sets

*   **4-piece Marechaussee Hunter:** This is Lyney's BiS artifact set. It provides a 15% Pyro DMG Bonus for 2-piece bonus. The 4-piece bonus grants a 10% CRIT Rate Bonus when HP increases or decreases, stacking up to 4 times for a maximum of 40% CRIT Rate. This set is exceptionally powerful on Lyney because his kit, particularly with the **Marechaussee Hunter** set, naturally causes his HP to fluctuate through the use of his Charged Attack and the effects of characters like Furina (if used, though not ideal for Lyney). The massive CRIT Rate boost allows players to focus more on CRIT DMG and ATK.
*   **4-piece Crimson Witch of Flames:** A strong generalist Pyro artifact set that increases Pyro DMG and enhances Overload, Burning, and Vaporize reactions. While Lyney doesn't heavily rely on these reactions, the Pyro DMG Bonus and the set's ability to boost his Charged Attack damage make it a viable alternative if **Marechaussee Hunter** is unavailable.
*   **2-piece Crimson Witch of Flames / 2-piece Gladiator's Finale (or Shimenawa's Reminiscence):** A decent combination that provides a 15% Pyro DMG Bonus and an 18% ATK Bonus, respectively. This can be a good placeholder set while farming for the optimal **Marechaussee Hunter** set.

## Optimal Team Compositions

Lyney's kit strongly incentivizes **Mono Pyro** or near-Mono Pyro team compositions to maximize the benefits from his passive talent, **"T শাহরুখ's Trickery."** This talent grants him a significant Elemental DMG Bonus based on the number of different Elemental Types in his party, capping at 200% with 3 different Elemental Types. However, the most effective teams often feature multiple Pyro characters to benefit from Pyro resonance and specific support capabilities.

### Mono Pyro Core

The most optimal team structure for Lyney typically includes:

1.  **Lyney (Pyro DPS):** The primary damage dealer, focusing on his Charged Attack.
2.  **Bennett (Pyro Support):** Provides a massive ATK buff through his Elemental Burst, heals, and enables Pyro Resonance with Lyney, granting an additional 25% ATK.
3.  **Xiangling (Pyro Sub-DPS/Support):** Her Pyronado deals significant off-field Pyro DMG and can contribute to Lyney's stack generation if timed correctly. She also benefits from Bennett's ATK buff.
4.  **Anemo Support (Kazuha/Sucrose/Venti):** This slot is crucial for grouping enemies, providing crowd control, and shredding enemy Elemental RES with the Viridescent Venerer artifact set.
    *   **Kazuha:** His Elemental Skill can group enemies and his Elemental Burst can swirl Pyro, dealing additional damage and providing a Pyro DMG Bonus to the team. He is generally considered the best option due to his powerful buffs and crowd control.
    *   **Sucrose:** Offers similar grouping and Elemental Mastery buffs, which can indirectly benefit Pyro reactions if present, though Lyney's kit doesn't heavily rely on EM.
    *   **Venti:** Excellent for grouping lighter enemies, but his Burst might not always swirl Pyro effectively depending on enemy density.

**Example Team Composition:** Lyney, Bennett, Xiangling, Kazuha

This team composition allows Lyney to reach the maximum 200% Elemental DMG Bonus from his passive talent (Pyro, Pyro, Pyro, Anemo = 2 distinct elements). Bennett provides a substantial ATK buff, Xiangling adds significant off-field damage, and Kazuha groups enemies, buffs Pyro DMG, and shreds resistance. This setup maximizes Lyney's personal damage output and overall team damage.

### Variations and Considerations

*   **Dehya (Pyro):** Can be used as a defensive option, providing interruption resistance and some off-field Pyro application. However, her damage contribution is lower than Xiangling's, and she doesn't offer the same level of utility as an Anemo support.
*   **Zhongli (Geo):** While not ideal for maximizing Lyney's passive, Zhongli can be used for his unparalleled shielding and RES shred. If survivability is a major concern, he can be a substitute for the Anemo slot, but this will result in a lower damage bonus from Lyney's passive.
*   **Fischl/Yelan/Xingqiu:** These characters are generally not recommended for Lyney's core teams as they introduce too many different Elemental Types, significantly reducing the DMG Bonus from **"T শাহরুখ's Trickery."** While they can provide strong off-field damage or utility, Lyney's personal damage ceiling is so high in Mono Pyro that these compositions are usually less effective overall.

The key to Lyney's optimal team composition is to maintain a high concentration of Pyro characters while incorporating an Anemo unit for buffs and crowd control. This strategy leverages his unique passive talent to its fullest potential, resulting in exceptionally high single-target and AoE damage output within his specialized team archetypes.
