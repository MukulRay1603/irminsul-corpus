# Sucrose

## Lore and Background

Sucrose is an alchemist affiliated with the Knights of Favonius in Mondstadt, serving as the diligent assistant to the Chief Alchemist, Albedo. Her primary focus is bio-alchemy, a field she pursues with an almost obsessive passion, constantly seeking to improve existing flora and fauna through alchemical means. This dedication stems from a deep-seated curiosity about the natural world and a desire to understand its fundamental principles. Her experiments, while often well-intentioned, can sometimes lead to unpredictable and even chaotic results, reflecting her own anxious and easily flustered personality.

Despite her often-overwhelmed demeanor, Sucrose possesses a sharp intellect and a meticulous approach to her work. She meticulously records every detail of her experiments, often filling numerous notebooks with observations and hypotheses. Her anxiety, however, frequently manifests in her interactions with others, causing her to stutter, fidget, and apologize profusely, even when no offense has been taken. This nervousness is a stark contrast to her unwavering determination when engrossed in her research.

Her relationship with Albedo is one of deep respect and admiration. She views him as a brilliant mentor and a guiding light in her alchemical pursuits, often striving to meet his high standards. Albedo, in turn, recognizes Sucrose's potential and dedication, often offering quiet encouragement and guidance. He trusts her implicitly with his research and considers her an indispensable member of his laboratory.

An unexpected and heartwarming friendship has blossomed between Sucrose and Fischl, the Adventurers' Guild investigator who speaks in a dramatic, theatrical persona. While many in Mondstadt are amused or bewildered by Fischl's elaborate pronouncements, Sucrose takes her at her word, engaging with her as if she were a genuine princess from another realm. This genuine acceptance, free from judgment or skepticism, has forged a strong bond between the two. Sucrose finds Fischl's unique perspective refreshing and often uses her fantastical stories as inspiration for her bio-alchemical experiments, much to Fischl's delight. This unlikely friendship highlights Sucrose's open-mindedness and her ability to connect with others on a genuine level, despite her social anxieties.

## Combat Kit Mechanics

Sucrose is a 4-star Anemo Catalyst user whose primary role in combat revolves around crowd control, Elemental Mastery (EM) buffing, and Swirl reaction amplification. Her kit is designed to group enemies, spread elemental effects, and significantly boost the damage of teammates who rely on elemental reactions.

### Elemental Skill: Forbidden Creation Isomer

Sucrose's Elemental Skill, **Forbidden Creation Isomer**, unleashes a **Wind Spirit** that pulls enemies and objects towards its location, dealing Anemo DMG. This skill has a relatively short cooldown of **6 seconds** and can be cast quickly, making it excellent for repositioning enemies and setting up for larger AoE attacks or Swirl reactions. The pull strength is moderate, capable of gathering smaller enemies and even some medium-sized ones, though larger enemies like Ruin Guards are unaffected. The skill's damage scales with Sucrose's ATK and can be further enhanced by her Elemental Mastery through her passive talents.

### Elemental Burst: Chaos Theory

Sucrose's Elemental Burst, **Chaos Theory**, creates a large **Wind Domain** that continuously pulls enemies and objects towards its center, dealing Anemo DMG over time. The initial cast of the Burst also triggers a Swirl reaction with any element present on the field. The Wind Domain lasts for **8 seconds** with a cooldown of **20 seconds**. The pull strength is more significant than her Skill, making it highly effective for grouping large numbers of enemies. Crucially, the Burst has a unique property: if it comes into contact with Hydro, Pyro, Cryo, or Electro, it will continuously infuse itself with that element and deal additional elemental DMG of that type. This allows Sucrose to consistently trigger Swirl reactions with a specific element, amplifying its effects and damage.

### Passive Talents

Sucrose possesses two key passive talents that define her supportive capabilities:

*   **Astable Conduction:** When Sucrose triggers a Swirl reaction, all party members gain **20%** of Sucrose's Elemental Mastery for **8 seconds**. This is a significant buff that directly increases the damage of elemental reactions for the entire team. The EM shared is capped at **200 EM** (meaning Sucrose needs at least 1000 EM for the maximum 200 EM share).
*   **Grand Experimentation:** When Forbidden Creation Isomer or Chaos Theory hits an opponent, Sucrose will grant the active character(s) within the Wind Domain **50%** of her Elemental Mastery as an additional buff. This buff is separate from the Swirl-triggered EM share and stacks with it, providing a substantial EM boost to the on-field character. This means a character can benefit from both the 20% EM share from Swirl and the additional 50% EM from the Burst/Skill.

These passive talents make Sucrose an exceptional enabler for reaction-based teams, significantly boosting the damage output of characters who rely on Elemental Mastery for their damage.

## Builds and Equipment

Sucrose's build primarily focuses on maximizing her Elemental Mastery and providing her with enough Energy Recharge to consistently use her Elemental Burst.

### Weapons

*   **Best-in-Slot (BiS):** **Sacrificial Fragments** is her signature weapon and often considered her BiS. Its passive grants a chance to reset the cooldown of her Elemental Skill upon hitting an opponent with it. This allows Sucrose to cast her Elemental Skill twice in quick succession, leading to more enemy grouping, more Swirl triggers, and thus more frequent Elemental Mastery sharing for the team. The weapon also provides a significant chunk of Elemental Mastery as its secondary stat.
*   **Strong Options:**
    *   **Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers (TTDS):** This 3-star Catalyst is an excellent budget option. While its passive doesn't directly benefit Sucrose's personal damage or utility, it provides a massive ATK buff (up to 48% at R5) to the next character on the field after Sucrose switches out. This buff is incredibly powerful for reaction-based teams where ATK is a significant damage multiplier. Sucrose can use her Skill and Burst, then switch out to grant the ATK buff.
    *   **Magic Guide:** A 3-star Catalyst that provides Elemental Mastery as its secondary stat. Its passive increases DMG against enemies affected by Hydro or Electro, which can be situationally useful in Taser teams.
    *   **Favonius Codex:** A 4-star Catalyst that provides Energy Recharge. While not ideal for EM, the ER can help Sucrose Burst more consistently, which is crucial for her team buffing. The passive also generates Elemental Particles, further aiding team energy needs.
*   **Other Considerations:** Any Catalyst that provides Elemental Mastery or Energy Recharge can be a viable option depending on team needs and available weapons.

### Artifacts

*   **Best-in-Slot (BiS):** **4-piece Viridescent Venerer (VV)** is almost universally her best artifact set.
    *   **2-piece bonus:** +15% Anemo DMG Bonus.
    *   **4-piece bonus:** Increases Swirl DMG by 60%. Decreases opponent's Elemental RES to the element infused in Swirl by 40% for 10 seconds. This set is crucial for any Anemo character focused on enabling reactions, as the RES shred significantly boosts the damage of the elements being swirled.
*   **Main Stats:**
    *   **Sands:** Elemental Mastery or Energy Recharge (ER). If Sucrose has Sacrificial Fragments and sufficient ER from substats, EM is preferred. If ER is an issue, ER Sands is necessary.
    *   **Goblet:** Elemental Mastery. Anemo DMG Bonus can be considered if EM is extremely high and personal damage is prioritized, but EM is generally better for her supportive role.
    *   **Circlet:** Elemental Mastery. CRIT Rate/DMG is only viable if she is built for personal damage, which is rare.
*   **Substats:** Prioritize Elemental Mastery, Energy Recharge, CRIT Rate/DMG (for personal damage, less important for support), and ATK%. Aim for enough ER to comfortably Burst every rotation (typically around 180-220% ER depending on team and Sacrificial Fragments).

## Role and Team Compositions

Sucrose excels as a budget Anemo crowd control and Elemental Mastery buffer, often filling a similar role to Kaedehara Kazuha, albeit with less personal damage and a different buffing mechanism.

### Budget Kazuha Replacement

While Kazuha offers a more potent ATK% buff and higher personal damage, Sucrose provides a comparable EM buff and excellent crowd control. Her 20% EM share on Swirl trigger and additional 50% EM from her Burst/Skill can significantly boost the damage of reaction-based teams, especially those that scale heavily with EM, like Electro-Charged or Vaporize/Melt compositions. Her crowd control is also vital for grouping enemies, allowing elemental effects to spread more efficiently.

### Team Compositions

Sucrose fits well into many reaction-focused teams.

*   **National Team (Budget Version):**
    *   **Core:** Xiangling, Bennett, Xingqiu.
    *   **Flex Slot:** Sucrose.
    *   **Explanation:** Sucrose can group enemies for Xiangling's Pyronado and Xingqiu's Rain Swords, while her EM buff enhances the damage of Vaporize reactions triggered by Xiangling and Xingqiu. This version sacrifices some of Kazuha's ATK buff for Sucrose's EM buff and crowd control.
*   **Taser Teams (Electro-Charged):**
    *   **Core:** Electro character (e.g., Fischl, Beidou, Raiden Shogun), Hydro character (e.g., Xingqiu, Yelan, Kokomi), Sucrose.
    *   **Explanation:** Sucrose is a premier choice for Taser teams. She groups enemies, spreads Electro and Hydro through Swirl, and her EM buff significantly boosts the damage of Electro-Charged reactions. The consistent application of Electro and Hydro from characters like Fischl and Xingqiu allows Sucrose to continuously trigger Swirls, maximizing her EM sharing. Teams often include Fischl for off-field Electro damage and energy generation, and Xingqiu for Hydro application and damage reduction.
*   **Freeze Teams:**
    *   **Core:** Cryo DPS (e.g., Ayaka, Ganyu), Hydro applicator (e.g., Mona, Kokomi), Cryo support (e.g., Diona, Shenhe), Sucrose.
    *   **Explanation:** Sucrose can group enemies and apply Cryo via Swirl if infused with Cryo from the team. While not as optimal as Kazuha for Freeze due to his DMG bonus and RES shred on specific elements, Sucrose's crowd control and EM share can still be beneficial for teams that incorporate reactions alongside Freeze.

## How Sucrose's EM Share Mechanic Works

Sucrose's Elemental Mastery sharing is a cornerstone of her supportive utility and operates through two distinct mechanics:

1.  **Astable Conduction (Swirl Triggered EM Share):** Whenever Sucrose triggers a Swirl reaction (Anemo DMG hitting an enemy affected by Hydro, Pyro, Cryo, or Electro), all party members gain **20%** of Sucrose's current Elemental Mastery for **8 seconds**. This buff is applied to the entire team, regardless of who is on the field. The key here is that Sucrose must *trigger* the Swirl. If another character triggers the Swirl, Sucrose's EM is not shared. This encourages players to ensure Sucrose is the one applying Anemo to initiate the Swirl. The maximum EM that can be shared through this passive is **200 EM**, meaning Sucrose needs at least **1000 EM** to reach this cap. Any EM she has beyond 1000 will not be shared via this specific passive.

2.  **Grand Experimentation (Burst/Skill Infused EM Share):** When Sucrose's Elemental Skill (Forbidden Creation Isomer) or Elemental Burst (Chaos Theory) hits an opponent, the *active character(s)* within the effect's area gain an additional buff equal to **50%** of Sucrose's Elemental Mastery. This buff is applied *only* to the character currently on the field within the Anemo effect's radius. This means if Sucrose casts her Burst and an Electro character is on the field within the Burst's area, that Electro character gains 50% of Sucrose's EM. This buff stacks additively with the EM shared from Astable Conduction. For example, if Sucrose has 800 EM, and she triggers a Swirl while her Burst is active with an on-field character, that character would receive:
    *   From Astable Conduction: 20% of 800 EM = 160 EM
    *   From Grand Experimentation: 50% of 800 EM = 400 EM
    *   Total EM for the on-field character: 160 + 400 = 560 EM.

This dual buffing mechanism makes Sucrose exceptionally valuable in teams where Elemental Mastery is a primary damage scaling stat. Building Sucrose with high Elemental Mastery is therefore paramount, as it directly translates into increased damage for her entire team through enhanced reaction damage. Energy Recharge remains a crucial secondary stat to ensure consistent Burst uptime, as the Grand Experimentation buff is tied to her Burst and Skill.
