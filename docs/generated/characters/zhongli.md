# Zhongli

## Lore

Zhongli is the mortal guise of Rex Lapis, the Geo Archon and former ruler of Liyue for over six millennia. His reign was characterized by a deep understanding of contracts, a principle he instilled in Liyue's society and his own governance. He is the oldest of the Seven Archons, having participated in the Archon War millennia ago and ultimately establishing Liyue Harbor. His divine authority was absolute, and he was revered by the people of Liyue and the Adepti alike. The Adepti, powerful beings of elemental energy and ancient spirits, served as his loyal protectors and enforcers, maintaining order and defending Liyue from external threats. Notable among them are the "Four Winds" (though not all are strictly Adepti, they represent Liyue's elemental guardians) and the Yakshas, a group of warrior spirits who fought against demonic corruption under his command, albeit at a great personal cost.

Driven by a desire to see if Liyue could stand on its own without his direct intervention, Rex Lapis orchestrated his own demise during the Rite of Descension. This elaborate deception was designed to test the resilience and self-sufficiency of his people and their leaders. Following this "death," he relinquished his Gnosis, the divine symbol of his Archon status, to the Tsaritsa of Snezhnaya, the Cryo Archon, as part of a complex, undisclosed agreement. Shedding his divine responsibilities, he adopted the persona of Zhongli, a consultant at the Wangsheng Funeral Parlor, an establishment dedicated to ushering souls into the afterlife. In this mortal form, he experiences the world from a new perspective, often grappling with the mundane aspects of human life, particularly the concept of "mora" (Liyue's currency) and its value, which he, as the creator of mora, often struggles to comprehend or manage.

His interactions at the Wangsheng Funeral Parlor provide a unique lens through which to view his character. He works alongside Hu Tao, the eccentric and enigmatic 77th Director of the parlor, whose playful yet profound understanding of life and death often contrasts with Zhongli's stoic and ancient wisdom. Despite his retirement from active Archon duties, Zhongli maintains a quiet watch over Liyue, his presence a comforting, albeit often unnoticed, assurance of its continued safety. His relationships with the Adepti remain cordial, though tinged with the melancholy of his departure from their shared past. They respect his decision but also feel the absence of his direct guidance. Zhongli's journey is one of self-discovery, a divine being seeking to understand humanity by living amongst them, shedding the weight of eons to embrace the present.

## Kit Mechanics

Zhongli's kit revolves around his unparalleled defensive capabilities and potent crowd control, primarily through his Geo constructs and Elemental Burst. His **Elemental Skill, Dominus Lapidis**, allows him to summon a Stone Stele, a Geo construct that pulses with Geo energy. This pulse deals AoE Geo DMG and can resonate with other nearby Geo constructs, including other Steles, Geo Traveler's Earthwave, Ningguang's Jade Screen, and Klee's Jumpty Dumpty, dealing additional AoE Geo DMG. The resonance damage is significant and scales with Zhongli's DEF and ATK, though its primary utility is not damage but its synergy with other Geo constructs. Crucially, the Stone Stele also generates Geo energy particles and, more importantly, applies a **20% Universal RES Shred** to all enemies within its AoE. This RES shred affects all damage types, making it an incredibly valuable buff for any team composition, regardless of elemental focus. Holding the Elemental Skill button allows Zhongli to aim the placement of the Stele, and if there are enemies nearby, it will automatically target them. A unique interaction occurs when the Stele is struck by Geo DMG (e.g., from another Stele's resonance or an enemy's Geo attack); it will emit a shockwave that deals AoE Geo DMG and knocks back smaller enemies.

His **Elemental Burst, Planet Befall**, is a devastating ability that calls down a colossal meteor to strike enemies in a large AoE. It deals massive Geo DMG and, most importantly, **Petrifies** all enemies hit for a duration of 4 seconds. This petrification effect is a powerful form of crowd control, completely immobilizing enemies and preventing them from attacking or moving, allowing for safe damage windows or repositioning. The Petrify status is considered a unique debuff and can be extended by subsequent Petrify applications if the duration has not fully expired. The cooldown for Planet Befall is 12 seconds, and it costs 40 Energy, making it relatively spammable and easy to recharge, especially with some Energy Recharge investment.

The cornerstone of Zhongli's defensive utility lies in his **Geo Pillar shield**, generated by holding his Elemental Skill. This shield, the **Jade Shield**, scales primarily with Zhongli's **Max HP**. The shield's strength is substantial, providing a robust layer of protection that can absorb a significant amount of damage. Furthermore, the Jade Shield absorbs **150% of Physical DMG**, making it exceptionally effective against physical attackers. When a Jade Shield is active, Zhongli's Normal Attacks, Charged Attacks, and Plunging Attacks will deal increased Geo DMG, though this is a secondary benefit for most players focusing on his support role. The shield also grants Zhongli resistance to interruption, meaning he will not be staggered or knocked back while the shield is active, allowing him to attack or cast abilities uninterrupted. This interruption resistance is a significant quality-of-life improvement and contributes greatly to his overall defensive utility. The shield's duration is 20 seconds, and its cooldown is 10 seconds, meaning it can be maintained almost perpetually with proper rotation.

## Builds

Zhongli's build path heavily depends on whether the player prioritizes his shielding capabilities, his damage potential, or a hybrid approach.

### Best-in-Slot (BiS) for Shieldbot Zhongli

For players who want to maximize Zhongli's shielding capabilities and the universal RES shred, the **Black Tassel** is surprisingly the best-in-slot 3-star weapon. Its passive grants a 36% DMG Bonus against slimes, which is irrelevant for shielding. However, its main stat provides a massive **46.9% Max HP** at level 90. This HP bonus directly translates to a stronger Jade Shield, as the shield's strength scales with Zhongli's Max HP. The goal for a pure shieldbot is to reach extremely high HP values, often exceeding 50,000 HP, to make the shield virtually unbreakable.

**Artifact Set:**
*   **4-piece Tenacity of the Millelith:** This set is the premier choice for Zhongli. The 2-piece bonus grants 20% HP, further boosting his shield strength. The 4-piece bonus triggers when the Stone Stele or a Geo construct hits an enemy, granting the entire party a 20% ATK buff and 1% Shield Strength buff for 3 seconds, stacking up to 3 times. This provides a valuable ATK buff to the team and a small but appreciated boost to his own shield. The trigger condition is easily met by the Stone Stele's pulses or resonance.
*   **2-piece Tenacity of the Millelith + 2-piece Archaic Petra:** This combination provides a 20% HP bonus and a 15% Geo DMG Bonus. While the Geo DMG bonus is less impactful for a pure shieldbot, the combined HP boost is still very strong.
*   **2-piece Tenacity of the Millelith + 2-piece Vourukasha's Glow:** This combination offers a 20% HP bonus from Tenacity and another 30% HP bonus from Vourukasha's Glow, for a total of 50% HP. This is the absolute highest HP stacking option for a shieldbot.

**Main Stats:**
*   **Sands:** HP%
*   **Goblet:** HP%
*   **Circlet:** HP%

**Substats Priority:** HP% > Flat HP > Energy Recharge (to ensure Burst uptime if desired) > DEF% (minor contribution)

### Hybrid/DPS Zhongli

For players who want to incorporate some damage alongside his support capabilities, the **Staff of Homa** is his undisputed best-in-slot 5-star weapon. It provides a substantial Crit DMG substat and a passive that grants HP% and ATK based on a percentage of the wielder's Max HP, with an additional ATK bonus when HP is below 50%. This weapon significantly boosts both his HP (for shielding) and his ATK (for damage).

**Alternative 5-star Weapons:**
*   **Primordial Jade Winged-Spear:** Offers Crit Rate and an ATK% buff on hit.
*   **Vortex Vanquisher:** Provides ATK% and a shield strength buff, but requires a shield to be active to maximize its potential.

**Alternative 4-star Weapons:**
*   **Deathmatch:** Battle Pass weapon, provides Crit Rate.
*   **Favonius Lance:** Excellent for team-wide energy generation, especially if ER substats are lacking on artifacts.

**Artifact Set:**
*   **2-piece Noblesse Oblige + 2-piece Archaic Petra:** This is a strong hybrid set, boosting Burst DMG with Noblesse and Geo DMG with Petra.
*   **4-piece Noblesse Oblige:** If Zhongli is the primary Burst damage dealer and the team benefits from the ATK buff after his Burst.
*   **2-piece Tenacity of the Millelith + 2-piece Gladiator's Finale/Shimenawa's Reminiscence:** For a more balanced HP and ATK build.

**Main Stats:**
*   **Sands:** HP% or ATK% (HP% for more shield, ATK% for more damage)
*   **Goblet:** Geo DMG Bonus%
*   **Circlet:** Crit Rate% or Crit DMG%

**Substats Priority:** Crit Rate%/Crit DMG% > HP% > ATK% > Energy Recharge

### F2P Options

*   **Weapons:**
    *   **Favonius Lance:** The best F2P option for energy generation, allowing for more frequent Bursts and thus more shield uptime and RES shred. Requires Crit Rate substats to proc its passive.
    *   **Prototype Starglitter:** Provides Energy Recharge and a DMG bonus after using Elemental Burst.
    *   **Black Tassel:** Still the best F2P option for pure shielding.
*   **Artifacts:**
    *   **2-piece Tenacity of the Millelith + 2-piece Defender's Will:** Offers a 20% HP boost and a 30% DEF bonus, which indirectly helps shield strength as DEF also contributes to shield scaling.
    *   **Mix and Match:** Combine any 2-piece sets that provide HP% or DEF% until better sets are farmed.

## Gold Standard Defensive Support

Zhongli is widely considered the gold standard for defensive support in Genshin Impact due to a confluence of factors that make him uniquely invaluable across virtually all team compositions. His **Jade Shield** is not merely a damage mitigation tool; it is a near-impenetrable barrier that scales exceptionally well with HP, allowing players to invest heavily in his survivability without sacrificing his core function. The **150% absorption of Physical DMG** makes him a crucial counter against enemies with high physical damage output, such as Ruin Graders or certain Abyss floor enemies.

Beyond raw shielding power, Zhongli provides **unparalleled interruption resistance** to the active character. This means that even without his shield, Zhongli's presence on the field, or the active character being protected by his shield, prevents stagger and knockback. This quality-of-life improvement is immense, allowing characters like catalysts users or slow-attacking claymore users to unleash their full damage potential without fear of being interrupted.

The **20% Universal RES Shred** applied by his Stone Stele is arguably his most significant contribution. This debuff applies to all enemies and affects all damage types, including Physical, Elemental, and Geo. This means that every character in the party, regardless of their elemental alignment or damage type, benefits directly from Zhongli's presence. This universal buff is incredibly potent and often leads to significant increases in overall team DPS, making him a force multiplier in any offensive strategy.

His **Elemental Burst, Planet Befall**, offers a potent combination of AoE Geo DMG and a 4-second **Petrify** status. This crowd control is invaluable for grouping enemies, setting up damage windows, or safely repositioning. The ability to completely immobilize dangerous enemies allows for precise targeting, safe healing, or the execution of complex combos without interruption. The low Energy cost and short cooldown make it a readily available tool for controlling the battlefield.

Finally, Zhongli's **ease of use and low investment requirements** for his primary role contribute to his status. While a DPS Zhongli requires significant investment in Crit stats and ATK, a pure shieldbot Zhongli primarily needs HP. This makes him accessible to players at all stages of the game. His kit is straightforward: deploy a Stele, hold Skill for a shield, and use Burst when available. This simplicity, combined with his overwhelming utility, solidifies his position as the definitive defensive support in Genshin Impact.
