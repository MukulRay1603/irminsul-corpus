# Nahida and Sumeru History

## The Legacy of Rukkhadevata and the Cataclysm

Sumeru's history is deeply intertwined with the figure of the original Dendro Archon, known as Greater Lord Rukkhadevata. She was a benevolent deity who presided over Sumeru for millennia, nurturing its flora and fauna and guiding its people. Her reign was characterized by wisdom, compassion, and a profound connection to the natural world. However, this era of peace was shattered by a cataclysm known as the Withering, a mysterious blight that began to corrupt the land, turning lush forests into desolate wastelands and driving creatures mad.

The Withering was an existential threat to Sumeru, and Rukkhadevata dedicated herself to combating it. She fought tirelessly against the encroaching darkness, but the blight proved too powerful. In a desperate act of sacrifice, she absorbed the entirety of the Withering into her own being, intending to purify it and prevent it from consuming her nation. This immense act of selflessness, however, came at a terrible cost. Rukkhadevata's physical form was destroyed, and her consciousness was scattered. She managed to preserve a fragment of her being, which would eventually manifest as the current Dendro Archon, Nahida. The cataclysm and Rukkhadevata's sacrifice left an indelible mark on Sumeru, fostering a deep-seated fear of the Withering and a complex relationship with the divine.

## The Founding and Corruption of the Akademiya

Following the cataclysm and Rukkhadevata's sacrifice, the people of Sumeru, under the guidance of surviving sages and scholars, established the Sumeru Akademiya. Initially, the Akademiya was founded with noble intentions: to preserve knowledge, advance understanding, and ensure the prosperity of Sumeru. It became the central hub for learning, research, and governance, attracting brilliant minds from across the land. The six Darshans – Kshahrewar, Amurta, Vahumana, Haravatat, Spenta, and Rta – were established to cover diverse fields of study, from engineering and medicine to history and ethics.

However, over the centuries, the Akademiya's pursuit of knowledge began to warp. The fear of another cataclysm, coupled with the Archon's absence, led to a growing emphasis on control and the suppression of potentially dangerous information. The Sages, the ruling council of the Akademiya, became increasingly authoritarian, prioritizing the preservation of their own power and the perceived stability of Sumeru over genuine intellectual freedom. This led to the gradual corruption of the Akademiya into an apparatus that controlled and curated knowledge, rather than fostering its open exploration. Certain historical events, particularly those related to the Archon and the cataclysm, were deliberately obscured or erased from public record.

## Nahida's Imprisonment and Connection to Irminsul

The current Dendro Archon, Nahida, was born from the remnants of Rukkhadevata's consciousness after the cataclysm. However, her existence was immediately deemed a threat by the Akademiya Sages. Fearing her potential power and the possibility of her challenging their authority, they decided to imprison her. For five hundred years, Nahida was confined to the Sanctuary of Surasthana, a gilded cage within the Palace of Alcazarzaray. She was denied direct interaction with the world and its people, her only connection being through the Irminsul, the celestial tree that records all knowledge and history in Teyvat.

Through the Irminsul, Nahida gained an unparalleled understanding of Sumeru's past, present, and the intricate workings of the world. She could access memories, observe events, and learn from the collective consciousness of humanity. Furthermore, she developed the ability to enter the dreams of Sumeru's inhabitants, subtly influencing them and offering guidance without revealing her true identity or predicament. This unique perspective, gained from isolation and observation, shaped her into a wise and compassionate Archon, despite her physical confinement. Her existence became a paradox: a ruler who had never truly ruled, a god who lived in secret.

## King Deshret and the Ancient Desert Civilization

The history of Sumeru extends far beyond the lush rainforests and the Akademiya's influence. The vast desert regions hold the remnants of an ancient and advanced civilization, most notably that of King Deshret. Deshret was a powerful ruler who sought to elevate his people through knowledge and technology, particularly in the fields of alchemy and astronomy. His civilization was renowned for its architectural marvels, sophisticated irrigation systems, and a deep understanding of the stars.

King Deshret's era was marked by a significant event: the arrival of a celestial calamity, often referred to as the "Sky-Dweller" or a similar cosmic threat. In an attempt to protect his people and his kingdom from this disaster, Deshret embarked on a monumental project to construct a divine artificial paradise, a grand palace in the sky. He also delved into forbidden knowledge and sought the aid of forbidden powers, leading to the creation of the Tanit tribe and the development of potent, yet dangerous, technologies. His ambition, while aimed at salvation, ultimately led to the downfall of his civilization, leaving behind ruins and legends that hint at a forgotten past and a cautionary tale of unchecked ambition. The desert's history, therefore, represents a crucial, often overlooked, layer of Sumeru's past, predating the current Archon and the Akademiya's dominance.

## The Scarlet King Mythology

The mythology surrounding the Scarlet King is deeply intertwined with King Deshret and the ancient desert civilization. The Scarlet King is often depicted as a rival or a counterpart to Deshret, representing a different approach to power and dominion. Legends describe him as a figure who wielded immense power, possibly even challenging the divine order. Some tales suggest he was a usurper, while others portray him as a tragic figure who sought to protect his people through more ruthless means.

The Scarlet King is associated with the concept of "red," symbolizing passion, power, and perhaps even destruction. His influence is felt in the lingering myths and rituals of the desert tribes, particularly the Tanit. The Tanit's lore speaks of a pact made with a powerful entity, possibly the Scarlet King himself, in exchange for protection and power. This entity is often associated with the desert's harshness and its hidden dangers. The Scarlet King's story serves as a reminder of the ancient conflicts and power struggles that shaped Sumeru's foundational history, a history that the Akademiya has largely sought to suppress or reframe.

## The Akademiya's Scheme with Scaramouche and the Gnosis

In their pursuit of control and their desire to circumvent the natural order, the Akademiya Sages devised a dangerous plan involving the Fatui Harbinger, Scaramouche, also known as the Wanderer. They sought to obtain a powerful artifact known as the Gnosis, which is the divine essence of an Archon. Their ultimate goal was to create a new Dendro Archon, one that they could fully control and manipulate, thereby securing their own power and preventing any future threats to their authority.

The Sages entered into an agreement with Scaramouche, promising him a Gnosis in exchange for his assistance in their scheme. They believed that by manipulating the divine essence and potentially using Scaramouche's unique nature as a puppet, they could engineer a new Archon that would be subservient to them. This plan was not only morally reprehensible but also incredibly dangerous, as it involved tampering with the fundamental forces of Teyvat and risked catastrophic consequences for Sumeru and potentially the entire world. This scheme highlights the extent of the Sages' corruption and their willingness to engage with dangerous external forces for their own gain.

## Nahida's Sacrifice for the Irminsul

During the events that led to the Traveler's confrontation with the Akademiya Sages and Scaramouche, Nahida faced a profound choice. She discovered that the Sages' plan, if successful, would not only lead to her replacement but also potentially corrupt the Irminsul itself, the repository of all knowledge and history. The Irminsul is fundamental to the stability of Teyvat, and any alteration to its records could have devastating and far-reaching consequences, erasing memories and rewriting reality.

To prevent this catastrophic outcome and to protect the integrity of the Irminsul, Nahida made a monumental sacrifice. She chose to allow Scaramouche to absorb the Gnosis and to proceed with his own ambitions, which ultimately led to his own confrontation with the Traveler. In a critical moment, Nahida, with the Traveler's help, managed to sever Scaramouche's connection to the Irminsul and prevent him from fully rewriting history. However, in doing so, she had to relinquish her own divine authority and her connection to the Irminsul, effectively sacrificing her Archon status and her access to the collective knowledge of Teyvat. This act of selflessness ensured the preservation of history and the stability of Teyvat, even at the cost of her own power and position. She gave up her divine right and her ability to directly influence the Irminsul to safeguard the world's true history.
