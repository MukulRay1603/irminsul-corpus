# Khaenri'ah and the Abyss

Khaenri'ah was a technologically advanced, underground nation that existed approximately 500 years prior to the current in-game timeline. Unlike other nations in Teyvat, Khaenri'ah rejected divine authority, famously having no Archon and no god. This lack of divine connection fueled their pursuit of knowledge and mastery over alchemy, machinery, and the very essence of life. Their achievements were unparalleled, allowing them to create sophisticated automatons, advanced weaponry, and even artificial lifeforms. The nation's prosperity and defiance of the divine order ultimately drew the ire of the Heavenly Principles, leading to its catastrophic destruction during the Cataclysm.

The Cataclysm, a world-altering event that occurred 500 years ago, was the direct consequence of Khaenri'ah's downfall. While the exact sequence of events remains shrouded in mystery and conflicting accounts, it is understood that Celestia, the divine realm, orchestrated Khaenri'ah's destruction. The reasons cited are Khaenri'ah's hubris in defying the divine and their pursuit of forbidden knowledge, particularly concerning the manipulation of life and the Abyss. The Cataclysm not only annihilated Khaenri'ah but also unleashed a wave of destruction and corruption across Teyvat, leaving deep scars on the world and its inhabitants.

A pivotal figure in Khaenri'ah's history and its subsequent destruction is Rhinedottir, also known as "Gold." She was a renowned alchemist and artificer, credited with creating some of Khaenri'ah's most remarkable and terrifying creations. Her most famous works include the "puppets" and "homunculi," artificial beings crafted with intricate alchemical processes. Among these creations is Albedo, the Chief Alchemist of the Knights of Favonius, who is revealed to be Rhinedottir's "first and most perfect creation," a homunculus designed to understand the world. Rhinedottir's alchemical prowess was so profound that she is also believed to be responsible for the creation of the "puppets" that became the monsters of the Cataclysm, and potentially the very means of Khaenri'ah's destruction. Her legacy is intertwined with both the nation's zenith and its nadir, representing the pinnacle of Khaenri'ah's scientific ambition and its ultimate undoing.

The fate of Khaenri'ah's people after the Cataclysm was a brutal and eternal punishment. Those who were part of the Khaenri'an nobility, who likely held positions of power and influence, were transformed into monstrous beings, cursed to wander the land as abominations. These creatures are often associated with the Abyss and its corrupted energies. The common folk, the ordinary citizens of Khaenri'ah, suffered a different, yet equally grim fate. They were transformed into Hilichurls, immortal wanderers cursed to an unending existence of primitive survival and a loss of their former selves. This transformation stripped them of their intellect, memories, and their very humanity, leaving them as shambling remnants of a once-great civilization. Their immortality ensures they are doomed to witness the passage of time and the decay of the world without end.

King Irmin, the last ruler of Khaenri'ah, met a particularly tragic end. He was corrupted by the powers of the Abyss, becoming known as the "Eclipse King." This transformation signifies the nation's descent into darkness and its ultimate succumbing to the very forces it may have sought to control or understand. The corruption of their king symbolizes the perversion of Khaenri'ah's ideals and the tragic culmination of their defiance. The details of his corruption and the extent of his influence as the Eclipse King are still largely unknown, but it is implied to be a significant factor in the nation's final moments.

Dainsleif, known as the "Bough Keeper," is a unique survivor of Khaenri'ah. He was cursed with immortality, not as a reward, but as a perpetual penance. His curse compels him to witness the world's changes and the lingering consequences of Khaenri'ah's fall, forever burdened by the memories of his lost homeland and its people. He roams Teyvat, observing and occasionally intervening, driven by a complex mix of regret, a desire for atonement, and a deep-seated hatred for Celestia and the divine order. His immortality is a constant reminder of his failure to protect his nation and his people, making him a tragic figure haunted by the past.

The Abyss Order is a clandestine organization that emerged in the wake of Khaenri'ah's destruction. It is composed of the descendants of Khaenri'ah, including the transformed nobility and those who retain some semblance of their former selves, as well as other individuals who have been corrupted or drawn to the power of the Abyss. Their primary objective is to overthrow Celestia and dismantle the current divine order that they believe is unjust and responsible for the suffering of their people. They seek to restore Khaenri'ah or, failing that, to exact vengeance upon those who destroyed it. The Abyss Order actively seeks to reclaim lost Khaenri'an artifacts and knowledge, believing these hold the key to achieving their goals. They employ dark magic, corrupted creatures, and the remnants of Khaenri'an technology in their operations.

The Traveler's twin sibling plays a crucial and enigmatic role within the Abyss Order. After being separated from the Traveler at the beginning of their journey, the sibling encountered and was eventually swayed by the powers and ideology of the Abyss. They have risen to a position of leadership within the organization, acting as a guiding force and a prominent figurehead. The sibling's motivations are complex; while they seem to genuinely believe in the Abyss Order's cause and its potential to bring about a better world, their methods and ultimate goals remain ambiguous. They appear to have a deep understanding of the Abyss and its powers, and their influence has significantly shaped the Order's trajectory. The sibling's leadership suggests a potential for either a radical transformation of Teyvat or a complete descent into destructive chaos, depending on their true intentions and the extent of their corruption. Their connection to the Traveler adds a deeply personal dimension to the conflict, as the Traveler is caught between understanding their sibling's choices and opposing the destructive path of the Abyss Order.
