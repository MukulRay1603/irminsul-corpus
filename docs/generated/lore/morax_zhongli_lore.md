# Morax and Liyue History

Morax, known also as Rex Lapis, is the Geo Archon, the God of Contracts, and the oldest of the Seven Archons currently in power. His reign over Liyue spans millennia, shaping the nation's culture, economy, and very identity through his unwavering adherence to contracts and his profound understanding of humanity. He is a figure of immense power, wisdom, and stoicism, having personally participated in and shaped the foundational events of Teyvat's history.

## The Archon War and the Founding of Liyue

Morax's genesis predates the Archon War, a cataclysmic conflict where seven powerful beings vied for divine dominion over Teyvat. During this era, the land was rife with chaos, ruled by ancient gods and primordial beings. Morax, a formidable warrior and strategist, fought with unparalleled ferocity, forging alliances and defeating numerous rivals. His most significant contributions to the eventual victory of the Seven involved his mastery of Geo, allowing him to raise mountains, reshape landscapes, and create impenetrable defenses. He was instrumental in the defeat of powerful entities like Haishan and the primordial god of storms, Azhdaha, whom he later subdued and bound to his service as the first of the Adepti.

Following the Archon War, Morax, as the victor and the embodiment of order, claimed dominion over the land that would become Liyue. He did not seek to rule through brute force alone, but rather through the establishment of a fundamental principle: contracts. Morax believed that the most stable and prosperous society could be built upon mutual agreement, trust, and clearly defined obligations. This philosophy became the bedrock of Liyue, influencing every aspect of its development, from trade agreements and legal systems to personal relationships. He envisioned a nation where every interaction, no matter how small, was governed by a binding promise, ensuring fairness and predictability.

## The Adepti and the Human-God Pact

To aid in governing Liyue and to protect it from the lingering threats of ancient gods and monsters, Morax summoned and bound numerous powerful beings, collectively known as the Adepti. These included celestial beasts, ancient spirits, and powerful mortals who had achieved a form of immortality through their service and devotion. Among the most prominent of these were the Yakshas, a group of powerful warriors tasked with protecting Liyue from demonic forces, and the Liyue Qixing, a council of influential mortal leaders who would eventually share governance.

Morax established a sacred pact between himself, the Adepti, and the mortal inhabitants of Liyue. This pact was not merely a decree but a living agreement, wherein the Adepti would protect Liyue from external threats and internal chaos, while humanity would honor Morax and his laws, fostering prosperity and upholding the principles of contract. This symbiotic relationship allowed Liyue to flourish, becoming a beacon of civilization and economic power in Teyvat. The Adepti, bound by their loyalty and the ancient pact, acted as guardians and advisors, while the humans, guided by Morax's wisdom, built a thriving society.

## Rex Lapis and the Rite of Descension

For thousands of years, Morax, under the guise of Rex Lapis, ruled Liyue directly. He was revered as the "Prime of the Archons" and the "God of Contracts." His presence was a constant, a symbol of Liyue's stability and prosperity. The Rite of Descension, an annual ceremony held on the anniversary of Liyue's founding, was a testament to his enduring influence. During this ritual, Rex Lapis would descend from the heavens in his true form, a colossal dragon of jade, to commune with his people and the Liyue Qixing, discussing the state of the nation and reaffirming the pact. This event was a cornerstone of Liyue's culture, a moment of profound connection between the divine and the mortal.

The mythology surrounding Rex Lapis was vast, with countless tales of his wisdom, his battles, and his unwavering commitment to justice. He was credited with teaching humans metallurgy, agriculture, and the art of governance. His statues, depicting him in his draconic form, dotted the landscape, serving as constant reminders of his power and his protection. The concept of "Contracts" permeated Liyuean society, from the meticulous record-keeping of the Millelith to the intricate trade negotiations that fueled the nation's economy.

## The Defeat of Osial and the Primordial Gods

Morax's reign was not without its challenges. He faced numerous threats from ancient, primordial gods who resented his dominion and the rise of mortal civilization. The most significant of these challenges was the re-emergence of Osial, the Great God of the Sea, and his consort, the Great God of the Vortex, who sought to reclaim Liyue and drown its cities. Morax, alongside the Adepti, engaged in a titanic battle against these ancient forces. He personally fought Osial, a battle that shook the very foundations of the earth and sea. Using his immense Geo power, Morax ultimately defeated and sealed away Osial and his allies, further solidifying his position as the protector of Liyue and demonstrating the futility of challenging the established order. This victory cemented the belief that Liyue was under divine protection and that its future was secure under Morax's guidance.

## The Decision to Abdicate and the Gnosis

After millennia of direct rule, Morax began to contemplate the future of Liyue. He recognized that his people had grown, learned, and developed the capacity for self-governance. He saw that the principles of contract and order he had instilled had taken root, becoming an intrinsic part of Liyuean society. The constant need for divine intervention was diminishing as humanity matured. This realization led him to a momentous decision: to relinquish his Gnosis and fake his own death.

His primary motivation was to test Liyue's readiness to stand on its own. He orchestrated the "Shattered Mask" incident, a meticulously planned event where he appeared to be killed during a staged confrontation with the Fatui, specifically during the Rite of Descension. This act plunged Liyue into a period of uncertainty and grief, forcing the Liyue Qixing and the Adepti to step up and assume greater responsibility. It was a final, grand contract: a test of Liyue's resilience and independence.

The Gnosis, the divine symbol of his Archon status, was entrusted to the Tsaritsa, the Cryo Archon. This was not an act of surrender or defeat, but a calculated move. Morax designed the terms of this exchange himself, a contract with the Tsaritsa. He foresaw the Fatui's machinations and the potential for the Gnosis to be used for nefarious purposes. By handing it over under his own terms, he ensured that it would not be used to directly harm Liyue and that the Tsaritsa would be bound by the contract he established. He believed that the Tsaritsa, despite her current path, understood the weight of divine power and would honor the contract, at least to a degree that would not jeopardize Liyue. He also recognized that the Tsaritsa's pursuit of a new divine order might, in its own way, serve as a counterweight to other potential threats to Teyvat.

## Liyue's Readiness for Self-Governance

Morax's ultimate assessment was that Liyue was ready to govern itself. The thousands of years of his rule, coupled with the inherent Liyuean values of diligence, integrity, and adherence to principles, had cultivated a society capable of navigating its own destiny. The "Shattered Mask" incident, despite its initial chaos, ultimately proved his point. The Liyue Qixing, particularly figures like Ningguang and Keqing, demonstrated remarkable leadership, rallying the people and maintaining order. The Adepti, while still present, allowed humanity to take the lead. Morax, observing from the shadows, saw that the contracts he had established had become ingrained in the very fabric of Liyuean life, guiding their decisions and ensuring their continued prosperity. His departure was not an abandonment, but a graduation, a testament to his success as a ruler and his faith in the people he had guided for so long. He had successfully transitioned Liyue from a nation ruled by a god to a nation governed by its people, upholding the spirit of contract and order he so deeply valued.
