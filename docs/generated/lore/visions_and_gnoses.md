# Visions and Gnoses Explained

## Visions: The Spark of Ambition

Visions are crystalline artifacts imbued with elemental power, granted by the divine entity known as Celestia to individuals who demonstrate extraordinary ambition, will, or a deep connection to a specific element. These are not mere trinkets but conduits that allow mortals to wield elemental abilities far beyond their natural capacity. The process by which Celestia bestows a Vision is shrouded in mystery, but it is understood to be a recognition of a profound, often life-defining, aspiration or a moment of intense emotional struggle and resolve. For example, Keqing's Hydro Vision was granted after she passionately advocated for reform in Liyue, believing in a future where humanity, not Adepti, steered its own destiny. Similarly, Xiangling's Pyro Vision manifested during a particularly challenging culinary endeavor, fueled by her unwavering dedication to mastering the art of cooking and pushing its boundaries.

The elemental attunement of a Vision is not arbitrary. It reflects the core of the individual's ambition or the nature of the trial they overcame. A Pyro Vision often signifies passion, a fiery spirit, or a desire to protect and forge. Cryo Visions are frequently associated with resilience, a hidden strength, or a desire for control and order. Anemo Visions are linked to freedom, a yearning for change, or a spirit that cannot be contained. Geo Visions are granted to those with steadfast resolve, a deep sense of responsibility, or an unyielding foundation. Electro Visions are typically given to those with sharp intellect, a rebellious streak, or a desire for swift, decisive action. Dendro Visions, the rarest, are bestowed upon individuals with a profound connection to life, knowledge, or growth. Hydro Visions are often granted to those with adaptability, empathy, or a deep emotional wellspring.

The power granted by a Vision is not limitless. While it allows for the manipulation of elemental energy, the extent of this power is largely determined by the Vision holder's own skill, training, and understanding of their abilities. A Vision is a tool, and like any tool, its effectiveness depends on the wielder. The Traveler, for instance, can attune to different elements by interacting with Statues of The Seven, demonstrating that the capacity for elemental manipulation is inherent, with Visions acting as catalysts or amplifiers for specific manifestations. The philosophical debate surrounding Visions centers on whether they are genuine gifts of empowerment or subtle chains binding mortals to Celestia's will. Some, like Keqing, view them as validation of human potential, while others, like the Fatui, see them as instruments of divine control, designed to foster dependence and obedience. The act of a Vision shattering, as seen with some characters under extreme duress or despair, signifies a loss of that connection to ambition or a fundamental shift in their being, leading to the forfeiture of their elemental powers.

## Gnoses: The Archons' Divine Authority

Gnoses are the physical manifestations of an Archon's divine authority and their direct connection to Celestia. They are the source of an Archon's immense power, allowing them to govern their respective nations and maintain their elemental dominion. Unlike Visions, which are granted to individuals, Gnoses are intrinsic to the Archons themselves, representing their status as divine rulers. Each Archon possesses a Gnosis corresponding to their element: the Pyro Gnosis for Murata, the Cryo Gnosis for the Tsaritsa, the Electro Gnosis for Beelzebul, the Geo Gnosis for Zhongli, the Hydro Gnosis for the God of Justice, the Anemo Gnosis for Barbatos, and the Dendro Gnosis for the Dendro Archon.

The primary function of a Gnosis is to channel and amplify the Archon's elemental power, enabling them to perform feats that would be impossible for even the most powerful Vision holders. It is also believed to be Celestia's primary means of control over the Archons and, by extension, the mortal world. By holding the Gnoses, Celestia ensures that the Archons adhere to its divine mandate and maintain the established order. The collection of Gnoses by the Tsaritsa, the current Cryo Archon, is the central driving force behind the Fatui's machinations. Her goal is to wage war against Celestia and overthrow the divine order, and she believes that by accumulating the Gnoses, she can strip the other Archons of their divine status and power, thereby weakening Celestia's grip on Teyvat.

The loss of a Gnosis has profound consequences for an Archon. It diminishes their divine power, severs their direct link to Celestia, and effectively strips them of their formal divine status. This does not necessarily mean they lose all their power or their connection to their element entirely, but their authority and capacity to rule are severely compromised. Zhongli, the Geo Archon, voluntarily surrendered his Gnosis to the Tsaritsa as part of a pact, signifying his weariness with his millennia-long role and his desire for humanity to forge its own path. Following this, he retained his immense power and knowledge but relinquished his direct divine mandate and connection to Celestia's authority. Similarly, the Electro Archon, Beelzebul, lost her Gnosis to the Fatui, which contributed to her internal struggles and the subsequent pursuit of eternity as a means to prevent further loss and maintain control. The precise mechanics of how a Gnosis is transferred or stolen are not fully detailed, but it is understood to be a significant event that alters the cosmic balance.

The implications of these events are far-reaching. They suggest that divine authority is not absolute and can be challenged. The Archons, despite their power, are not entirely independent of Celestia, and their Gnoses serve as both a source of power and a symbol of their subservience. The Tsaritsa's rebellion, fueled by the collection of Gnoses, represents a direct confrontation with this divine hierarchy. It raises questions about the nature of power itself: is it derived from a higher authority, or can it be seized and wielded through individual will and ambition? The stories of Archons losing their Gnoses highlight the fragility of divine power and the potential for mortals, or even other gods, to disrupt the established order. The eventual fate of the Gnoses and the outcome of the Tsaritsa's war will undoubtedly reshape the understanding of divinity and power in Teyvat, potentially ushering in an era where mortal agency and ambition hold greater sway than the decrees of Celestia. The loss of a Gnosis does not necessarily equate to the loss of an Archon's essence or their connection to their people, but it fundamentally alters their position within the divine framework and their ability to exert direct, divinely sanctioned influence. Zhongli's continued presence and influence in Liyue after relinquishing his Gnosis exemplifies this, as his wisdom and power remain, albeit without the direct backing of Celestia's authority.
