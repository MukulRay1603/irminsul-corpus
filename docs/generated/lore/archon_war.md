# The Archon War and Ancient History

The Archon War was a cataclysmic period in Teyvat's history, spanning millennia, during which countless gods and their followers clashed for dominance, territory, and the fervent devotion of mortals. This era predates the current world order and saw the rise of the Seven Archons who now govern the seven nations. The war was a brutal crucible where divine power was tested, and only the strongest, most cunning, or most adaptable deities could endure. The outcome of this conflict fundamentally reshaped Teyvat, establishing the foundations for the current political landscape and the divine hierarchy overseen by Celestia.

## The Genesis of Conflict and the Nature of Divinity

Before the Archon War, Teyvat was a realm teeming with an immeasurable number of gods, each with their own domains, powers, and mortal worshippers. These deities were not necessarily benevolent rulers; they were beings of immense power who often demanded absolute loyalty and tribute, engaging in territorial disputes and wars of attrition. The concept of "Archon" itself emerged from this period, signifying a god who had successfully claimed dominion over a territory and its people, often through military might and the accumulation of sufficient faith. The intensity of mortal worship directly fueled a god's power, making the acquisition and retention of followers a primary objective. This created a perpetual cycle of conflict, as gods sought to expand their influence by either converting or conquering the followers of their rivals. The very fabric of Teyvat was shaped by these divine battles, with landscapes scarred by divine energies and entire civilizations rising and falling based on the fortunes of their patron deities. The Archon War was not a single, unified conflict but rather a series of interconnected wars, alliances, and betrayals that spanned vast stretches of time, with periods of intense warfare punctuated by uneasy truces.

## The Rise of the Seven Archons

The current Seven Archons, each representing one of the seven nations, ascended to their positions by outlasting and defeating their divine competitors. This process was not merely about raw power but also about strategic acumen, the ability to inspire loyalty, and often, a degree of ruthlessness.

### Morax and the Foundation of Liyue

Morax, the Geo Archon and former Rex Lapis, was a pivotal figure in the Archon War, particularly in the region that would become Liyue. His reign was characterized by his unparalleled martial prowess and his strategic brilliance in forging alliances and crushing his enemies. Morax engaged in countless battles against numerous gods, including the formidable Guizhong, the God of Dust, whom he eventually defeated and absorbed into his domain, and the monstrous Osial, the Great God of the Sea. His most significant contribution was the unification of the various tribes and nascent civilizations of Liyue under his banner, a process that involved both conquest and diplomacy. Morax's battles were legendary, shaping the very geography of Liyue. He is credited with carving out mountains with his Geo powers and creating fertile plains from barren lands. The contracts he forged with the Adepti, powerful beings who aided him in his wars, solidified his rule and established a unique system of governance that would endure for millennia. The Adepti, initially independent entities, were bound to Morax through solemn oaths and pacts, agreeing to protect Liyue in exchange for his protection and a place in his divine council. This alliance was crucial in securing Liyue's borders and repelling numerous divine incursions.

### Barbatos and the Liberation of Mondstadt

In stark contrast to Morax's martial approach, Barbatos, the Anemo Archon, achieved victory through guile and the empowerment of mortals. Before Barbatos, the land of Mondstadt was under the tyrannical rule of Decarabian, the God of Storms. Decarabian was a cruel deity who kept his people confined within a towering city of walls, perpetually shielded from the harsh winds and storms he himself created. A nameless bard, inspired by the suffering of his people and empowered by Barbatos, led a rebellion against Decarabian. This rebellion was not a direct divine confrontation but a mortal uprising, with Barbatos subtly aiding the bard and his followers by weakening Decarabian's control and manipulating the winds. The bard's defiance culminated in the destruction of Decarabian's walls and the eventual downfall of the Storm God. Barbatos, who had been observing and subtly influencing events, then descended and took the place of Decarabian, promising freedom and the winds of change. His victory was not one of conquest but of liberation, establishing a precedent for Mondstadt's future emphasis on freedom and individual liberty.

## The Fate of Defeated Gods

The Archon War was a brutal affair, and the fates of the defeated gods varied significantly. Some were utterly annihilated, their divine essence dispersed and their existence erased from the world. Others, however, survived in diminished forms, their power greatly reduced, their followers scattered, and their domains lost. These surviving deities often became lesser spirits, lingering in forgotten ruins or isolated pockets of the world, their influence minimal but their presence a reminder of the turbulent past. Some might have been imprisoned or sealed away by the victorious Archons. The exact number and identities of these defeated gods are largely lost to history, but their lingering echoes can be found in ancient myths and the desolate landscapes of Teyvat. The Archons were not always merciful; the complete eradication of a rival god was often the most efficient way to secure their territory and prevent future challenges.

## The Adepti War and the Pact with Morax

While the Archon War encompassed conflicts across Teyvat, Liyue experienced its own significant internal struggle, often referred to as the Adepti War. This period saw the powerful, immortal beings known as the Adepti, who predated the current Archons and possessed immense power, either align with or oppose Morax. Morax, recognizing the immense power and potential of these beings, sought to forge a lasting alliance. Through a series of arduous battles and strategic negotiations, Morax proved his strength and his vision for Liyue. The Adepti, impressed by his power and the promise of stability and protection for the land and its people, eventually entered into contracts with him. These contracts were not mere agreements but sacred oaths, binding the Adepti to serve and protect Liyue under Morax's command. In return, Morax pledged his own power and authority to safeguard their existence and their domains. This pact was instrumental in solidifying Morax's rule and establishing the unique symbiosis between the divine, the immortal, and the mortal in Liyue. The Adepti became the guardians of Liyue, a role they continue to fulfill even after Morax's abdication.

## The End of the War and the World Order

The conclusion of the Archon War marked a profound shift in Teyvat's history, ushering in an era of relative stability under the governance of the Seven Archons. The victors, having consolidated their power and territory, established a new divine order, with Celestia, the mysterious celestial power, seemingly overseeing and sanctioning this arrangement. The Seven Archons became the supreme rulers of their respective nations, their authority derived from their victory in the war and their continued ability to command the faith of their people. This new order brought an end to the widespread, chaotic divine warfare that had defined the previous millennia. However, the scars of the Archon War remained, etched into the land and woven into the myths and legends of Teyvat. The memory of countless fallen gods and the brutal struggle for survival served as a constant undercurrent, shaping the cultures and beliefs of the surviving nations. The current world order, with its distinct nations and their Archon rulers, is a direct legacy of the Archon War, a testament to the power struggles and divine machinations that forged the world as it is known today. The existence of Celestia as the ultimate arbiter, though its true nature remains enigmatic, suggests a higher power that ultimately dictated the terms of peace and established the current divine hierarchy.
