# Ei, Makoto, and Inazuma History

## The Archons of Eternity: Makoto and Ei

The Electro Archon, known as Beelzebul, is the current ruler of Inazuma. However, the title of Electro Archon was not always hers. The original Electro Archon was her twin sister, Makoto. Both sisters were born on Watatsumi Island, though Makoto later moved to Narukami Island. Makoto was the Archon of the ideal of "Eternity," which she interpreted as a state of continuous growth and evolution, a dynamic preservation that embraced change and progress. She was a compassionate and wise ruler, deeply loved by her people. Ei, on the other hand, was more pragmatic and inclined towards introspection. She was a formidable warrior, skilled in combat and deeply protective of her sister and her nation. Their shared past on Watatsumi Island, a place of deep spiritual significance, forged a bond that transcended their differing philosophies.

## The Cataclysm and Makoto's Demise

Approximately 500 years before the current in-game timeline, Teyvat was ravaged by a cataclysm. The exact nature of this cataclysm is shrouded in mystery, but it involved a devastating conflict that threatened the very existence of the Seven Nations. During this tumultuous period, Makoto, the Electro Archon, sacrificed herself to protect Inazuma and its people. While the specifics of her death are not fully detailed, it is understood that she perished during this event, leaving Ei as the sole surviving Archon of Inazuma. This loss had a profound and lasting impact on Ei, plunging her into deep grief and isolation. The weight of her sister's death, coupled with the immense responsibility of leading a nation, fundamentally altered Ei's perspective on governance and the nature of eternity.

## Ei's Five Centuries of Solitude and the Creation of the Shogun Puppet

Following Makoto's death, Ei retreated into a state of profound isolation within the Plane of Euthymia, her personal domain. For five hundred years, she wrestled with her grief, the trauma of the cataclysm, and the fear of losing what remained of her nation. This period of intense introspection led her to a new interpretation of eternity. She came to believe that true preservation lay not in dynamic growth, but in absolute stasis – a state where nothing could be lost because nothing could change. This philosophical shift was a direct response to her perceived failure to protect her sister and her nation from the ravages of the cataclysm.

To govern Inazuma while she remained in seclusion, Ei conceived of the Shogun puppet. This automaton was designed to embody her will and enforce her vision of eternity. The puppet was imbued with immense power and programmed to rule with an iron fist, ensuring that Inazuma would remain unchanged and thus, in Ei's eyes, safe from further loss. The puppet served as a physical manifestation of Ei's desire for control and her fear of vulnerability. It was a tool to maintain order and prevent any disruption that might echo the chaos of the cataclysm. The creation of the puppet marked a significant departure from Makoto's more empathetic and adaptive leadership style.

## The Vision Hunt Decree: Grief Manifested as Policy

Ei's grief and her newfound philosophy of stasis culminated in the implementation of the Vision Hunt Decree. This decree, enacted 500 years after the cataclysm, was a direct consequence of her fear of change and loss. Believing that Visions, which represent mortal ambition and the potential for change, were a source of chaos and a threat to her vision of eternity, Ei ordered the confiscation of all Visions within Inazuma. The stated goal was to prevent the potential for individuals to ascend and challenge the divine order, thereby preserving the status quo.

However, the underlying motivation was deeply personal. Ei saw Visions as symbols of the very aspirations and desires that, in her mind, had led to the cataclysm and her sister's death. By removing these symbols of ambition, she sought to create a society where such potential for disruption was eliminated. This policy, born from profound personal pain, inflicted immense suffering on the people of Inazuma, sparking widespread rebellion and discontent. The decree was a stark manifestation of Ei's internal struggle, her inability to reconcile with her past, and her misguided attempt to control the uncontrollable through absolute rigidity.

## Philosophical Divergence: Eternity as Stasis vs. True Preservation

The core of the conflict in Inazuma lies in the differing interpretations of "Eternity." Makoto's vision of Eternity was one of dynamic preservation, a continuous process of growth, adaptation, and evolution that allowed for resilience and progress. This was a philosophy that embraced the natural order of life and change. In contrast, Ei's interpretation, born from her trauma, was Eternity as absolute stasis. This meant a rigid, unchanging state where nothing could be lost because nothing could ever be gained or altered. This philosophy stifled innovation, personal growth, and the natural progression of society.

The Vision Hunt Decree was the embodiment of Ei's stasis-driven Eternity. It aimed to freeze Inazuma in time, preventing any deviation from a perceived ideal state. This approach, while intended to prevent loss, ultimately led to stagnation and oppression. The Traveler's intervention and the subsequent confrontation in the Plane of Euthymia directly challenged this rigid ideology. The Traveler, representing the force of change and the interconnectedness of Teyvat, forced Ei to confront the destructive consequences of her static vision and the pain it inflicted. This confrontation was crucial in initiating Ei's slow journey towards understanding the limitations of her approach and the possibility of a more balanced perspective on eternity.

## Inazuma's Factions: Yashiro, Kujou, and Watatsumi

Inazuma's history is marked by the influence and interactions of its prominent clans and islands. The **Yashiro Commission** is one of the Three Commissions, historically responsible for the Grand Narukami Shrine and the spiritual well-being of Inazuma. Its members, such as the Kamisato siblings, Ayaka and Ayato, have often found themselves navigating the complex political landscape, balancing tradition with the demands of the current regime. Their role has been to maintain the spiritual heart of the nation, often acting as intermediaries between the divine and the populace.

The **Kujou Commission**, on the other hand, has historically served as the military and judicial arm of the Shogunate. Led by the Kujou family, they were instrumental in enforcing the Shogun's decrees, including the Vision Hunt Decree. Their authority was absolute within the Shogunate's framework, and they were responsible for maintaining order through force and law. Their actions, particularly during the Vision Hunt Decree, made them a symbol of the Shogunate's oppressive policies.

**Watatsumi Island**, a separate entity with its own unique culture and history, holds a distinct position. Its people worship Orobashi, a colossal serpent deity who was slain by the Electro Archon centuries ago. This event, while integral to the formation of Inazuma, also sowed seeds of resentment towards the mainland and the Shogunate. Watatsumi's isolation and its distinct religious practices set it apart from the rest of Inazuma. During the Vision Hunt Decree, Watatsumi Island became the focal point of the resistance against the Shogunate, led by Sangonomiya Kokomi, a descendant of Orobashi's lineage. Their struggle was not only against the Shogun's policies but also a fight for their autonomy and the preservation of their cultural identity, which the Shogunate's rigid ideology threatened to erase.

## The Traveler's Intervention and Ei's Awakening

The climax of the Inazuma Archon Quests involves the Traveler's direct confrontation with Ei in the Plane of Euthymia. This battle was not merely a physical struggle but a profound philosophical and emotional clash. The Traveler, having witnessed the suffering caused by the Vision Hunt Decree and the suppression of ambition, challenged Ei's rigid definition of eternity. Through their journey across Inazuma, the Traveler embodied the very essence of change and connection that Ei had sought to eradicate.

During their battle within Ei's mindscape, the Traveler forced Ei to confront the consequences of her actions and the pain she had inflicted. The Traveler's unwavering resolve and their ability to connect with others, even those who opposed them, highlighted the flaws in Ei's isolationist and static approach. This confrontation led Ei to question her own beliefs and the true meaning of eternity. It was a catalyst for her gradual shift away from absolute stasis towards a more balanced understanding of preservation that could accommodate growth and individual freedom. The Traveler's intervention was pivotal in initiating Ei's personal growth and paving the way for the eventual abolition of the Vision Hunt Decree, marking the beginning of a new era for Inazuma.
