# Furina, Focalors, and Fontaine History

The history of Fontaine is inextricably linked to the ancient prophecy foretelling the dissolution of its people into the Primordial Sea, a fate stemming from the original Archon, the Hydro Dragon Sovereign, being judged by the Heavenly Principles. This judgment led to the loss of the Dragon's authority and the subsequent rise of the Seven Archons, with the Hydro Archon, Focalors, inheriting the responsibility to rectify this original sin. The prophecy dictated that when the people of Fontaine, who had accumulated the original sin through their Archon's transgression, reached a certain threshold of wealth and technological advancement, they would all dissolve into the Primordial Sea. Focalors, recognizing the inevitability of this prophecy and the inherent injustice of her people bearing the burden of a sin not entirely their own, devised a millennia-spanning plan to break the curse.

## Focalors' Grand Design: The Separation of Consciousness

Focalors' plan was audacious and deeply personal: to split her divine consciousness into two distinct entities. One part would remain as the divine consciousness, a being of immense power and foresight, tasked with accumulating the accumulated wealth and faith of Fontaine to eventually pay the price for the original sin. This divine aspect would wait within the Veiled Sanctum, a sacred space hidden from the world, for the appointed time. The other part of her consciousness would be incarnated into a mortal vessel, a human being stripped of her divine power and memories of her true nature. This mortal vessel was named Furina. Focalors orchestrated this separation approximately 500 years before the Traveler's arrival in Fontaine, coinciding with the rise of the current Court of Fontaine and its unique societal structure.

Furina was thus born into a world where she was presented as the Hydro Archon, the embodiment of justice and the divine ruler of Fontaine. However, she possessed no divine power, no true understanding of her Archonship, and crucially, no memory of her true identity as Focalors' human vessel or the grand plan. Her existence was a meticulously crafted illusion, a performance designed to maintain the facade of a ruling Archon and to fulfill her role in Focalors' scheme. She was tasked with embodying the concept of justice, presiding over trials, and inspiring the faith of her people, all while being utterly unaware of the true nature of her existence and the prophecy she was helping to subvert.

## The Psychological Toll on Furina

The five centuries of Furina's "reign" were a profound psychological ordeal. Living as the Hydro Archon, the symbol of justice and divine authority, without any actual divine power or true understanding of her responsibilities, placed an immense burden on her. She was constantly under scrutiny, expected to embody ideals she could not fully grasp, and perpetually performing a role she did not understand. This prolonged deception, coupled with the isolation of her true nature, led to significant psychological distress. She developed a theatrical persona, often exaggerated and dramatic, as a coping mechanism and a way to project an image of confidence and authority she did not internally possess. The weight of her perceived failures, the constant fear of being exposed as a fraud, and the loneliness of her unique existence contributed to deep-seated insecurities and anxieties that defined her character for centuries. Her vibrant, often over-the-top personality was a shield, a carefully constructed performance to hide the profound emptiness and confusion within.

## The Court of Fontaine: Justice as Theater

The Court of Fontaine's justice system, renowned throughout Teyvat, was a direct manifestation of Furina's performance and Focalors' grand design. It was structured as a grand theatrical institution, where trials were elaborate spectacles designed to uphold the illusion of divine justice. Furina, as the perceived Hydro Archon, presided over these trials, her pronouncements and judgments forming the core of the proceedings. The entire system was built upon the foundation of her illusion; the faith and belief of the Fontainian people in their Archon and their justice system were essential components of Focalors' plan to accumulate enough divine power and faith. The Melusines, a race of gentle, diligent beings with a strong sense of duty, played a crucial role as administrative staff and investigators within the judicial system, their unwavering dedication contributing to the smooth functioning of the courts and reinforcing the perception of Fontainian justice. They were integral to the daily operations, ensuring that the machinery of justice, however illusory its ultimate purpose, continued to run.

## The Breaking of the Prophecy: Sacrifice and Tears

The prophecy was ultimately broken through a complex interplay of sacrifice and the unique nature of the Hydro Dragon Sovereign, Neuvillette. Focalors' plan culminated in a ritual that required the accumulated wealth and faith of Fontaine to be converted into a divine power capable of severing the original sin. This power was to be offered to the Primordial Sea. Focalors, having separated her consciousness, knew that her divine self would have to be sacrificed to complete the ritual. She orchestrated a scenario where the accumulated "original sin" of Fontaine, which had been accumulating for centuries, would be judged and purged.

The key to breaking the curse lay in the tears of the Hydro Dragon Sovereign. Neuvillette, as the current Hydro Dragon Sovereign and the embodiment of the original Hydro Archon's authority, possessed a unique connection to the Primordial Sea and the concept of judgment. Focalors, in her divine form, confronted Neuvillette, and through a series of events, managed to provoke him into shedding tears. These tears, imbued with the immense power and sorrow of a Dragon Sovereign, were the catalyst. Focalors then used her own divine essence, along with the accumulated faith and wealth of Fontaine, to absorb the original sin and then, in an act of ultimate sacrifice, dissolved herself into the Primordial Sea, taking the sin with her. This act purified the people of Fontaine, severing their connection to the original sin and thus nullifying the prophecy.

## Furina's Choice: A New Beginning

With Focalors' sacrifice, the prophecy was broken, and the threat of dissolution was lifted from Fontaine. However, this also meant the end of Furina's role as the Hydro Archon. Her five centuries of performance were over, and the illusion she had lived within finally dissolved. Freed from the burden of her false Archonship, Furina was finally able to live as an ordinary human. She chose to embrace this newfound freedom, shedding the persona of the divine and seeking a life of simple joys and experiences. Despite the immense psychological trauma she endured, Furina demonstrated remarkable resilience and a desire to live authentically. Her journey from a performance of divine authority to a humble, ordinary existence marked the true culmination of Focalors' plan, allowing the people of Fontaine to be free and Furina herself to finally find peace and self-acceptance. She chose to continue living, to experience the world as a mortal, and to find meaning in a life no longer dictated by a grand, deceptive plan.
