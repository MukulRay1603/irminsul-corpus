# The Traveler and Their Sibling

The Traveler, known as the "Outlander" or "Wanderer," and their twin sibling are the central protagonists of Genshin Impact's narrative. They originate from a world beyond Teyvat, a place of advanced technology and interdimensional travel. Their journey began when they attempted to depart Teyvat, only to be intercepted by a divine entity referred to as the "Sustainer of Heavenly Principles." This encounter resulted in the separation of the twins, with the Traveler being sealed and eventually awakening centuries later in a Teyvat vastly changed from the one they first encountered. Their sibling, however, was taken by the Sustainer, initiating the Traveler's primary quest: to reunite with them and uncover the truth behind their separation and the world's history.

The Traveler's sibling, after being separated, endured a different fate. Witnessing the cataclysm that befell Teyvat and the destruction of Khaenri'ah, a technologically advanced nation that defied the divine, profoundly impacted them. This experience, coupled with the trauma of separation and the apparent indifference of the divine powers to the suffering of mortals, led the sibling to embrace a path of rebellion. They became a prominent figure within the Abyss Order, an organization dedicated to overthrowing the current celestial order and avenging the injustices inflicted upon Teyvat and its inhabitants, particularly Khaenri'ah. The sibling's transformation is marked by a shift from a hopeful, perhaps naive, individual to a determined, albeit morally ambiguous, leader. Their actions, while driven by a desire for justice and liberation from divine tyranny, often involve methods that are destructive and cause further suffering, creating a deep emotional and ideological chasm between them and the Traveler.

The relationship between the Traveler and their sibling is characterized by a complex mix of love, longing, and ideological conflict. Throughout the Traveler's journey across Teyvat, they repeatedly encounter their sibling, often in moments of confrontation or manipulation. The sibling frequently appears to the Traveler, sometimes offering cryptic advice or warnings, other times actively working against the Traveler's efforts to maintain the status quo. This dynamic creates significant emotional tension, as the Traveler grapples with the desire to rescue their sibling and the growing realization that their sibling has embraced a cause that clashes with the Traveler's own evolving understanding of Teyvat and its people. The sibling's motivations are presented as stemming from a deep-seated anger and a belief that the current divine order is inherently corrupt and must be dismantled. They see the Traveler's attempts to forge alliances with the ruling powers of Teyvat as naive and ultimately futile, a betrayal of the suffering they have both witnessed.

The sibling's goals are ostensibly to liberate Teyvat from the "tyranny of the heavens" and to restore what was lost, particularly concerning Khaenri'ah. They believe that the Archons and the divine powers are complicit in the suffering of mortals and that true freedom can only be achieved through a complete overthrow of the existing celestial hierarchy. Their methods, however, are often extreme. The Abyss Order, under their influence, engages in acts of sabotage, manipulation, and direct conflict with the nations and their rulers. This raises a critical question about the justification of their cause: is a noble goal achievable through morally reprehensible means? The Traveler, while understanding their sibling's pain and anger, is often forced to oppose their actions, creating a tragic conflict where familial love is pitted against the pursuit of justice and the well-being of Teyvat.
