# The Cataclysm Five Hundred Years Ago

The Cataclysm, a series of catastrophic events that transpired approximately 500 years prior to the current game timeline, represents the most significant turning point in Teyvat's recorded history. This period marks the destruction of the technologically advanced, godless nation of Khaenri'ah, the death of the original Dendro Archon, and a profound reshaping of the political and spiritual landscape of the seven nations, directly influencing the motivations and circumstances of numerous key characters and factions. The repercussions of this event continue to ripple through Teyvat, serving as the central mystery and driving force behind the Traveler's journey.

## The Destruction of Khaenri'ah and the Eclipse King Incident

Khaenri'ah, a nation that existed entirely beneath the surface of Teyvat, was renowned for its advanced civilization, eschewing the worship of gods and instead relying on human ingenuity and alchemy. Their mastery of technology allowed them to create complex automatons, such as the Hilichurls and Abyss Order's various creatures, and even achieve a form of immortality for their people. However, this pursuit of knowledge and power ultimately led to their downfall. The "Eclipse King," a figure whose true identity and actions remain shrouded in mystery, is cited as the catalyst for Khaenri'ah's destruction. It is believed that his ambition or a specific forbidden act committed by Khaenri'ah, possibly related to challenging the divine order or delving into forbidden knowledge, provoked the wrath of the divine powers, specifically Celestia, the celestial authority residing above Teyvat. In response, Celestia, wielding immense power, unleashed a devastating force upon Khaenri'ah. This destruction was not merely military; it was an act of divine judgment, erasing the nation from the face of Teyvat and transforming its inhabitants into cursed beings. The exact nature of the "Eclipse King incident" is deliberately vague in in-game lore, but it is consistently presented as the primary transgression that warranted such extreme divine retribution. The destruction was so absolute that Khaenri'ah was effectively wiped from existence, its ruins buried deep underground, its people scattered and cursed.

## The Fate of the Original Dendro Archon and Other Archons

The Cataclysm also claimed the life of the original Dendro Archon, the god of wisdom who ruled Sumeru prior to the current Dendro Archon, Nahida. This original Archon was killed during the conflict, a sacrifice or casualty that directly led to Nahida's ascension to the Archonship. Nahida, as a result, inherited the burden of ruling Sumeru under the shadow of this loss and the lingering trauma of the Cataclysm. The events of 500 years ago also profoundly impacted other Archons. The Electro Archon, Raiden Ei, was deeply affected by the loss of her twin sister, Makoto, the previous Electro Archon, who died during the Cataclysm. This trauma led Ei to pursue an ideal of "Eternity" for Inazuma, seeking to prevent any further change or loss by freezing her nation in time. The Geo Archon, Morax (Zhongli), also played a significant role in the subsequent conflicts, particularly in Liyue, and the Cataclysm forced him to confront the limitations of divine power and the necessity of preparing his nation for a future without direct Archon intervention, culminating in his abdication. The trauma and the perceived failure to protect their nations or their fellow Archons left indelible marks on the surviving deities, shaping their ruling philosophies and their interactions with the mortal world.

## The Creation of the Abyss Order and Dainsleif's Curse

The survivors of Khaenri'ah, cursed by Celestia, became the progenitors of the Abyss Order. Stripped of their homeland and their divine favor, they were transformed into beings of immense power and bitterness, driven by a desire to reclaim what was lost or to exact revenge upon the divine powers that destroyed them. The Abyss Order, as encountered by the Traveler, is the direct descendant of these Khaenri'an survivors, their motivations rooted in the trauma and injustice of the Cataclysm. Dainsleif, the "Twilight Sword," was a loyal Khaenri'an guard who witnessed the destruction of his homeland firsthand. He was cursed with immortality, forced to wander Teyvat for eternity, witnessing the rise and fall of civilizations and the enduring consequences of the Cataclysm. His curse is a direct result of his involvement in the events of 500 years ago, a punishment or a consequence of his survival and his intimate knowledge of Khaenri'ah's fate. Dainsleif carries the immense burden of his people's destruction and his own eternal suffering, making him a pivotal figure in understanding the true nature of the Cataclysm and the forces at play in Teyvat. He actively seeks to understand and potentially undo the curse placed upon his people and himself, often clashing with both the Abyss Order and the divine powers.

## The Yakshas and the Demon Tide in Liyue

Following the destruction of Khaenri'ah, a wave of corrupted energy and monstrous entities, often referred to as the "demon tide," flooded Liyue. This surge of malevolent forces was a direct consequence of the Cataclysm, a chaotic fallout from the divine judgment upon Khaenri'ah. To combat this threat and protect Liyue, the Geo Archon, Morax, summoned the Yakshas, powerful warrior deities tasked with purging the land of these demonic entities. The Yakshas, led by the formidable Xiao, fought a brutal and protracted war against the demon tide for centuries. This conflict took a heavy toll on the Yakshas, both physically and mentally. The constant exposure to the corrupting influence of the demons, coupled with the immense violence they endured, led to their eventual demise or their descent into madness. Xiao is the sole surviving Yaksha, forever bearing the scars of this war and the guilt of his brethren's fates, a constant reminder of the devastating impact of the Cataclysm on Liyue. The remnants of this conflict can still be seen in Liyue, with corrupted areas and lingering malevolent energies.

## Fontaine's Prophecy and the Cataclysm's Significance

The Cataclysm of 500 years ago is intrinsically linked to the prophecy of Fontaine, the nation of justice. The prophecy foretells that the Hydro Archon and the people of Fontaine will eventually be judged and dissolved by water, a fate that has been looming over them for centuries. This prophecy is not an isolated event but is presented as a consequence of the divine order established after the Cataclysm. The divine judgment that befell Khaenri'ah set a precedent, and the subsequent actions and decisions of Celestia and the Archons have created a system where such judgments can be enacted. Fontaine's prophecy represents a potential future reckoning, a consequence of the original sin of Khaenri'ah and the subsequent actions of the divine. The Cataclysm is the pivotal event because it fundamentally altered the balance of power, the understanding of the divine, and the destiny of Teyvat. It exposed the terrifying power of Celestia, the vulnerability of even technologically advanced nations, and the deep-seated resentments that would fester for centuries. The Traveler's quest to find their lost sibling and understand the truth of Teyvat is inextricably linked to unraveling the mysteries of the Cataclysm, as it is the root cause of many of the world's current conflicts, character motivations, and impending doom. The event serves as the foundational trauma upon which much of Genshin Impact's narrative is built, explaining the existence of the Abyss Order, the Archons' current philosophies, and the underlying tension between humanity and the divine.
