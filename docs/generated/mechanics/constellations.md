# Constellation System

The Constellation system in Genshin Impact is a progression mechanic that enhances characters by unlocking powerful passive abilities and stat bonuses through duplicate character acquisition. Each character has six Constellation levels, denoted as C1 through C6, with each level requiring one duplicate of that specific character to unlock. Acquiring a duplicate character through Wishes (gacha pulls) automatically grants a "Stardust" item corresponding to the character's rarity. For 4-star characters, this is a Masterless Stardust, and for 5-star characters, it's a Masterless Stardust. These Stardust items can then be used to purchase specific characters or weapons from Paimon's Bargains, but the primary method of unlocking Constellations is through direct acquisition via Wishes.

The power spike from Constellations often follows a general pattern, though significant variations exist. Typically, C1 and C2 provide substantial quality-of-life improvements or moderate damage increases, making them highly desirable and often the most cost-effective Constellations to aim for. C3 and C5 usually increase the level cap of a character's Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst, respectively, leading to direct multiplier increases. C4 can offer a variety of effects, from stat boosts to unique mechanics. C6 is frequently a transformative Constellation, offering a significant overhaul to a character's kit, sometimes drastically altering their playstyle or unlocking new damage ceilings. However, some C6 Constellations can be detrimental or create undesirable side effects if not managed carefully.

Several notable Constellations significantly alter a character's playstyle or amplify their core mechanics:

*   **Bennett C6:** This Constellation infuses the active character's Normal, Charged, and Plunging Attacks with Pyro when affected by Bennett's Burst. While this can dramatically increase damage for characters who can benefit from Pyro infusion (e.g., Diluc, Keqing), it can also be detrimental for characters who rely on other Elemental infusions or reactions, such as a Freeze team or a Physical DPS. The Pyro infusion overrides other elemental infusions and can interfere with certain reaction setups.
*   **Kaedehara Kazuha C2:** This Constellation grants Kazuha an additional 200 Elemental Mastery when his Elemental Skill or Burst hits an opponent. Furthermore, allies within the Burst's AoE gain 200 Elemental Mastery. This significantly boosts Kazuha's own Anemo damage and Swirl damage, while also providing a substantial EM buff to the entire team, making him an even more potent support for reaction-based teams.
*   **Xingqiu C2:** Xingqiu's C2 increases the duration of his Elemental Burst, "Guhua Sword: Raincutter," by 3 seconds and reduces the cooldown of his Elemental Skill, "Guhua Sword: Fatal Rainscreen," by 20%. This extended Burst duration allows for more consistent Hydro application and damage, and the Skill cooldown reduction ensures better uptime for his damage reduction and minor healing effects.
*   **Fischl C6:** Fischl's C6, "Evernight Raven," allows Oz to attack alongside the active character. When Oz is active, Fischl's Normal Attacks trigger an additional lightning strike that deals 30% of Fischl's ATK as Electro DMG. This significantly enhances Fischl's off-field damage and Electro application, turning her into a more potent damage dealer and Electro enabler.
*   **Xiangling C4:** Xiangling's C4 extends the duration of her Elemental Burst, "Pyronado," by 4 seconds. This provides a substantial increase in Pyro application and damage over time, making her Pyronado a much more reliable and potent source of damage and elemental reactions.
*   **Raiden Shogun C2:** Raiden Shogun's C2 significantly increases the DMG dealt by her Elemental Burst, "Secret Art: Musou Shinsetsu." During the Burst's initial state, her attacks ignore 60% of opponents' DEF. This is a massive damage increase, especially against high-DEF enemies, and is often considered one of the most impactful C2s in the game for a damage dealer.
*   **Hu Tao C1:** Hu Tao's C1 allows her Charged Attacks to not consume Stamina during the duration of her Elemental Skill, "Guide to Afterlife." This is a crucial quality-of-life improvement that allows Hu Tao to unleash a barrage of Charged Attacks without worrying about stamina management, significantly increasing her damage output and fluidity in combat.

Evaluating the value of Constellations versus pulling for new characters is a complex decision that depends heavily on a player's account, goals, and available resources (Primogems, Intertwined Fates, and real-world currency).

**Constellation Value Assessment:**

*   **Targeted Constellations:** If a player has a favorite character or a core character for a specific team composition, investing in their Constellations can be highly rewarding. For example, unlocking Kazuha's C2 can transform him into an even more potent support, while Hu Tao's C1 dramatically improves her personal damage ceiling.
*   **Quality of Life vs. Damage:** Constellations that offer significant quality-of-life improvements (like Hu Tao C1, Xingqiu C2) are often more valuable than raw damage increases, especially for characters who already perform well. These Constellations make the character more enjoyable and easier to play.
*   **Transformative Constellations:** C6 Constellations that fundamentally change a character's role or unlock new playstyles can be game-changing. However, these are typically the most expensive to acquire and may require significant investment.
*   **Diminishing Returns:** For many characters, the first two Constellations (C1 and C2) offer the most significant power spikes relative to their cost. Subsequent Constellations might provide incremental gains, and the value proposition decreases as the cost increases.
*   **Team Synergy:** The value of a Constellation is also dependent on the team it's used in. Bennett C6, for instance, is a massive damage boost for some teams but a hindrance for others.

**Pulling for New Characters:**

*   **Account Diversity:** Acquiring new characters expands a player's roster, allowing for more team compositions, better performance in the Spiral Abyss, and greater flexibility in tackling different content.
*   **Meta Relevance:** New characters often introduce novel mechanics or fill gaps in existing team archetypes, potentially becoming meta-defining.
*   **Resource Allocation:** Pulling on limited character banners is the primary way to acquire new 5-star characters. Players must decide if a new character's potential impact on their account is greater than the benefits of investing in Constellations for existing characters.
*   **"Pulling on Rate-Up":** When aiming for specific Constellations, players are often pulling on banners featuring the character they want. This means they might acquire other featured 4-star characters or even a new 5-star character they weren't initially targeting, which can be a positive or negative outcome depending on the player's goals.

**Strategic Considerations:**

*   **"Sparking" and Pity System:** Genshin Impact's Wish system has a pity mechanic, guaranteeing a 5-star character within 90 pulls on the Character Event Wish and 80 pulls on the Standard Wish. A "spark" system, where players can choose a specific 5-star character after a certain number of pulls on a Character Event Wish banner, is not present. This makes acquiring specific 5-star Constellations a matter of luck and sustained resource management.
*   **F2P vs. Whales:** For Free-to-Play (F2P) players, acquiring even one copy of a desired 5-star character is a significant achievement. Investing in Constellations for 5-star characters is generally not feasible. F2P players often focus on acquiring strong 4-star characters and their early Constellations (C1, C2, C4) which are more attainable. For "Whales" (heavy spenders), acquiring C6 5-star characters is a common goal, unlocking their full potential.
*   **Character Archetypes:** Some characters are designed to be powerful at C0 (e.g., many early 5-star DPS units), while others scale exceptionally well with Constellations (e.g., Kazuha, Raiden Shogun). Understanding a character's Constellation scaling is crucial for making informed decisions.

Ultimately, the decision to invest in Constellations or pull for new characters is a personal one. Players should assess their current roster, their financial resources, and their long-term goals within the game to make the most strategic choices. Focusing on C1-C2 for key characters often provides a significant power boost without requiring the extreme investment of C6.
