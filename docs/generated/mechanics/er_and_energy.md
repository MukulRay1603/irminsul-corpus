# Energy Recharge and Energy System

The energy system in Genshin Impact is a fundamental mechanic that governs the use of Elemental Bursts, the most impactful abilities characters possess. Understanding how to generate, collect, and manage Elemental Particles is crucial for optimizing team damage and utility, often serving as the primary build bottleneck for many characters, especially supports.

## Elemental Particle Generation

Elemental Particles are small orbs of elemental energy that, when collected by an active character, restore a set amount of Energy to that character's Elemental Burst. There are two primary methods of generating Elemental Particles:

### On-Hit Generation

Many character abilities, particularly those that deal damage or apply elemental effects, generate Elemental Particles upon hitting an enemy. The number and type of particles generated depend on the specific ability. For example, Xiangling's Pyronado generates 2 Pyro particles every 2 seconds once it starts dealing damage. Fischl's Oz, when attacking, generates 1 Electro particle every 1.5 seconds. These particles are generated continuously as long as the ability is active and hitting enemies. The rate of generation is fixed for each ability and is not affected by Energy Recharge.

### On-Kill Generation

Certain abilities or passive talents can generate Elemental Particles upon defeating an enemy. This is less common for direct particle generation and more often tied to specific weapon passives or character effects. For instance, the Favonius weapons (Favonius Sword, Favonius Greatsword, etc.) have a chance to generate an Elemental Particle when the equipping character scores a CRIT hit. While not strictly "on-kill," the CRIT trigger often happens during combat where enemies are being defeated.

## Elemental Particle Collection

Once generated, Elemental Particles are released from the enemy or the source of the ability. An active character on the field can collect these particles. The amount of Energy restored depends on the particle's element and the character's Energy Recharge stat.

### On-Field vs. Off-Field Collection

A critical mechanic is how particles are collected based on whether the character is on-field or off-field.

*   **On-Field Collection:** When the currently active character collects an Elemental Particle, they receive 100% of its Energy value.
*   **Off-Field Collection:** When a character who is *not* currently on the field collects an Elemental Particle, they receive only 60% of its Energy value. This penalty significantly impacts team energy economy, making it essential to have the correct character on the field when particles are generated or to build higher Energy Recharge on off-field characters to compensate.

### Elemental Resonance for Particle Collection

Elemental Resonance plays a role in particle collection, particularly for characters of the same element. When two characters of the same element are in the party, they gain an Elemental Resonance bonus. For example, the Pyro Resonance (Fervent Flames) increases ATK by 25%. While not directly affecting particle *generation*, it influences the overall team's combat effectiveness, indirectly aiding in faster enemy defeat and thus more opportunities for particle generation. More importantly, when two characters of the same element are on the field and collect particles of that element, the energy distribution is more straightforward. However, the 60% penalty for off-field collection remains a universal rule.

## Energy Recharge (ER) Stat

Energy Recharge is a character stat that increases the amount of Energy restored when collecting Elemental Particles. The formula for Energy restored is:

`Base Energy Restored = (Base Energy from Particle) * (1 + Energy Recharge %)`

For example, a standard Elemental Particle restores 3 Energy. If a character has 100% Energy Recharge, they will gain `3 * (1 + 1.00) = 6` Energy from that particle. If they have 200% ER, they gain `3 * (1 + 2.00) = 9` Energy.

### ER as a Build Bottleneck

Energy Recharge is frequently the most challenging stat to balance in a character build, especially for characters whose Elemental Bursts are central to their kit or their team's performance. This is because:

1.  **High Burst Costs:** Many powerful Elemental Bursts have high Energy costs, ranging from 60 to 80 Energy. To consistently use these Bursts every rotation, characters need a significant amount of ER.
2.  **Particle Generation Limits:** The number of particles generated by abilities is finite and often not enough to fully recharge a high-cost Burst without substantial ER investment.
3.  **Off-Field Penalty:** The 60% collection penalty for off-field characters means that supports who primarily operate off-field often require significantly higher ER than on-field DPS characters to maintain their Burst uptime.
4.  **Team Dependency:** A character's ER needs are also influenced by the particle generation of their teammates. If a team has poor particle generation, individual characters will need more ER.

### ER Thresholds for Key Supports

The following are common ER requirements for specific support characters to ensure consistent Elemental Burst uptime in typical team compositions. These values assume a standard rotation where the Burst is used at the start of a new rotation and needs to be ready by the start of the *next* rotation. These can vary based on team composition, enemy types, and weapon choices.

*   **Xiangling:** 180-200% ER. Her Pyronado has an 80-cost Burst and is a primary damage source. She relies heavily on off-field Pyro particle generation from teammates (like Bennett) or her own Guoba, making her ER needs high.
*   **Bennett:** 170-190% ER. His Burst provides a massive ATK buff and healing, crucial for many teams. He also generates Pyro particles, but his own Burst cost (60) and the need for consistent uptime necessitate high ER.
*   **Xingqiu:** 180-200% ER. His Rain Swords provide damage reduction, interruption resistance, and Hydro application. His 80-cost Burst is essential for enabling Vaporize and Freeze reactions, requiring significant ER.
*   **Raiden Shogun:** 250-300% ER. While Raiden's own kit benefits from ER (her Burst damage scales with it, and her passive reduces team ER needs), her personal ER requirements are very high to ensure her own 90-cost Burst is always available. The higher end of this range is for teams where she is the primary on-field damage dealer and needs to trigger her Burst every rotation.
*   **Nahida:** 120-130% ER. Her Elemental Burst has a 50-cost, and she generates Dendro particles. While not as demanding as 80-cost Bursts, she often needs to be on-field for her Tri-Karma Purification to maximize damage and buffs, so consistent uptime is desired.
*   **Fischl:** 120% ER. Oz generates Electro particles consistently. Her 60-cost Burst is primarily for repositioning Oz or activating her A4 passive. With good particle generation from teammates or her own Oz, this ER is usually sufficient.
*   **Kazuha:** 140-160% ER. His 60-cost Burst provides crowd control and elemental damage buffs. The higher end of the range is for teams where he is the sole Anemo support and needs to ensure his Burst is available every rotation, especially if he is not generating many particles himself or is frequently swapped out.

## Weapon Interactions

Certain weapons significantly impact ER requirements and particle generation.

### Sacrificial Weapons

Weapons like the Sacrificial Sword, Sacrificial Greatsword, Sacrificial Bow, and Sacrificial Fragments have a passive ability that, upon dealing damage to an opponent with an Elemental Skill, has a chance to reset the cooldown of that Elemental Skill. This is incredibly powerful because:

1.  **Double Skill Usage:** It allows for two uses of an Elemental Skill in quick succession.
2.  **Increased Particle Generation:** If the Elemental Skill generates particles, using it twice means generating particles twice, effectively doubling the potential particle generation from that skill within a shorter timeframe.
3.  **Reduced ER Needs:** By generating more particles from skills, characters can often reduce their reliance on the ER stat, allowing for more offensive stats on artifacts. For example, Xingqiu with Sacrificial Sword often needs less ER than without it, as his skill generates Hydro particles.

### Favonius Weapons

Weapons like the Favonius Sword, Favonius Greatsword, Favonius Lance, Favonius Warbow, and Favonius Codex have a passive that grants a chance to generate a few Elemental Particles when the equipping character scores a CRIT hit.

*   **Particle Generation:** These particles are generated independently of the character's skills or bursts. They are "pure" particles, meaning they can be collected by any character on the team, regardless of their element.
*   **Team-Wide Benefit:** This makes Favonius weapons excellent for team energy needs, especially for characters who don't generate many particles themselves or for teams with diverse elemental compositions.
*   **CRIT Requirement:** The passive requires the wielder to score CRIT hits. Therefore, characters equipping Favonius weapons generally need a minimum CRIT Rate (often around 50-60%) to reliably trigger the particle generation.
*   **ER Reduction:** Equipping a Favonius weapon can significantly reduce the ER requirements of the wielder and potentially other team members, freeing up artifact stats. For example, a Bennett with a Favonius Sword can often run less ER and focus more on HP and healing bonus.

In conclusion, the energy system is a complex interplay of particle generation, collection mechanics, and character stats. Energy Recharge is a vital stat that directly impacts Burst uptime, and balancing it is often the most significant challenge in character building. Understanding the nuances of on-field vs. off-field collection, the impact of weapon passives, and the specific ER needs of key characters is essential for mastering team rotations and maximizing combat effectiveness in Genshin Impact.
