# Internal Cooldown (ICD)

Internal Cooldown (ICD) is a fundamental game mechanic in Genshin Impact that governs how frequently an Elemental application from a character's abilities can trigger Elemental Reactions. Most character abilities that apply Elements have an ICD, preventing them from triggering reactions on every single instance of application. Understanding ICD is crucial for optimizing team compositions, particularly for Elemental Reactions like Vaporize and Melt, which rely on rapid and consistent Elemental application.

## The Standard ICD Rule: 2.5 Seconds / 3 Hits

The most common ICD rule applied to character abilities is a **2.5-second cooldown** or a **3-hit limit**, whichever comes first. This means that an ability can apply an Element and trigger a reaction up to three times within a 2.5-second window. After the third application or after 2.5 seconds have passed since the first application, the ICD resets, and the ability can once again trigger reactions. This rule applies to the majority of Normal Attacks, Elemental Skills, and Elemental Bursts that have inherent Elemental application.

For instance, a character's Normal Attack might apply Pyro on hits 1, 2, and 3 within 2.5 seconds. If the attack sequence is slower, and 2.5 seconds pass between the first and second hit, the ICD will reset after the second hit, allowing it to trigger reactions again. Conversely, if the attacks are very fast, the 3-hit limit will be reached before the 2.5-second timer expires, and the ICD will reset after the third hit.

It is important to note that this rule applies to the *ability's* Elemental application, not necessarily to every individual damage instance. Some abilities have multiple damage instances that share a single ICD. For example, a single press of an Elemental Skill might deal damage twice, but both instances are governed by the same ICD.

## Abilities with No ICD

Certain abilities in Genshin Impact are designated as having "no ICD." This means they can trigger Elemental Reactions with every instance of Elemental application, without being subject to the 2.5-second or 3-hit limit. These abilities are exceptionally powerful for enabling consistent reactions and are often central to meta-defining team compositions.

Examples of abilities with no ICD include:

*   **Xingqiu's Elemental Skill (Gu hua: Fatal Flow)** and **Elemental Burst (Gu hua: Raincutter)**: The Rain Swords summoned by Xingqiu's abilities apply Hydro to the active character's attacks. Each sword strike applies Hydro without ICD, allowing for rapid Hydro application. This is the cornerstone of many Vaporize teams.
*   **Fischl's Elemental Skill (Shadow Spitfire)** and **Elemental Burst (Midnight Phantasmagoria)**: Oz, summoned by Fischl's Skill, applies Electro to enemies with his attacks. While Oz's initial summon has ICD, his subsequent attacks and the Electro application from Fischl's Burst (when she attacks as Oz) do not. This makes Fischl an excellent Electro enabler for reactions like Overload and Electro-Charged.
*   **Xiangling's Elemental Burst (Pyronado)**: The Pyronado, when active, applies Pyro to enemies in its rotating path. Each rotation of the Pyronado applies Pyro without ICD, making it an incredibly potent Pyro enabler for Vaporize and Melt.
*   **Bennett's Elemental Burst (Fantastic Voyage)**: While Bennett's Burst itself doesn't apply an element, the characters within its radius receive an Elemental infusion. However, the Pyro application from Bennett's *Normal Attacks* when infused by his own Burst *does* have ICD. The key is that the *infusion itself* is not subject to ICD, allowing for consistent elemental application from the active character.
*   **Klee's Elemental Skill (Pummelle)** and **Elemental Burst (Sparks 'n' Splash)**: Klee's Skill summons a Jumpty Dumpty that deals AoE Pyro DMG and applies Pyro. The initial summon and subsequent explosions have ICD. However, her Burst, Sparks 'n' Splash, has multiple Pyro explosions that can trigger reactions without ICD, making it a strong enabler.
*   **Hu Tao's Elemental Skill (Guide to Afterlife)**: While Hu Tao's Skill infuses her attacks with Pyro, the Pyro application from these infused attacks *does* have ICD. However, the initial Pyro DMG from the Skill itself and the subsequent Paramita Papilio state allow for consistent Pyro application.

## ICD's Impact on Reaction Proc Rates

The presence or absence of ICD significantly dictates how effectively a character can enable Elemental Reactions.

*   **Xingqiu Rain Swords**: With no ICD, each of Xingqiu's Rain Sword slashes applies Hydro. When paired with a character performing rapid attacks (like Hu Tao's charged attacks or Ayaka's Normal Attacks), these Hydro applications can trigger Vaporize or Freeze on nearly every hit, provided the attacking character applies their element fast enough. This makes Xingqiu indispensable for many top-tier Vaporize and Freeze teams. For example, Hu Tao's charged attacks, when infused with Pyro by her Skill, can trigger Vaporize on multiple hits from Xingqiu's swords, leading to massive damage.
*   **Fischl's Oz**: Oz's attacks, when they apply Electro, can trigger reactions consistently. In an Electro-Charged team, Oz's rapid Electro application can continuously trigger the reaction with Hydro applied by another character, dealing consistent Electro-Charged damage. For Overload teams, Oz's Electro application can trigger Overload with Pyro from characters like Xiangling or Bennett.
*   **Ganyu's Charged Attacks**: Ganyu's Frostflake Arrow Bloom, the secondary AoE damage from her Charged Attack (Level 2), applies Cryo. This Bloom has no ICD. This allows Ganyu to consistently trigger Melt with Pyro applied by characters like Xiangling or Bennett, or trigger Freeze with Hydro applied by characters like Mona or Kokomi. The lack of ICD on the Bloom is what makes Ganyu such a potent Melt and Freeze enabler.

## ICD and Vaporize/Melt Teams

Vaporize (Hydro + Pyro) and Melt (Cryo + Pyro) are two of the most powerful Elemental Reactions in Genshin Impact due to their significant damage multipliers. The effectiveness of these reactions hinges on the ability to apply the correct element (Pyro for Vaporize, Cryo for Melt) at the right time to an enemy already affected by the other element.

*   **Vaporize Teams**: To maximize Vaporize damage, a character needs to apply Pyro to an enemy that is consistently being hit with Hydro. Characters like Xingqiu and Yelan, with their no-ICD Hydro application, are ideal for this. They can apply Hydro to almost every hit of the active Pyro DPS character. Conversely, if the Pyro character is the primary enabler (e.g., Hu Tao), their Pyro application must be fast enough to overcome the ICD of their own attacks and trigger Vaporize with the Hydro applied by a support.
*   **Melt Teams**: Similar to Vaporize, Melt teams benefit greatly from consistent elemental application. Characters like Ganyu, with her no-ICD Frostflake Arrow Bloom, are excellent for triggering Melt. Alternatively, a Pyro character like Xiangling can apply Pyro consistently with her Pyronado, allowing Cryo characters to trigger Melt. The challenge in Melt teams is often ensuring the correct element is applied first and that the subsequent application is fast enough to trigger the reaction before the ICD resets. For example, if a Cryo character applies Cryo, and then a Pyro character attacks, the Pyro application must occur within the 2.5s/3-hit window of the Cryo application to trigger Melt.

## Working Around ICD in Team Construction

Effectively working around ICD involves understanding which abilities have it and which don't, and then coordinating their application.

1.  **Leveraging No-ICD Abilities**: The most straightforward way to bypass ICD is to build teams around characters with no-ICD Elemental application. Xingqiu, Yelan, Fischl, Xiangling, and Ganyu are prime examples. Their abilities can provide a consistent elemental aura for the active character to react with.
2.  **Fast Attack Sequences**: For characters with standard ICD, using abilities that allow for fast attack sequences can maximize reaction procs within the 2.5-second window. This includes Normal Attack combos, rapid Normal/Charged Attacks (like Hu Tao's), or rapid Elemental Skill activations.
3.  **Elemental Application Order**: The order of Elemental application matters. For Vaporize, it's generally more efficient for the Hydro applicator to have no ICD and apply Hydro to the Pyro DPS's attacks. For Melt, it can be beneficial for the Cryo applicator to have no ICD (like Ganyu's Bloom) or for the Pyro applicator to have no ICD (like Xiangling's Pyronado) to consistently trigger Melt.
4.  **Coordinated Elemental Bursts**: Some Elemental Bursts have extended durations and consistent application that can be timed with other abilities. For example, Xiangling's Pyronado can provide a steady stream of Pyro for a Hydro character to react with.
5.  **Enemy Aura Management**: Enemies can only hold one elemental aura at a time. If an enemy is affected by Hydro, and a character applies Pyro, the reaction occurs. If the Pyro application is too slow or infrequent, the Hydro aura may dissipate before the Pyro can react, or the Pyro application might simply overwrite the Hydro aura without triggering Vaporize. Understanding how quickly auras decay and how frequently an ability applies its element is key.

## Characters with Unusual ICD Patterns

While the 2.5s/3-hit rule is common, some characters deviate from this standard, possessing unique ICD mechanics that can significantly impact their utility.

*   **Raiden Shogun (Elemental Burst - Secret Art: Musou Shinsetsu)**: Raiden Shogun's Burst infuses her attacks with Electro and grants her a special state. The initial hit of her Burst has no ICD, applying Electro. However, her subsequent infused Normal Attacks *do* have the standard 2.5s/3-hit ICD. This means she can trigger Electro-Charged or Overload on her initial Burst hit, but subsequent hits will follow the standard ICD. This makes her less ideal for consistent reaction procs compared to characters with truly no-ICD attacks, but her high Electro application rate still makes her a strong contributor.
*   **Ayaka (Elemental Burst - Senho: Flower of Life)**: Ayaka's Burst unleashes a Frostflake Seki no To that deals Cryo DMG. The initial hit of the Burst has no ICD. However, the subsequent slashes of the Frostflake Seki no To *do* have a 2.5s/3-hit ICD. This means while the initial hit can reliably trigger Melt or Freeze, subsequent hits are subject to the standard ICD.
*   **Kazuha (Elemental Skill - Chihayaburu)**: Kazuha's Skill, when holding an element, can absorb it. The initial absorption and the subsequent burst of elemental damage *do* have ICD. However, if Kazuha's Skill is used to absorb an element and then his Burst (Kazuha Slash) is used, the Burst's AoE damage *can* absorb an element and apply it without ICD. This interaction allows for consistent elemental application for Swirl reactions.
*   **Childe/Tartaglia (Melee Stance Attacks)**: When Childe enters his Melee Stance, his Hydro-infused Normal and Charged Attacks have a 2.5s/3-hit ICD. This is a critical limitation for his Vaporize potential, as he cannot consistently trigger Vaporize on every hit of his melee combo. However, his Elemental Skill's initial Hydro application does not have ICD, and his Elemental Burst applies Hydro with no ICD.
*   **Yoimiya (Elemental Skill - Niwabi Fire-Dance)**: Yoimiya's Skill infuses her Normal Attacks with Pyro. These infused Normal Attacks *do* have the standard 2.5s/3-hit ICD. This means she cannot trigger Vaporize on every single arrow, and team compositions must account for this to maximize her Vaporize damage.

Understanding these ICD mechanics is paramount for players aiming to optimize their team's damage output and achieve consistent, powerful Elemental Reactions. The interplay between ICD, ability cooldowns, attack speeds, and elemental aura mechanics forms the bedrock of high-level Genshin Impact combat strategy.
