# Exploration Mechanics

Genshin Impact's exploration mechanics are multifaceted, encouraging players to traverse its vast world by interacting with various systems that consume resources, grant rewards, or reveal hidden secrets. These mechanics are fundamental to progression, character enhancement, and understanding the game's environment.

## Stamina System

The Stamina system governs actions such as sprinting, charging attacks, swimming, and climbing. A player's Stamina bar is represented by a yellow icon and depletes with continuous use of these actions. The base Stamina pool is 100 points, and it regenerates at a rate of 20 points per second when not actively being depleted.

*   **Climbing:** Climbing consumes Stamina at a rate of 13.3 points per second. When Stamina reaches zero while climbing, the character will fall. Certain characters have abilities that affect climbing, such as Kaeya's passive, "Gliding," which reduces Stamina consumption for sprinting by 20%, or Xiao's passive, "Alatus's Attributes," which reduces climbing Stamina consumption by 20%. Food items like "Stamella" (restores 60 Stamina) and "Hearty Meal" (restores 100 Stamina) can instantly replenish Stamina. Dishes that temporarily increase maximum Stamina, such as "Adepti's Temptation" (increases Max HP by 30% and Max HP by 5000) and "Golden Shrimp Balls" (increases CRIT Rate by 10% and CRIT DMG by 20%), are also beneficial for extended exploration. The maximum Stamina can be increased by collecting Anemoculi and offering them to Statues of the Seven in Mondstadt, increasing the pool by 5 points per Anemoculus offered, up to a maximum of 10 increases (50 Stamina) before further increases are tied to the Statue's level.

*   **Swimming:** Swimming consumes Stamina at a rate of 13.3 points per second, similar to climbing. If Stamina is depleted while swimming, the character will drown and be teleported back to the nearest shore or safe area, with a small amount of HP remaining.

*   **Gliding:** Gliding consumes Stamina at a rate of 9 points per second. While gliding, Stamina regeneration is paused. Certain items and mechanics can affect gliding: Wind Gliders have different appearances but identical mechanics. The "Anemoculus" collection in Mondstadt directly contributes to increasing the Stamina cap, with each Anemoculus offered to a Mondstadt Statue of the Seven granting 5 Stamina points.

*   **Sprinting:** Sprinting consumes Stamina at a rate of 9.9 points per second. This is a primary method of fast ground traversal.

## Elemental Sight

Elemental Sight (also known as Elemental Vision) is a core mechanic that allows players to perceive the flow of elemental energy in the environment. Activating Elemental Sight highlights interactive objects, enemies, and pathways with distinct colors corresponding to their elemental affiliations.

*   **Color Coding:**
    *   **Red:** Pyro
    *   **Blue:** Hydro
    *   **Yellow:** Electro
    *   **Green:** Dendro
    *   **White:** Anemo
    *   **Purple:** Geo
    *   **Pink:** Physical/General Interactive Objects (e.g., chests, ore veins, puzzles)
    *   **Orange:** Enemies
    *   **Light Blue:** Characters

*   **Applications:** Elemental Sight is crucial for solving environmental puzzles, identifying hidden mechanisms, locating elemental oases, tracking elemental trails, and finding hidden treasures. For instance, it can reveal invisible platforms, highlight pressure plates, or show the path of a moving elemental particle. The duration of Elemental Sight is limited, and its effect fades after a period, requiring re-activation.

## Wind Currents and Updrafts

Wind Currents and Updrafts are environmental features that facilitate rapid vertical and horizontal traversal.

*   **Wind Currents:** These are streams of wind that propel characters in a specific direction, often used to cover large distances quickly. They are visually represented by swirling wind effects. Activating a Wind Current allows for faster movement than sprinting or gliding.

*   **Updrafts:** These are vertical columns of wind that launch characters into the air, allowing for extended gliding or reaching higher platforms. They are visually distinct from wind currents and are essential for navigating vertical terrain and accessing secrets. Some characters, like Jean with her Elemental Skill "Gale Blade," can create temporary updrafts.

## Ley Line Disorders

Ley Line Disorders are modifiers that affect combat encounters within Ley Line Outcrops. These are specific challenges that alter the gameplay experience and reward players with Character EXP Materials or Artifacts.

*   **Ley Line Outcrops - Blossom of Revelation (Blue):** These Ley Lines reward Character EXP Materials. They typically have a disorder that buffs or debuffs characters based on specific conditions. For example, a common disorder might be "Enemies have increased resistance to Geo DMG" or "Characters deal increased DMG when their HP is above 50%."

*   **Ley Line Outcrops - Blossom of Wealth (Gold):** These Ley Lines reward Mora. Similar to the Blossom of Revelation, they feature unique Ley Line Disorders that influence combat. An example disorder could be "When characters take DMG, their ATK is decreased by 10% for 10s. This effect can occur once every 2s."

*   **Ley Line Disorders - Abyssal (Red):** These are not Ley Line Outcrops but rather modifiers found in the Spiral Abyss. They are more complex and often involve specific elemental or damage buffs/debuffs that change with each floor and chamber.

## Treasure Compass

The Treasure Compass is a Key Item obtained through the Liyue, Mondstadt, and Inazuma regional reputation systems. It is a tool that helps locate nearby chests within a limited radius.

*   **Functionality:** When activated, the Treasure Compass emits a pulse that, if a chest is within its range, will cause the compass to vibrate and emit a sound. The closer the character is to a chest, the more frequent and intense the vibrations and sounds become. The effective range of the Treasure Compass is approximately 10-15 meters.

*   **Acquisition:**
    *   **Mondstadt Treasure Compass:** Unlocked at Reputation Level 6 in Mondstadt.
    *   **Liyue Treasure Compass:** Unlocked at Reputation Level 6 in Liyue.
    *   **Inazuma Treasure Compass:** Unlocked at Reputation Level 6 in Inazuma.
    *   **Sumeru, Fontaine, Natlan Treasure Compasses:** These regions do not have a dedicated Treasure Compass item. Instead, their exploration is guided by other means, such as the Adventurer Handbook's exploration progress tracking and specific puzzle mechanics.

## Oculi Collection and Statue Upgrades

Oculi are rare, glowing orbs scattered throughout Teyvat. Collecting them and offering them to the corresponding Statues of the Seven in each region grants significant rewards, including Adventure EXP, Primogems, Stamina increases, and Sigils.

*   **Anemoculus:** Found in Mondstadt. Offering to Mondstadt Statues of the Seven increases Max Stamina by 5 per Anemoculus, up to 10 times (50 Stamina total). Subsequent offerings grant only Sigils.
*   **Geoculus:** Found in Liyue. Offering to Liyue Statues of the Seven grants Primogems, Adventure EXP, and Liyue Sigils. Stamina is not increased.
*   **Electroculus:** Found in Inazuma. Offering to Inazuma Statues of the Seven grants Primogems, Adventure EXP, and Inazuma Sigils. Stamina is not increased.
*   **Dendroculus:** Found in Sumeru. Offering to Sumeru Statues of the Seven grants Primogems, Adventure EXP, and Sumeru Dendro Sigils. Stamina is not increased.
*   **Hydroculus:** Found in Fontaine. Offering to Fontaine Statues of the Seven grants Primogems, Adventure EXP, and Fontaine Sigils. Stamina is not increased.
*   **Phygon:** Found in Natlan. Offering to Natlan Statues of the Seven grants Primogems, Adventure EXP, and Natlan Sigils. Stamina is not increased.

Each Statue of the Seven has multiple levels, and reaching certain levels requires a specific number of Oculi. The rewards are tiered, with higher levels offering more valuable rewards. The total number of Oculi in each region is finite, making their collection a key aspect of 100% exploration.

## Reputation Systems

The Reputation System is a nation-specific progression mechanic that rewards players for completing various tasks within a region. It is unlocked after completing the "Bounties Between Mountains" Archon Quest in Mondstadt.

*   **Mondstadt:** Reputation is gained through Bounties, Wanted Banners, and Quests. Rewards include the Anemoculus Collector's Map, Wind Glider, recipes, and the Treasure-Seeking Seelie.
*   **Liyue:** Reputation is gained through Bounties, Wanted Banners, and Quests. Rewards include the Liyue Treasure Compass, recipes, and the Portable Waypoint.
*   **Inazuma:** Reputation is gained through Bounties, Wanted Banners, and Quests. Rewards include the Electroculus Collector's Map, the Inazuma Treasure Compass, recipes, and the NRE (Emergency Dispenser).
*   **Sumeru:** Reputation is gained through Bounties, Wanted Banners, and Quests. Rewards include the Dendroculus Collector's Map, recipes, and the Portable Waypoint.
*   **Fontaine:** Reputation is gained through Bounties, Wanted Banners, and Quests. Rewards include the Hydroculus Collector's Map, recipes, and the Portable Waypoint.
*   **Natlan:** Reputation is gained through Bounties, Wanted Banners, and Quests. Rewards include the Phygon Collector's Map, recipes, and the Portable Waypoint.

Each region's Reputation system has 10 levels, with increasing requirements for each level. Higher reputation levels unlock more beneficial tools, recipes, and cosmetic items.

## Regional Specialty Gathering

Regional Specialties are unique flora and fauna found only in specific regions of Teyvat. They are primarily used as Ascension Materials for characters and weapons. Gathering these items is a fundamental part of character progression, especially for higher-level content.

*   **Examples:**
    *   **Mondstadt:** Dandelion Seeds, Wolfhook, Small Lamp Grass.
    *   **Liyue:** Glaze Lily, Noctilucous Jade, Silk Flower.
    *   **Inazuma:** Sakura Bloom, Sea Ganoderma, Dendrobium.
    *   **Sumeru:** Padisarah, Kalpalata Lotus, Sumeru Rose.
    *   **Fontaine:** Lumidouce Bell, Rainbow Rose, Marcipor.
    *   **Natlan:** (Specific specialties will be detailed upon game release, but will follow the pattern of region-specific flora/fauna).

These materials often respawn on a 48-hour cycle after being gathered, requiring players to plan their farming routes. Some specialties are found in difficult-to-reach locations, encouraging exploration and the use of traversal mechanics. Certain characters have passives that highlight regional specialties on the mini-map, such as Klee's "All Things Amazing" for Mondstadt specialties or Yanfei's "Case File: Cheatsheet" for Liyue specialties.
