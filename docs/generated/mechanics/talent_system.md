# Talent Leveling and Priority

Talent leveling is a fundamental progression system in Genshin Impact, directly enhancing a character's combat effectiveness by increasing the potency of their abilities. Each character possesses three distinct talent types: Normal Attack, Elemental Skill, and Elemental Burst. Understanding their functions and strategic prioritization is crucial for optimizing team performance.

## Talent Types and Their Functions

### Normal Attack (NA)
This talent governs the damage multipliers for a character's standard Normal Attacks, Charged Attacks, and Plunging Attacks. For most characters, these attacks scale with their ATK stat. Some characters, however, have unique mechanics where their Normal Attacks are infused with an element and scale off other stats or their Elemental Skill/Burst talent level (e.g., Raiden Shogun's Musou Isshin state Normal Attacks scale with her Elemental Burst talent level).

### Elemental Skill (E)
The Elemental Skill is a character's primary elemental ability, typically with a shorter cooldown than their Burst. It can generate Elemental Particles, apply elemental auras, deal damage, provide utility (e.g., shields, healing, buffs), or set up elemental reactions. The Elemental Skill talent level directly increases its damage, healing, shielding, or buffing multipliers.

### Elemental Burst (Q)
The Elemental Burst is a character's ultimate ability, requiring a significant amount of Energy to activate and typically having a longer cooldown. Bursts often deliver high damage, powerful buffs, extensive healing, or large-scale crowd control. Like the Elemental Skill, the Burst talent level directly enhances its damage, healing, or buffing capabilities.

## General Talent Prioritization by Role Archetype

Talent prioritization is highly dependent on a character's primary role within a team composition. Players should focus resources on talents that contribute most significantly to a character's intended function.

*   **On-Field Main DPS (Hypercarry):** For characters whose primary damage contribution comes from being on-field and actively attacking, Normal Attack is often a high priority, alongside their Elemental Skill or Burst if those abilities significantly amplify their on-field damage.
    *   *Example:* Hu Tao (Normal Attack and Skill), Klee (Normal Attack and Skill), Ayaka (Burst and Normal Attack).
*   **Off-Field Sub-DPS (Enabler/Damage Dealer):** Characters who deal substantial damage from off-field, often through persistent elemental effects or large Burst damage, prioritize their Elemental Skill and/or Elemental Burst. Their Normal Attack talent is typically irrelevant.
    *   *Example:* Xiangling (Burst), Xingqiu (Burst and Skill), Fischl (Skill and Burst).
*   **Support (Healer/Buffer/Shielder/Crowd Control):** For characters focused on utility rather than direct damage, their Elemental Skill and/or Elemental Burst are prioritized based on which ability provides their core support function. Normal Attack is almost always irrelevant.
    *   *Example:* Bennett (Burst), Zhongli (Skill), Diona (Skill and Burst), Kazuha (Skill and Burst).

## Talent Materials and Acquisition

Leveling talents requires specific materials, Mora, and a Crown of Insight for the final level.

1.  **Talent Books:** These are specific to regions and days of the week. Each region has three types of talent books, with two types available on Monday/Thursday, two on Tuesday/Friday, and two on Wednesday/Saturday. Sunday allows access to all book types from that region.
    *   **Mondstadt (Forsaken Rift):** "Freedom," "Resistance," "Ballad"
    *   **Liyue (Taishan Mansion):** "Prosperity," "Diligence," "Gold"
    *   **Inazuma (Violet Court):** "Transience," "Elegance," "Light"
    *   **Sumeru (Steeple of Ignorance):** "Admonition," "Ingenuity," "Praxis"
    *   **Fontaine (Artisanship Commission):** "Equity," "Justice," "Order"
    *   *Acquisition:* Farmed from specific Talent Domain challenges
