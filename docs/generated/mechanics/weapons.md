# Weapon System and Refinement

Genshin Impact features a tiered weapon system, categorized by rarity: 1-star, 2-star, 3-star, 4-star, and 5-star. Higher rarity weapons generally offer superior base stats and more impactful passive abilities. Weapons are equipped to characters and provide primary stats, secondary stats (substats), and unique passive effects.

## Weapon Tiers and Base Stats

Weapons are obtained through Wishes, event-exclusive banners, and sometimes through specific in-game quests or events.

### 5-Star Weapons
These are the rarest and most powerful weapons in the game. Their primary stat is always ATK, with the exception of a few specific weapons designed for other scaling attributes. For instance, the **Aqua Simulacra** (Sniper Rifle) provides CRIT DMG as its secondary stat, and the **Staff of Homa** (Polearm) offers HP% as its secondary stat, with its passive scaling off HP. 5-star weapons typically have a high base ATK, ranging from 608 at Level 90 for most, to 741 for the **Primordial Jade Winged-Spear**. Their passive abilities are often highly specialized, providing significant buffs to damage, energy recharge, or utility that can define a character's optimal build.

### 4-Star Weapons
These weapons are more accessible and offer a good balance of stats and utility. Their base ATK at Level 90 typically ranges from 510 to 565. Many 4-star weapons have ATK% as their secondary stat, but others offer CRIT Rate (e.g., **Festering Desire**), Energy Recharge (e.g., **Favonius Codex**), Elemental Mastery (e.g., **Dragon's Bane**), or HP% (e.g., **Black Tassel**). The passive abilities of 4-star weapons can be very strong, sometimes rivaling or even surpassing 5-star options for specific characters or team compositions.

### 3-Star Weapons
These are the most common weapons and are generally considered inferior for endgame content due to their low base ATK (around 350-400 at Level 90) and often weak or niche passive effects. However, some 3-star weapons can serve as temporary placeholders or have specific niche uses. The **Skyward Blade** is a notable exception, being a 5-star weapon.

## Weapon Ascension and Leveling

Weapons are leveled up using Weapon EXP materials (Fine, Adventurer's, Hero's Wits) and Mora. Ascending a weapon unlocks its level cap and enhances its base stats and passive ability. Each ascension phase requires specific weapon ascension materials, which are farmed from Elite Enemy drops (e.g., Hilichurl masks, Fatui Insignias), regional specialties, and domain materials that rotate daily. For example, the **Decimation** series of materials (used by swords) are found in the Forsaken Rift domain on Wednesdays, Saturdays, and Sundays.

## Refinement System

Refinement is a system that enhances the passive ability of a weapon by consuming another copy of the same weapon. This process can be repeated up to four times, bringing the weapon to Refinement Rank 5 (R5).

### Refinement Effects
Refinement ranks significantly boost the effectiveness of a weapon's passive. The exact increase varies per weapon, but generally, each rank adds a percentage point or a flat increase to the passive's effect.

*   **Example: The Catch** (4-star Polearm)
    *   **R1 Passive:** Increases Elemental Burst DMG by 6%, and Elemental Burst CRIT Rate by 12%.
    *   **R5 Passive:** Increases Elemental Burst DMG by 12%, and Elemental Burst CRIT Rate by 24%.

*   **Example: Primordial Jade Winged-Spear** (5-star Polearm)
    *   **R1 Passive:** On hit, increases ATK by 3.2% for 6s. Max 7 stacks. This effect can be triggered once every 0.3s. At max stacks, DMG is increased by 12%.
    *   **R5 Passive:** On hit, increases ATK by 6.4% for 6s. Max 7 stacks. This effect can be triggered once every 0.3s. At max stacks, DMG is increased by 24%.

The impact of refinement is most pronounced on weapons with powerful, scaling passives. For 5-star weapons, refinement is often a luxury, as obtaining multiple copies is difficult and expensive. However, for certain 4-star weapons, reaching R5 can make them exceptionally strong.

### Weapon Acquisition for Refinement
*   **Gacha:** The primary method for obtaining weapon copies is through the **Epitome Invocation** banner (weapon banner), which uses **Fate** currency. This is the most expensive method.
*   **Starglitter Exchange:** Some 4-star weapons can be purchased from the **Paimon's Bargains** shop using **Masterless Starglitter** obtained from pulling duplicate 4-star or 5-star characters and weapons.
*   **Event Rewards:** Certain limited-time events have offered exclusive 4-star weapons that can be obtained and refined through event currency or by collecting duplicate copies during the event. **The Catch** is a prime example, obtainable from the Inazuma Fishing Association.
*   **Battle Pass:** The **Gnostic Hymn** (paid Battle Pass) offers a choice of several 4-star weapons that can be refined by purchasing additional copies.

## Competitive 3-4 Star Weapons and Refinement Priority

While 5-star weapons are generally superior, several 4-star and even a niche 3-star weapon are highly competitive and often form the backbone of F2P or low-spending player builds.

### Notable 4-Star Weapons:

*   **The Catch:** An excellent polearm for characters who rely heavily on their Elemental Burst for damage, such as **Xiangling** and **Raiden Shogun**. Its passive directly buffs Burst DMG and CRIT Rate. It is obtainable through fishing in Inazuma and can be refined to R5 for free. Its R5 passive provides a substantial 12% Burst DMG and 24% Burst CRIT Rate.
*   **Favonius Weapons (Sword, Lance, Codex, Warbow):** These weapons are invaluable for team-wide Energy Recharge. Their passive generates Elemental Particles on a CRIT hit, which can significantly reduce the Energy needs of the entire team, allowing for more offensive substats on artifacts. They are particularly useful on characters who can reliably trigger the passive, such as **Kazuha** (Sword), **Zhongli** (Lance), **Sucrose** (Codex), and **Venti** (Warbow). Their refinement priority is generally high for any character needing ER and a consistent Crit Rate to proc the passive.
*   **Dragon's Bane:** A 4-star Polearm with Elemental Mastery as its secondary stat. Its passive increases DMG against opponents affected by Hydro or Pyro. This makes it an exceptional choice for characters focused on Vaporize or Melt reactions, such as **Hu Tao** and **Xiangling** when not using The Catch, or **Kazuha** in specific EM-focused builds. Its R5 passive offers a substantial 20% DMG bonus against affected targets.
*   **Black Tassel:** A 3-star Polearm with HP% as its secondary stat. While its base ATK is low, its HP% substat makes it surprisingly effective on **Zhongli** for his Geo DMG scaling with HP, especially for shield-bot builds. Its passive increases DMG against slimes, which is niche but irrelevant for its primary use case. It's a strong F2P option for Zhongli's shield strength.
*   **Mappa Mare:** A 4-star Catalyst with Elemental Mastery as its secondary stat. Its passive grants Elemental DMG Bonus after triggering an elemental reaction. It's a solid F2P option for reaction-based Catalyst users like **Sucrose** or **Klee**.
*   **Iron Sting:** A 4-star Sword with Elemental Mastery as its secondary stat. Its passive increases all DMG after dealing elemental damage. It's a good F2P option for characters like **Kazuha** or **Xingqiu** in certain builds.

### Refinement Priority for F2P Players

For free-to-play players, focusing on refining key 4-star weapons is crucial:

1.  **The Catch:** Prioritize obtaining and refining this to R5 for Xiangling and Raiden Shogun. Its accessibility through fishing and the significant boost it provides makes it a top priority.
2.  **Favonius Weapons:** Refine these based on team needs. A Favonius Sword for Bennett or Kazuha, a Favonius Lance for Zhongli or Thoma, and a Favonius Warbow for archers like Ganyu or Fischl can be game-changers for team energy. Prioritize based on which characters/teams you use most.
3.  **Dragon's Bane:** If you run Hydro/Pyro reaction teams and lack a 5-star option, refining Dragon's Bane to R5 is highly beneficial for characters like Hu Tao.
4.  **Event-Exclusive Weapons:** Weapons obtained from events, like **Festering Desire** or **Cinnabar Spindle**, should be refined to R5 if they are good for a character you use, as they are often unobtainable again.

Understanding the weapon system, its refinement mechanics, and prioritizing the acquisition and refinement of accessible, powerful 4-star weapons is essential for optimizing character performance in Genshin Impact, especially for players who do not engage in heavy spending on the gacha system.
