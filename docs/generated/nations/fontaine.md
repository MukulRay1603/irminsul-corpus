# Fontaine — Nation of Justice

Fontaine, the Nation of Justice, is a land of advanced technology, theatrical performances, and a deeply ingrained judicial system, all set against a backdrop of breathtaking aquatic landscapes. Governed by the Hydro Archon Furina, its society is built upon the pursuit of "Justice," a concept that transcends mere legal statutes and permeates every aspect of Fontainian life. The nation's unique culture, technological prowess, and the very nature of its existence are intrinsically linked to its Hydro element and the ancient lore surrounding the Primordial Sea.

## Geography and Key Locations

Fontaine's geography is dominated by water, both above and below the surface. The **Court of Fontaine**, the capital city, is a marvel of engineering and artistry, characterized by its towering structures, intricate canals, and the iconic Opera Epiclese. This grand theater serves as the central stage for the nation's judicial proceedings, which are presented as elaborate public trials. The city's architecture blends European-inspired aesthetics with a distinct steampunk flair, featuring gears, pipes, and clockwork mechanisms integrated into its design.

Beneath the surface of the Court of Fontaine lies a vast and complex underwater realm. This submerged territory is not merely a passive environment but an integral part of Fontaine's exploration and puzzle-solving mechanics. Players gain the ability to swim and dive freely in Fontaine's waters, allowing access to hidden areas, ancient ruins, and unique resources.

The **Fontaine Research Institute** is a significant location, representing the pinnacle of Fontainian scientific and technological achievement. This institute is dedicated to understanding and advancing various fields, including mechanics, energy, and the study of Fontainian phenomena. Its facilities are often characterized by advanced machinery and experimental apparatus, reflecting the nation's innovative spirit.

A stark contrast to the grandeur of the Court of Fontaine is the **Fortress of Meropide**, an imposing underwater prison. This colossal structure, built deep beneath the waves, houses Fontaine's criminals and serves as a testament to the nation's commitment to its judicial system, even for those deemed transgressors. The Fortress of Meropide is a self-contained environment with its own unique challenges and inhabitants, often featuring complex mechanical systems and hidden pathways.

Another crucial underwater location is the **Hydro Dragon's Tear Basin**. This area is steeped in ancient lore and is believed to be connected to the origins of the Primordial Sea and the Hydro Archon's lineage. It often contains remnants of ancient civilizations and holds secrets vital to understanding Fontaine's past and its prophesied future. The basin's environment is typically serene yet mysterious, hinting at the deep history embedded within Fontaine.

## The Fontainian Trial System and Cultural Significance

The **Fontainian trial system** is the cornerstone of the nation's identity and culture. Unlike conventional legal systems, Fontainian trials are public spectacles, held in the Opera Epiclese, where evidence is presented, testimonies are heard, and a verdict is delivered before the entire populace. These trials are not merely about dispensing justice but are also elaborate performances, reflecting the Fontainian penchant for drama and theatricality. The Hydro Archon, Furina, presides over these trials, often acting as both the judge and a key performer, embodying the nation's pursuit of truth and justice.

The cultural importance of justice in Fontaine is paramount. It is the guiding principle that shapes their laws, their social interactions, and their very self-perception. However, Fontainian justice is not solely about adherence to rigid laws; it is about the pursuit of absolute truth and the resolution of disputes in a manner that satisfies the collective conscience of the nation. This emphasis on "justice" over mere "law" stems from a deep-seated fear of judgment from a higher power and a desire to prove their worthiness. The Fontainians believe that by achieving perfect justice, they can absolve themselves of a primordial sin and ascend to a higher state of being. This cultural obsession with justice fuels their technological advancements and their intricate societal structures, all aimed at achieving an unblemished record of fairness and truth.

## Melusines: Unique Citizens of Fontaine

The **Melusines** are a unique sentient species native to Fontaine, distinguished by their distinct appearance, often featuring aquatic traits like fins and tails, and their exceptional sensory abilities. They are known for their unwavering loyalty, their keen senses of smell and hearing, and their innate connection to the waters of Fontaine. Despite their unique nature, Melusines are fully integrated into Fontainian society, serving in various capacities, including as police officers, investigators, and even judges. Their presence highlights Fontaine's progressive and inclusive societal structure, where even non-human beings are afforded rights and responsibilities.

Melusines possess a unique biological trait: their lifespan is significantly shorter than that of humans, and they gradually lose their memories as they age. This poignant aspect of their existence adds a layer of melancholy to their otherwise cheerful disposition. They are often depicted as diligent and earnest, striving to uphold justice and contribute to Fontainian society before their memories fade. Their role in the judicial system is particularly significant, as their heightened senses often provide crucial evidence or insights that human investigators might miss.

## The Ancient Prophecy and its Origins

Fontaine's history is deeply intertwined with an **ancient prophecy** that foretells a cataclysmic event and a subsequent judgment. This prophecy speaks of a time when the people of Fontaine will be judged by a higher power, and their fate will be determined by the purity of their pursuit of justice. The origins of this prophecy are tied to the **Primordial Sea**, a primordial substance that predates the current world and is intrinsically linked to the Hydro Archon's power and the original sin of the gods.

The prophecy suggests that the current Archon, Furina, is not the true Hydro Archon but an imposter, and that the real Hydro Archon is undergoing a long trial to absolve the people of Fontaine of their sins. This narrative creates a sense of urgency and existential dread within the nation, driving their relentless pursuit of justice as a means of atonement. The prophecy serves as a constant reminder of their precarious existence and the ultimate judgment that awaits them, fueling their theatricality and their obsession with proving their worthiness.

## Pneuma and Ousia: Fontaine's Mechanical System

Fontaine's technological advancements are largely powered by a unique energy system based on **Pneuma** and **Ousia**. These are two opposing forces that permeate the world of Teyvat, but in Fontaine, they have been harnessed and manipulated to power intricate machinery and solve complex puzzles. Pneuma represents a more stable, orderly energy, while Ousia is more volatile and chaotic.

Many of Fontaine's puzzles and mechanisms require the player to utilize characters with either Pneuma or Ousia alignment to activate specific devices. For example, certain energy conduits might require an Ousia-aligned attack to overload them, while others might need a Pneuma-aligned burst to stabilize. This system is integrated into the environment, encouraging players to experiment with different character abilities and elemental interactions to progress. The distinction between Pneuma and Ousia is crucial for understanding the mechanics of many Fontainian challenges and the underlying principles of their advanced technology. Characters like Lyney, Lynette, and Freminet exhibit these alignments, and their abilities are often tied to manipulating these energies.

## The Primordial Sea Lore

The **Primordial Sea** is a foundational element of Fontaine's lore, representing a primordial, unformed state of water that existed before the current world. It is said to be the source of all Hydro power and is intrinsically linked to the original sin committed by the gods. The Primordial Sea possesses the ability to dissolve and erase anything that comes into contact with it, making it an existential threat to the people of Fontaine, whose Archon is intrinsically tied to this primordial substance.

The lore suggests that the Primordial Sea is a manifestation of the original sin, and the Hydro Archon is tasked with bearing this burden and finding a way to absolve humanity of it. This connection creates a constant tension and a sense of impending doom for Fontaine, as the Primordial Sea represents a potential end to their existence. The Fontainians' relentless pursuit of justice is, in part, a desperate attempt to prove their innocence and escape the fate foretold by the prophecy, which is intrinsically tied to the Primordial Sea's destructive potential.

## Fontainian Steampunk Technology Without Geo or Divine Backing

Fontaine's remarkable **steampunk technology** developed without direct intervention or support from the Geo Archon or other divine entities, a rarity in Teyvat. While other nations often rely on elemental powers or divine blessings for their advancements, Fontaine's technological leap is largely attributed to the ingenuity and relentless pursuit of knowledge by its people, particularly its scientists and engineers.

The development of Pneuma and Ousia energy manipulation, coupled with a deep understanding of mechanics and hydraulics, allowed them to create complex machinery, automatons, and even advanced transportation systems. Their focus on theatricality and public spectacle also played a role, as grand performances often required elaborate stagecraft and mechanical marvels. This self-reliance and innovative spirit, driven by their unique cultural values and existential anxieties, allowed Fontaine to forge its own path in technological advancement, creating a distinct identity separate from the influence of other Archons or divine powers. Their intricate clockwork mechanisms, steam-powered devices, and sophisticated automatons are a testament to their independent innovation.

## Justice, Not Law: Fontaine's True Value

At its core, Fontaine's true value lies not in its adherence to strict laws, but in its unwavering pursuit of **Justice**. This distinction is crucial to understanding Fontainian culture and motivations. While laws provide a framework, justice in Fontaine is a more profound concept, encompassing truth, fairness, and the resolution of disputes in a manner that satisfies the collective moral compass.

The Fontainians believe that by achieving absolute justice, they can prove their worthiness to a higher power and escape the fate foretold by the prophecy. This existential drive fuels their elaborate judicial system, their theatrical performances, and their technological advancements. They are not merely seeking to punish wrongdoers but to uncover the absolute truth and achieve a state of moral purity. This pursuit of justice is a deeply ingrained cultural imperative, shaping their identity and driving their actions, making it the true essence of the Nation of Justice. The constant performance of trials, even for minor infractions, serves as a ritualistic reaffirmation of their commitment to this ideal.
