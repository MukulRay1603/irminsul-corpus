# Inazuma — Nation of Eternity

Inazuma, the Nation of Eternity, is an archipelago nation located east of Liyue, characterized by its distinct islands, unique culture, and the omnipresent influence of the Electro Archon, Baal (later revealed to be Beelzebul, with Makoto having been the original Archon). The nation's pursuit of "Eternity" as a societal ideal, particularly under the Raiden Shogun's rule, has shaped its history, governance, and the lives of its inhabitants. This pursuit, while aiming to prevent loss and suffering, ultimately led to profound societal upheaval and internal conflict.

## The Archipelago and its Islands

Inazuma is comprised of six major islands: Narukami, Kannazuka, Yashiori, Watatsumi, Seirai, and Tsurumi. Each island possesses unique geographical features, cultural nuances, and historical significance tied to the nation's past and the Electro Archon's decrees.

### Narukami Island

Narukami Island is the spiritual and political heart of Inazuma, home to the Grand Narukami Shrine and the Tenshukaku, the seat of the Raiden Shogun's power. It is the most developed island, featuring bustling cities like Inazuma City and serene landscapes such as the Chinju Forest. The Grand Narukami Shrine, dedicated to the Narukami Ogosho, is a central religious site, managed by the Yae Publishing House and overseen by Yae Miko. The island's primary deity is the Electro Archon, and its culture deeply reveres the concept of Eternity. The Sacred Sakura, a colossal tree rooted at the Grand Narukami Shrine, plays a crucial role in Inazuma's spiritual well-being, purifying the land and warding off malevolent spirits. Its health is directly linked to the nation's prosperity and stability. The Sacred Sakura's roots extend across Inazuma, and its vitality is maintained through offerings and the Archon's power.

### Kannazuka Island

Kannazuka Island is characterized by its industrial past and the lingering scars of war. It was once a site of significant conflict during the Archon War and later during the Tatarigami's spread. The Kujou Camp, a military stronghold, is located here, symbolizing the nation's military might. The island is also home to the Tatarigami's influence, particularly around the abandoned Tatarasuna forge, where the remnants of a great battle and the curse's origin can be found. The abandoned forge, once a center of Inazuma's metal production, is now a desolate area, a testament to the destructive power of the curse. The presence of the Kujou Clan's military forces on Kannazuka highlights the island's strategic importance and its role in maintaining order.

### Yashiori Island

Yashiori Island is infamous for the Tatarigami curse, a potent miasma originating from the remains of the defeated god Orobashi. This curse manifests as a perpetual storm and a deadly affliction that slowly consumes living beings. The island is largely uninhabited and dangerous, dotted with the colossal bones of Orobashi, a constant reminder of the battle that took place here. The Tatarigami curse is a direct consequence of Orobashi's demise, a divine serpent slain by the Electro Archon. The curse’s miasma causes severe illness, madness, and physical decay in those exposed for prolonged periods. The Raiden Shogun's decree to isolate Yashiori Island was a measure to contain the curse, further emphasizing the nation's struggle with the consequences of past actions. The storm that perpetually rages over the island is a manifestation of the lingering divine energy and the curse itself.

### Watatsumi Island

Watatsumi Island is a stark contrast to the rest of Inazuma, known for its peaceful atmosphere, unique marine life, and distinct culture. The islanders worship Orobashi, the serpent god slain by the Electro Archon, and maintain a degree of autonomy, often clashing with the policies of the Raiden Shogun. The Sangonomiya Shrine, led by Sangonomiya Kokomi, serves as the spiritual and political center of Watatsumi. The island's culture is deeply tied to the sea and its benevolent deity, Orobashi, who is revered for protecting them. This worship has historically created tension with the Electro Archon's authority, as Orobashi was a rival deity. The island's inhabitants are known for their resilience and their deep connection to the ocean, which influences their traditions and way of life. The presence of unique flora and fauna, such as the Lumidouce Bell and the Sango Pearl, further distinguishes Watatsumi.

### Seirai Island

Seirai Island is a perpetually storm-wracked land, dominated by the colossal Amakumo Peak. The island is a testament to the destructive power of divine wrath, constantly battered by thunderstorms. The lingering divine energy from a past conflict, specifically the battle between the Raiden Shogun and the Thunder Manifestation, has imbued the island with its perpetual tempest. Navigating Seirai requires careful attention to the storm's patterns and the use of specialized mechanisms to temporarily quell its intensity. The island's landscape is rugged and volcanic, with remnants of ancient structures hinting at a forgotten civilization. The Thunder Manifestation, a powerful Electro entity born from the storm, is a constant threat to any who dare to venture onto Seirai.

## The Three Commissions

The governance of Inazuma is structured around three powerful commissions, each with distinct responsibilities:

### Yashiro Commission

The Yashiro Commission is responsible for the administration of shrines and temples, including the Grand Narukami Shrine. It oversees religious ceremonies, festivals, and the spiritual well-being of the nation. The commission is also involved in cultural preservation and the management of sacred sites. Yae Miko, the Guuji of the Grand Narukami Shrine, holds significant influence within this commission, often acting as an intermediary between the divine and mortal realms. The commission's duties extend to managing the Sacred Sakura's health and ensuring its continued vitality, which is crucial for Inazuma's spiritual balance.

### Kanjou Commission

The Kanjou Commission is in charge of trade, commerce, and economic affairs within Inazuma. It regulates shipping, customs, and the flow of goods between islands and with other nations. The commission plays a vital role in maintaining Inazuma's economy and ensuring its prosperity. They are responsible for managing the Inazuma-wide trade routes and ensuring fair practices among merchants. The commission's headquarters are located in Inazuma City, where they oversee all major trade operations. Their authority is paramount in regulating Inazuma's economic interactions.

### Kujou Commission

The Kujou Commission is the military arm of Inazuma, responsible for defense, law enforcement, and maintaining order. It commands the Shogunate's army and navy, enforcing the Raiden Shogun's decrees and protecting the nation from internal and external threats. The commission is headquartered at the Kujou Camp on Kannazuka Island and is led by the Kujou Clan. Their primary objective is to uphold the Shogunate's authority and ensure the security of Inazuma, often through stringent enforcement of laws and decrees.

## The Vision Hunt Decree and Societal Impact

The Vision Hunt Decree, enacted by the Raiden Shogun, was a pivotal event that plunged Inazuma into turmoil. The decree mandated the confiscation of all Visions, the glowing gems that grant individuals elemental abilities. The Raiden Shogun believed that Visions were a source of chaos and a threat to her ideal of Eternity, as they represented ambition and the potential for change, which she sought to suppress. The decree led to widespread fear, resistance, and the formation of rebel groups, most notably the Watatsumi Island resistance led by Sangonomiya Kokomi. The confiscation of Visions was carried out by the Tenryou Commission, often with brutal efficiency, creating a climate of oppression and distrust. This act fundamentally altered the social fabric of Inazuma, pitting citizens against the Shogunate and forcing many to question the true meaning of Eternity. The decree's impact was felt across all social strata, from commoners to nobles, as the potential for power and individual expression was systematically removed.

## Watatsumi Island's Worship and Historical Tensions

Watatsumi Island's unique cultural identity is deeply rooted in its worship of Orobashi, the colossal serpent god. Orobashi was a benevolent deity who protected the islanders from the ravages of war and the harshness of the sea. This reverence for Orobashi created a historical tension with the Electro Archon, as Orobashi was a rival deity during the Archon War. The Raiden Shogun's victory over Orobashi, while securing Inazuma's dominance, also led to the curse that afflicted Yashiori Island. The Watatsumi people view Orobashi's demise as a tragedy and maintain a distinct cultural and religious identity separate from the mainland's reverence for the Electro Archon. This historical divergence in worship and allegiance has been a source of friction between Watatsumi and the central Inazuman government.

## The Tatarigami Curse

The Tatarigami curse on Yashiori Island is a direct consequence of the Electro Archon's battle with Orobashi. Upon Orobashi's defeat, its immense divine blood spilled across the land, imbuing the island with a potent, malevolent energy. This energy manifests as a deadly miasma that causes severe illness, madness, and physical decay in living beings exposed to it. The curse is so potent that it has rendered most of Yashiori Island uninhabitable, creating a perpetual storm and a dangerous environment. The remnants of Orobashi's colossal body serve as a constant reminder of the curse's origin. The Raiden Shogun's response was to isolate the island, attempting to contain the curse and prevent its spread, a measure that further highlighted the destructive consequences of divine conflict.

## The Thunder Manifestation

The Thunder Manifestation is a powerful Electro entity that resides on Seirai Island, born from the lingering divine energy of a past conflict. It is a manifestation of the storm's wrath and a formidable foe, capable of unleashing devastating Electro attacks. The Raiden Shogun herself battled this entity during her quest to quell the perpetual storm on Seirai. Its existence is a testament to the raw, untamed power that permeates Inazuma, particularly in areas scarred by divine intervention. The Thunder Manifestation's attacks are characterized by rapid Electro pulses and powerful AoE bursts, making it a significant environmental hazard and a challenging boss encounter.

## The Raiden Shogun's Design: Governance and Eternity

The Raiden Shogun, Ei (Beelzebul), governs Inazuma with a singular focus on achieving "Eternity." This pursuit is not merely a philosophical ideal but a deeply ingrained mechanism of governance designed to prevent loss, suffering, and the chaos she associates with change. Having witnessed the devastation of the Archon War and the loss of her sister, Makoto, Ei became convinced that true peace could only be achieved by freezing time and preventing any further change or ambition. The Vision Hunt Decree was a direct manifestation of this philosophy, aiming to eliminate individual aspirations and the potential for unpredictable outcomes. The Raiden Shogun's design as a governance mechanism is one of absolute control and stagnation, believing that by preventing loss, she could create an eternal, unchanging state of peace. However, this approach ultimately led to internal strife, rebellion, and a profound questioning of what true peace entails. The arc of Inazuma's story is fundamentally about the unintended consequences of such an extreme pursuit of preventing loss, demonstrating that true stability cannot be achieved by suppressing individual will and the natural flow of change. The nation's journey involves confronting the sacrifices made in the name of Eternity and understanding that true strength lies not in stagnation, but in embracing change and learning from loss.
